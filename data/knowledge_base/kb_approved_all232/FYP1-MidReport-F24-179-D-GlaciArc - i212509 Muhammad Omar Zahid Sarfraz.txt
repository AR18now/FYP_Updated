2.3 Non-Functional Requirements
2.3.1 Reliability
• Requirement: The GlaciArc system shall maintain a Mean Time Between Failures
(MTBF) of at least 1,000 hours.
• Failure Definition: A failure occurs when the system crashes, produces erroneous
results, or experiences significant processing delays.
• Consequences: Software failures may lead to inaccurate predictions about glacier
movements and GLOFs, adversely affecting disaster management decisions.
• Failure Protection: Regular data backups and performance logging will be imple-
mented to safeguard against data loss.
• Error Detection Strategy: The system will employ robust exception handling to
capture runtime errors and notify system administrators.
• Correction Strategy: A rollback mechanism will be in place to restore the system to
its last stable state in the event of a failure.
2.3.2 Usability
• Requirement: Users shall be able to perform common tasks (e.g., downloading
images, viewing predictions) within three clicks.
• Learning Ease: New users should comprehend basic functionalities within 30 min-
utes of interaction.
• Error Avoidance : The system shall feature an undo functionality for critical actions
(e.g., deleting predictions).

System Overview
3.1 Architectural Design
Our project follows a procedural programming structure, where each task is executed in
a sequential manner. The workflow is based on a pipeline architecture, where the out-
put of one step serves as the input for the next. The process begins with satellite image
acquisition, downloading images from Google Earth Engine. These images are then pre-
processed, followed by classification into categories such as glacier, infrastructure, water
body, and vegetation. The segmented images are compared against ground truth values.
After that the images are analyzed for glacier movement and other critical features to as-
sess potential risks. Once the analysis is complete, the images are annotated with danger
and safe zones. Finally, the results are visualized on the dashboard, providing a compre-
hensive view of the risks.
