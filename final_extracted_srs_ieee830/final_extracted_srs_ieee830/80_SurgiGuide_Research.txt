SOFTWARE REQUIREMENTS SPECIFICATION (IEEE 830)
Project: SurgiGuide - AI-Driven Real-Time Surgical Guidance System
================================================================================

1. INTRODUCTION
1.1 Purpose
This document outlines the requirements for SurgiGuide, an AI-based decision support system designed to address preventable surgical mistakes by providing real-time guidance to surgeons.

1.2 Scope
The research focuses on developing a system to identify surgical phases in real-time, suggest next steps, point out mistakes, and provide remedies. It aims to improve surgical decision-making and reduce errors.

1.3 Definitions, Acronyms, Abbreviations
- AI: Artificial Intelligence
- TEP: Totally Extraperitoneal Inguinal Hernia Repair
- RARP: Robot-Assisted Radical Prostatectomy
- CNN: Convolutional Neural Network
- ViT: Vision Transformer

2. OVERALL DESCRIPTION
2.1 Product Perspective
SurgiGuide seeks to increase awareness during surgery, improve outcomes, and enhance surgical growth by providing contextually relevant data and corrective feedback.

2.2 Product Functions (Summary)
- Real-time identification of surgical phases.
- Prediction of the next surgical step.
- Identification and flagging of mistakes.
- Provision of corrective actions.

2.3 User Classes and Characteristics
- Surgeons: Primary users who will receive real-time guidance and feedback.
- Surgical Trainees: Who will benefit from real-time feedback as an educational tool.

2.4 Operating Environment
Integrates with operating room video streams.

3. FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS
3.1 Surgical Phase Recognition
FR1.1: The system shall analyze live surgical video streams.
FR1.2: The system shall identify the current phase of surgery.
FR1.3: The system shall use models like ViT and 3D CNN for accurate phase recognition.

3.2 Next Step Suggestion
FR2.1: Based on the current phase, the system shall predict the sequence of actions to be taken next.

3.3 Mistake Identification
FR3.1: The system shall compare actual surgical activities against a database of standards and mishaps.
FR3.2: The system shall flag deviations from standard procedures.

3.4 Remedy Provision
FR4.1: The system shall provide recommendations for corrective adjustments when a mistake is indicated.

4. NON-FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS
4.1 Performance
NFR1: The system must process video and provide feedback with minimal latency for real-time application.

4.2 Accuracy
NFR2: The system must achieve high accuracy in phase and mistake recognition.

4.3 Reliability
NFR3: The system must be highly dependable and work in diverse surgical environments.

4.4 Usability
NFR4: The user interface must integrate smoothly into the workflow of surgeons and nurses without being intrusive.

4.5 Validation
NFR5: The system's performance must be validated in live and complex surgical scenarios.
