SOFTWARE REQUIREMENTS SPECIFICATION (IEEE 830)
Project: CyberQuest - VR-Based Cybersecurity Awareness Game
================================================================================

1. INTRODUCTION
1.1 Purpose
This document outlines the requirements for CyberQuest, a 3D game-based intervention designed to increase cybersecurity awareness among teenagers and young adults in the Indo-Pak region by simulating realistic scenarios and teaching preventive measures.

1.2 Scope
To design, develop, and implement a 3D game (with VR integration) that simulates real-world locations (homes, universities, restaurants) and cyberattacks (phishing, social engineering, malware). The game features a social life storyline, reputation and mood bar system, in-game economy (coins), and educational content. It covers common cyberattacks but excludes others like DDoS and SQL injection.

1.3 Definitions, Acronyms, Abbreviations
- VR: Virtual Reality
- NPC: Non-Player Character
- UI: User Interface
- UX: User Experience

2. OVERALL DESCRIPTION
2.1 Product Perspective
CyberQuest is an immersive educational game that uses VR and interactive storytelling to teach cybersecurity concepts, focusing on player decision-making and its consequences within a simulated daily life.

2.2 Product Functions (Summary)
- Game Mechanics (player movement, object interaction)
- Game Elements (visual design of environments, game objects)
- Assets Creation (environment, characters)
- Game Scenarios (storylines, narrative building, scenario integration)
- UI/UX (intuitive menus, game flow objects)
- VR Integration (VR-ready controls, optimization)

2.3 User Classes and Characteristics
- Students: Primary users (16-25) who will face cybersecurity threats in virtual real-world locations.
- Faculty Members: Cybersecurity professionals or experts who might use the game for training or evaluation.
- Cybersecurity Experts: Evaluate the game's accuracy and effectiveness in teaching concepts; may provide feedback.
- Game Administrator: Maintains the game, updates scenarios, and ensures VR platform functionality.

2.4 Operating Environment
PC game optimized for VR platforms, including Oculus Quest 2.

3. FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS
3.1 User Interface (UI) & User Experience (UX)
FR1.1: The system shall provide a simple, intuitive, and consistent UI for navigating menus, settings, and tutorials.
FR1.2: The game shall offer real-time visual feedback in response to player actions.
FR1.3: The game shall allow the player to view basic information about their overall performance, level, and scores.
FR1.4: The system shall include a "Mood Bar" reflecting the player's social status with two states: "happy" and "dead inside".
FR1.5: The system shall guide players through challenges with contextual prompts.
FR1.6: The audio subsystem shall adjust ambient sounds and music based on player interactions.

3.2 Virtual Reality Integration
FR2.1: The system shall be compatible with VR headsets, including Oculus.
FR2.2: VR environments shall simulate real-world locations (buses, universities, restaurants).
FR2.3: The system shall allow players to interact with NPCs through dialogue and decision-making.
FR2.4: Player decisions in virtual environments shall dynamically change the scenario and storyline.

3.3 Cybersecurity Attack Simulation
FR3.1: The game shall simulate cyberattacks (phishing, malware, ransomware) in daily life scenarios.
FR3.2: The system shall provide educational content for each simulated attack, explaining its nature and preventive measures.
FR3.3: After a successful attack, the system shall provide players with damage control strategies.

3.4 Reputation & Social Points System
FR4.1: The system shall adjust the player's reputation based on their responses to social interactions and cyberattacks.
FR4.2: The game shall reward positive actions with social points and in-game currency (coins) and penalize negative actions.
FR4.3: The "Mood Bar" shall visually reflect the player's accumulated social points.
FR4.4: Losing all social points shall result in failure of the current level.

3.5 Educational Content & Tutorials
FR5.1: The game shall provide real-time feedback and hints to reinforce proper responses to cyberattacks.
FR5.2: After an attack, the system shall give preventive tips to avoid similar attacks in future levels.

3.6 Game Progression & Levels
FR6.1: The game shall consist of multiple levels, each representing a different phase of life (e.g., university, early career).
FR6.2: Players shall unlock new virtual environments by completing tasks and reaching milestones.
FR6.3: Each level shall present increasingly complex challenges requiring application of cybersecurity knowledge.

3.7 Coins & Rewards System
FR7.1: The game shall reward players with coins for making good decisions (e.g., protecting personal data, avoiding attacks).
FR7.2: Players shall use earned coins to unlock in-game items or progress.
FR7.3: The system shall provide visual and auditory feedback when coins are earned.

3.8 Decision-Making & Scenario Selection
FR8.1: The system shall present players with decisions that impact the game's storyline and future challenges.
FR8.2: The outcome of these decisions shall dynamically alter the next set of challenges.
FR8.3: Player decisions shall influence the behavior of NPCs.

3.9 Non-Player Characters (NPCs) Interaction
FR9.1: NPCs shall exhibit different behaviors based on the player's decisions.
FR9.2: NPCs shall act as attackers, mentors, or allies depending on the context.
FR9.3: The player shall interact with NPCs through dialogues and decision points.

3.10 Task & Milestone Management
FR10.1: The game shall feature milestones like reaching academic or personal goals.
FR10.2: The system shall present challenges and tasks in each level that must be completed to move forward.
FR10.3: Each task must integrate a learning component related to cybersecurity.

3.11 Level Failure & Restart Mechanism
FR11.1: Players shall lose a level if they reach the "dead inside" state on the mood bar.
FR11.2: The game shall provide checkpoints within levels.
FR11.3: The system shall offer tips or guidance when players fail a level.

3.12 Daily Life Simulation
FR12.1: The game shall simulate daily activities (e.g., traveling on a bus, attending classes).
FR12.2: Daily activities must be interwoven with cybersecurity education (e.g., using free Wi-Fi safely).

3.13 Level Save Progression Management
FR13.1: The game shall automatically save player progress at predefined checkpoints.
FR13.2: Players shall have the option to manually save their current progress.
FR13.3: The game shall allow players to load their saved progress.

3.14 Performance
FR14.1: The player shall be able to view their current performance.
FR14.2: The player shall be able to interact with the updated gaming based on their performance.

3.15 Ending
FR15.1: The game shall end once the player successfully reaches their office milestone.

4. NON-FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS
4.1 Reliability Requirements
NFR1: The game shall recover from unexpected crashes within 10 seconds and restore progress to the last saved checkpoint.

4.2 Usability Requirements
NFR2: The VR system shall allow users to easily navigate menus using simple hand gestures or VR controllers.
NFR3: The game must be simple and have a user-friendly interactive interface.
NFR4: The game shall provide on-screen prompts to guide players.
NFR5: The game must display error messages related to gameplay.
NFR6: The game must allow users to pause, save, and resume at any time.

4.3 Performance Requirements
NFR7: The game must load scenarios within 10 seconds.
NFR8: System shall be responsive towards the user's actions.
NFR9: The system shall ensure that all in-game data processing is completed within 1 second.

4.4 Security Requirements
NFR10: Profile of one user shall not be accessed by any other user.
