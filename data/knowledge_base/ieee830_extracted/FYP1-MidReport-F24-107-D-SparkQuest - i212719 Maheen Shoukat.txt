1.3 Modules
SparkQuest, an E-learning web app, is based on a total of seven modules. These modules
are divided into two modes, User mode and Evaluation mode. The modules are interlinked
components at the back end that will provide a complete learning experience to a primary-
grade student to promote additional self-learning.
1.3.1 Module 1 - Two modes of the Client Web Application
This module will consist of two modes, User mode and Evaluation mode. The user mode
will be for guided learning of students where they can learn from the material by asking
questions or getting summaries from it. The evaluation mode will be a test-based mod-
ule for the students with add-on features like Parental controls to ensure that the student
learning curve is supervised.

System Overview
SparkQuest is an all-inclusive, AI-based educational tool meant for primary-grade learn-

multiple modes including User Mode in which students can access educational content
such as text, image, or video and ask through texts and even spoken questions which
would be answered by AI. It also includes an Evaluation Mode where the student will be
able to take quizzes and tests to evaluate his performance. The multilingual support of
English and Urdu language along with the requirement of image and video as input to
solve the problems become highly flexible in terms of learning.
Other than the features that will cater to the students, SparkQuest has developed a Parental
Monitoring module where parents and guardians will be able to monitor their student’s
progress, access the chat history, and retrieve their evaluation results from a dashboard.
Having a layered structure with presentation, business, and data layers, the system pro-
vides an ability to scale the system and make a proper management of data. It main-
tains the privacy of data records, quizzes, chats, and other contents while enabling asyn-
chronous and synchronous conversations. The following chapter explains why SparkQuest
is developed for young students with flexible learning preferences and parental control.
3.1 Architectural Design
The architecture of SparkQuest is based on a multi-tiered structure, which breaks the sys-
tem into three distinct layers: the Presentation Tier, the Business Logic Tier, and the Data
Tier. The Presentation Tier is the outermost and communicates between students, parents,
and guardians through interfaces such as for progress and result evaluations. Here, at the
Business Logical Tier, actual operations such as content management, question answer-
ing, evaluations and chat logging take place. Last but not least, the Data Tier is responsible
for structurally storing information about students and the contents they are enrolled in,

The database of SparkQuest consists of three main tables: Students, Guardians, and Chat-
Log. The Students table keeps all the data related to each student separately using the
fields email, password, grade, and a link to the guardian. The basic details of each
guardian, including name, email, and password, are maintained in the Guardians table
where a foreign key is used to link it to students with many-to-one relationships. The
ChatLog table acts as a container for all various aspects of chat, including questions, an-
swers, or even reports, as messages. The Entries in the ChatLog table are linked to the
Students and the Guardians table respectively, with the help of foreign keys. This setup
allows quick access to and easy chats data storage while keeping good relationships be-
tween students, guardians, and their chats, eliminating the need for different tables for
various kinds of chats as in shown figure 3.83.8.
