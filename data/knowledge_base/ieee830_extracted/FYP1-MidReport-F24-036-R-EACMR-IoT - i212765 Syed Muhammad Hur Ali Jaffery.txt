Introduction
The rapidly growing Internet of Things (IoT) is transforming businesses and everyday life
around the world. From smart homes to industrial-made appliances, IoT devices facilitate
flawless communication and data-driven decision-making. As there are more and more
devices being connected together, the importance of improved efficiency, enhanced secu-
rity, and driven innovation continues to grow for IoTs [66] . The widespread adoption of
these Internet of Things devices has reshaped how we engage with technology, resulting
in a smarter and more linked society.
One of the most important components in enabling this communication and interaction,
especially in resource-limited settings[1313], is the Routing Protocol for Low-Power and
Lossy Networks. This protocol is responsible for managing data flow in networks where
devices have limited power, bandwidth, and processing capacity, making it a widely
adopted protocol for IoT deployments [ 77]. The hierarchical structure, which is based on
Destination-Oriented Directed Acyclic Graph (DODAG)formation and dynamic rout-
ing capabilities [55], means if there is a node that is unavailable due to energy depletion,
it can automatically reroute through other available nodes without disrupting the traffic,
making it an essential tool for optimising network performance in IoT applications such
as healthcare, industrial automation, and smart cities.
However, the rapid growth of IoT networks has also exposed critical security weaknesses
within the RPL protocol. IoT devices, often deployed in large, distributed, and low-power
environments, are increasingly at risk of data breaches, unauthorised access, and other
cyber threats [22]. The lack of built-in security features in RPL makes these networks par-
ticularly vulnerable to attacks aimed at compromising the confidentiality and authenticity
of data.
This proposal paper proposes a methodology for defining an effective authentication and
confidentiality mechanism in RPL-based IoT networks, where the critical Contiki-NG op-
erating system is deployed in upcoming IoT devices from various sectors, such as smart

1.1 Problem Domain
This study will examine the critical security challenges faced by constrained IoT de-
vices operating within RPL-based networks. The Internet of Things (IoT) has rapidly
expanded across various sectors, embedding intelligence into everyday devices to enable
communication and data exchange. Nevertheless, the resource limitations of IoT devices
(processing power, memory and energy) are real critical barrier today when it comes to the
adoption of traditional security mechanisms. These limitations create weaknesses in the
areas of authentication and confidentiality, which are vital for secure operation of IoT
networks, especially in sensitive environments like healthcare, smart cities, and industrial
automation.
At the heart of many IoT networks is the Routing Protocol for Low-Power and Lossy
Networks (RPL), which was designed to facilitate efficient communication among de-
vices in resource-constrained environments [ 1010]. While RPL excels in managing data
flow and optimising network resources, it lacks robust built-in security features. This
creates several security gaps, particularly in the areas of data integrity, authentication,
and confidentiality. As a result, RPL-based IoT networks are highly vulnerable to at-
tacks such as actors can exploit the lack of secure authentication mechanisms to gain
unauthorized access or disrupt network operations.
The primary gap in this domain is the lack of lightweight cryptographic solutions tai-
lored to the specific needs of constrained IoT devices [55]. This leads to weakened security
or, at worst case, a lack of security as traditional cryptographic algorithms are often too
computationally expensive over such devices. Moreover, RPL networks’ poor key man-
agement can be exploited to steal keys and prevent forward/backward secrecy which is
important because devices join/leave frequently [55]. As such, this work addresses a crit-
ical gap in prior research by examining use of lightweight cryptographic mechanisms
delivering strong authentication and privacy whilst being low-footprint to the constrained
resources of IoT devices.

1.2 Research Problem Statement
1.2 Research Problem Statement
The exact problem addressed in this research is the absence of efficient and lightweight
security mechanisms for RPL-based IoT networks, specially when it comes to authenti-
cation and confidentiality. With the expansion of constrained IoT devices into areas like
health care and industrial manufacturing, many security solutions are still too resource-
intensive, leaving networks susceptible to routing attacks that can leave data interceptions
and unauthorized access pathways open. However, theRPL protocol while great at deal-
ing with low power communication does not have built in security measures to an effective
shield against such threats [55]. This creates a significant gap in ensuring secure data trans-
mission and the reliable authentication of devices, particularly as IoT networks scale and
evolve.
This research aims at bridging this gap by designing a lightweight security mechanism
that uses Elliptic Curve Cryptography (ECC) for authentication and the Simon and
Speck algorithms for confidentiality. The study aims to develop this cryptographic solu-
tions for RPL based networks, considering the limitations of IoT devices and providing
a framework that will provide security without affecting network performance or effi-
ciency. This approach will address the critical need for scalable, secure communication in
resource-constrained[1010] environments, providing a solution that balances security with
computational and energy limitations. The significance of this research lies in its potential
to enhance the resilience of IoT networks to external attacks, ensuring a safer and more
reliable infrastructure for interconnected devices in various real-world applications.

2.1.5 Cryptography Algorithms for Enhancing IoT Security
2.1.5.1 Summary of the research item:
The research article "Cryptography Algorithms for Enhancing IoT Security" [ 99] exten-
sively discusses the role of lightweight cryptographic algorithms, particularly Simon and
Speck, in improving IoT device security. Simon and Speck are block ciphers developed by
the NSA specifically designed to address the security needs of resource-constrained envi-
ronments, such as IoT, where devices have limited processing power, memory, and energy.
The main focus of the study is on how these ciphers can effectively provide authentication
and confidentiality, which are critical security requirements in IoT applications.
Simon and Speck [ 44] are highly optimised for their respective implementations: Simon
for hardware and Speck for software. Simon uses a simple structure based on bitwise op-
erations, rotations, and modular additions, making it efficient for devices like RFID tags,
sensors, and embedded systems that rely on minimal computational power. While Speck
employs simple operations, it is more versatile in software environments and offers bet-
ter performance in terms of speed and flexibility. The study highlights that these ciphers
maintain a balance between security and efficiency, making them ideal for IoT applica-
tions where lightweight yet robust encryption is essential to protect against unauthorised
access and data breaches.
2.1.5.2 Critical analysis of the research item:
Strengths
1. Efficiency for IoT Devices : The simplicity of Simon and Speck ciphers makes
them highly suitable for IoT environments, as they require minimal computational
overhead, memory, and power consumption which are critical factors for resource-
constrained devices.

for varying levels of security and performance.
3. Low Power Consumption: Simon’sdesign, focused on logical shifts and bitwise

2.1.7.1 Summary of the research item:
The paper "Lightweight Cryptography Algorithms for Resource Constrained IoT
Devices: A Review, Comparison and Research Opportunities"[1010] focusses on lightweight
cryptography algorithms specifically designed for resource-constrained IoT devices. These
algorithms are essential for IoT applications that involve low-power devices like RFID
tags, sensors, and smart cards. The study[1010] provides a comparison of over fifty lightweight
cryptography algorithms based on performance metrics such as memory, gate area, power
consumption, and resistance to attacks. Simon and Speck, designed by the NSA, are
praised for their compact design and low computational cost, making them suitable for
both hardware and software implementations. The paper[1010] concludes by identifying the
challenges of balancing security, performance, and cost in lightweight cryptography and
calls for further research into new solutions that can better meet these requirements.

2.1.8.1 Summary of the research item:
The paper "IoT Security: Implementation of Xtea, Simon/Speck Lightweight Block
Ciphers" [44] by Levent Ertaul and Arpit Chauhan examines the performance of Xtea,
Simon, and Speck block ciphers on resource-constrained IoT devices. Given the lim-
ited memory, processing power, and energy of devices like microcontrollers used in IoT
applications, the study highlights the need for lightweight encryption. The authors imple-
mented and tested these ciphers on an ESP8266 microcontroller, focussing on execution
time, power consumption, and memory usage. Simon and Speck, developed by the NSA,
were designed for constrained environments, with Simon optimised for hardware and
Speck for software applications, both excelling in low-resource settings.
2.1.8.2 Critical analysis of the research item:
1. Practical Evaluation: The study provides real-world insights by testing Simon,
Speck, and Xtea ciphers on an ESP8266 microcontroller, offering valuable data
for IoT applications where devices have limited memory, processing power, and
energy.
2. Flexibility and Scalability: Simon and Speck are highlighted for their adaptability,
supporting various block and key sizes, making them suitable for a wide range of
IoT devices, from RFID tags to more complex embedded systems.
3. Hardware and Software Suitability: Simon’s optimization for hardware and Speck’s
for software make these ciphers versatile, broadening their use cases within IoT en-
vironments.
Weaknesses:
1. Performance Trade-offs: Simon and Speck are less efficient than Xtea in terms of
execution speed and memory usage. Their additional operations (rotations, AND,
XOR) increase computational complexity, leading to slower encryption times and
higher power consumption.
2. Limited Security Discussion: The study lacks an in-depth analysis of the ciphers’
resistance to cryptographic attacks, which is crucial for evaluating their security
robustness in IoT environments.

Proposed Approach
The solution will be implemented in the RPL Protocol which is a Routing Protocol for
Low Power and Lossy networks below is the working3.13.1. The term Lossy networks mean
a high packet drop rate. In IoT devices have low power and Lossy networks, so RPL
protocol is widely used. The RPL is mainly used with IPv6 but can also implemented in
IPv4. The RPL is a distance vector routing protocol, it is based on Destination Oriented
Directed Acyclic Graphs (DODAG). Above is the working of the protocol of how it works,
where DODAG is created and nodes are joining in through messages.
Figure 3.1: Working of RPL Protocol
To address the identified security vulnerabilities in RPL-based IoT networks, the pro-
posed solution involves the development of an Efficient Authentication and Confiden-
tiality Mechanism for RPL-Based IoT Networks (EACMR-IoT) . The solution will
22
leverage Elliptic Curve Cryptography (ECC) for robust authentication and the Simon
and Speck lightweight block ciphers for ensuring data confidentiality. The proposed
system will be implemented in phases as seen in the componenents diagram below 3.23.2,
including:
1. Enhancing RPL Protocol Security: The proposed approach revolves around the
development of the Secure RPL (SRPL) protocol, specially tailored to address the
security challenges encountered by constrained IoT devices utilizing the RPL pro-
tocol. This will be done in RPL code in the Contiki-NG operating system. This will
involve different cryptographic mechanisms discussed in below points.
2. Robust Registeration and Authentication: An authentication server or border
router will ensure that the mitigation of external attacks by integration of Elliptic
Curve Cryptography (ECC) is used for robust authentication, key management,
and revocation. ECC is selected for its efficiency in resource constrained environ-
ments due to its smaller key size. The Secure distribution of global parameters will
also be done from this server among IoT devices through DIO message (RPL Pro-
tocol). Each Iot device will be responsible to generate its own public key and share
it with its Unique Device Identification to server with DAO message. This will
result in device registration as it will store correspond ID with its public key. Once
this is done then, server will send a nonce to the registered node, when this nonce is
received by the registered nonce it will use digital signature (encrypt the nonce with
its private key) and send it back to the server to authenticate itself, as it can only be
decrypted by the public key of registered node. Now the node is authenticated.
3. Forward and Backward Privacy: The design will also include feature which will
ensure both forward and backward privacy. Forward privacy will safeguard against
the compromise of current session keys from enabling unauthorised access to past
sessions. Meanwhile, backward privacy will be implemented to prevent disclosure
of future session keys even if a current session key is compromised.
4. Confidentiality: Our solution will integrate the lightweight cryptographic algo-
rithm such as SIMON and SPECK, ensuring the protection of sensitive data within
the constrained IoT network. The SIMON and SPECK will be used to generate
symmetric key which is passed on to each authenticated node for maintaining the
confidentiality of data transmissions. The symmetric key is encrypted through the
public key of each node and sent to them so that each node, that can decrypt it using
their own private key. This further ensures high security.
5. Session Creation: Secure communication sessions will be established using dy-
namically managed encryption keys. This process involves generating and distribut-
ing encryption keys securely to the nodes, making sure that each session maintains
confidentiality and is protected against unauthorized access.

The implementation will be carried out on IoT networks operating with the RPL proto-
col, utilizing the Contiki-NG operating system. The Cooja Simulator will be used for
testing and validation. The solution will aim to provide a secure communication proto-
col that not only meets the authentication and confidentiality needs of IoT networks but
also guarantees forward and backward privacy, which is really important for maintaining
security when nodes leave or join the network. This method will be scalable, resource-
efficient, and compatible with existing IoT infrastructures, making it a practical solution
for enhancing the security of RPL-based IoT networks.

Conclusions and Future Work
EACMR-IoT addresses significant security weaknesses in the RPL-based IoT system by
including strong cryptographic methods that are optimised for resource-limited settings.
Furthermore, it employs the Elliptic Curve Cryptography (ECC) for robust authentication
and the lightweight cryptography of Simon and Speck to guarantee its data secrecy. This
integration not only improves security but also preserves resource efficiency, which is
essential for IoT devices with restricted power and computing capacities.
In addition, the EACMR-IoT framework will provide a key revocation mechanism to
effectively invalidate compromised keys, hence enhancing network security against po-
tential data breaches. The implementation of the RPL protocol change in the Contiki-
NG operating system demonstrates the effective utilisation of a secure routing protocol,
thereby improving the security of IoT communications against unauthorised access and
cyber threats in general. Furthermore, the system’s forward and backward privacy mea-
sures guarantee that in the event of session keys being stolen, the security of both previous
and future conversations remains uncompromised.
Conclusively, the EACMR-IoT framework offers a compact and effective solution that
greatly enhances the security of IoT networks, making it highly suitable for implementa-
tion in important areas such as smart cities, healthcare, and military applications.
