1.2 Scope
The scope of the project entails creating an AI system that enables accurate and efficient
medical transcription generation through communication between a doctor and a patient
either online or onsite. The main functionalities encompasses a speech to text system that
captures and writes down all information that was captured in the medical dialogue and
regardless of some challenges like background noise, medical words, etc. This solution
will also incorporate speaker split to determine the talker as the doctor or the patient
during recording for clear transcription later. Also an audio based chatbot will be added
to assist in patient friendly session of collecting initial information or data from patients
prior to the doctor attending to them. This decrease the necessity for doctors or assistants
to ask questions manually and facilitates the completion of the consultation. The system
will be integrated with telemedicine platform for online consultation. This will be a very
convenient solution for patients that cannot travel or for those who live in rural areas
where professional or relevant doctors are not available. For those patients who want to
have consultation with the relevant doctors onsite, the system will also be integrated with
Google Maps so that they can find the relevant doctors nearby for onsite consultation.
The initial reports will be shared with the doctors for analysis before consultation. The
system will incorporate speech recognition technology and natural language processing
(NLP) to generate initial and accurate reports of patients through their interaction with
the chatbot. The reports will then be shared with the relevant doctor for further analysis.
During patient’s and doctor’s conversation, transcription will be generated. The errors
will be rectified in both the initial reports and transcripts to avoid any misconception or
discrepancies. The system will be designed and available as a service to patients and
healthcare providers.

2.2 Functional Requirements
• Procedures:
This system monitors conversations or dialogues between the doctor and patient and
prescriptions given by the doctor. The system processes the conversation through
speech-to-text technology. The transcription is diarized, separating the doctor’s
contribution and the patient’s input. Transcription is then shared with the doctor
and patient. The physician checks the transcription and adds any remarks or other
medical remarks. That transcription is then stored within the system’s database for
future reference.
• Exception: If there are still some errors or missing information in transcription, the
doctor can manually correct them.
2.2 Functional Requirements
FR 1: Speech-to-Text Transcription
• This would enable the system to transcribe real-life conversations between physi-
cians and patients in printed text with reasonable accuracy.
• The system shall transcribe real-time audio conversations into text.
• The system should remove background noises and other irrelevant sounds during
transcription.
• The system should also record the medical terms mentioned in the conversation.
• Every speaking doctor and patient will be associated with their respective conver-
sations when the system is translating.
• It won’t err in transcription during pauses and breaks in conversation.
FR 2: Speaker Identification and Diarization
• It would basically help point out who was speaking, if it was a physician or a patient.
• The system must identify speakers by their names or enroll them as the patient or
doctor.
• It will hence make an accurate diarization of a multi-speaker conversation.
• There must also be provision for human override in case of misidentification of
speakers.

System Overview
It is a patient-doctor interaction system powered by telemedicine capabilities and a chat-
bot, mostly based on gathering and exchanging patient medical information for consul-
tation and accurate transcription generation from consultation. The system will provide
smooth interactions between patients and doctors, collect health information, generate
accurate transcription, and enable online discussions through a telemedicine platform or
onsite at a physical location.
3.0.1 Functionality:
1. User Authentication:
It allows patients and doctors to log in securely through authentication. It has a
feature of registering a new user by filling a form. Different dashboards are received
by the patients or doctors on entering their credentials.
2. Chatbot Interaction:
It has a chatbot interacting with the patient to gather some of the initial medical
information, such as symptoms, history, diet, and lifestyle information, right from
the informal background of the patient. The natural language processing of the
chatbot will gather this information accurately with its conversational interface.
3. Report Generation:
The data taken by it from patients would be processed by the system to result in a
structured medical report. Such a report indicates the patient’s state of health and
shall be presented to a doctor for further consultancy.
4. Consultation Options:
Two consultation options for the patients:

Online consultation: It is a telemedicine platform that enables consulting with a
doctor using video/audio.
On-site Consultation: Clinic visits to the doctor where the system shall be aligned
with Google Maps in locating physicians nearby.
5. Doctor Interaction:
Doctor would have the reports of the patient, and they can analyze data collected
and schedule a consultation with the patient either online or onsite. They can also
view patient history for better analysis.
6. Speech to Text and Transcription:
The system captures all the conversations between the doctor and patient during
onsite or online consultations. Speech is converted into text, and then the transcript
of conversation is generated and shared with both the doctor and the patient and
saved for their record keeping and follow up purposes.
3.0.2 Context:
It will exist in the health space, trying to improve communications between patients
and doctors whose friction around data sharing and communication will be reduced. It
will avail of the modern technologies of chatbots, NLP, speech-to-text transcription, and
telemedicine platforms in an attempt to reach out to more patients with efficient consulta-
tions.
3.0.3 Design:
Several interconnected modules make up the system’s design:
1. Easy-to-use interface: Patients as well as doctors interact through a comfortable
and intuitive interface, wherein login registration with dashboards managing reports
and consultation with the patient are included.
2. Backend Processing: This involves processing algorithms that power the chat-
bot and report generation feature through the processing of structured medical data
from the patient’s initial input.
3. Telemedicine Link: Connects to integrated telemedicine platform which can offer
a virtual consultation and Google Maps that can identify nearby doctors for in-
person consultations.
4. Transcription: Speech-to-texting technology captures conversation and transcribes
their documents for the benefit of both parties to share.

3.0.4 Background Information:
This project reflects the general trend of digital transformation in healthcare as telemedicine
and AI-based health care systems have been taken to the center. Telemedicine creates
some solutions that become inevitable by allowing a reduction of physical visits to hos-
pitals and faster, more effective treatment of the patients. Second, the chatbot technology
addresses the issue of AI-based patient intake, making doctors pay less attention to col-
lecting initial data and more to treatment in a further stage. This solution also responds
to the increasing need for telehealth services, especially in instances where onsite visits
may not be possible-for example during a pandemic or by patients in quite far areas. This
development underscores a necessity to integrate medical expertise with technology when
developing new tools that are expected to improve health-process-related factors, upgrade
the patient’s experience, and make doctors rely on time-to-date data in making accurate
diagnoses.
