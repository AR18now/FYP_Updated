Project: EDA - Enhanced Driver Assist System

1. INTRODUCTION
1.1 Purpose
This document outlines the requirements for the Enhanced Driver Assist (EDA) system, which aims to improve road safety by assisting drivers with real-time alerts and monitoring.

1.2 Scope
The EDA system integrates three primary modules: driver attention monitoring (via in-cabin camera), lane departure monitoring (via road camera), and driving pattern analysis (via OBD-II data). It provides voice alerts and is designed for highways and motorways in Pakistan.

1.3 Definitions, Acronyms, Abbreviations
- EDA: Enhanced Driver Assist
- OBD-II: On-Board Diagnostics II
- MTBF: Mean Time Between Failures
- TPU: Tensor Processing Unit

2. OVERALL DESCRIPTION
2.1 Product Perspective
EDA aims to reduce traffic accidents caused by driver fatigue, distraction, and lane departure by providing timely alerts and feedback, especially on highways.

2.2 Product Functions (Summary)
- Driver Attention Monitoring (drowsiness detection).
- Lane Detection and Departure Warning.
- Driving Pattern Monitoring (via OBD-II).
- Voice Alerts.
- Hardware Integration (Raspberry Pi/Nvidia Jetson Nano, Coral TPU, dashcams).

2.3 User Classes and Characteristics
- Driver: Primary user who receives alerts and feedback.
- Fleet Manager: Oversees multiple vehicles and reviews aggregated data.

2.4 Operating Environment
In-vehicle hardware (Raspberry Pi/Nvidia Jetson Nano) with connected cameras and OBD-II sensor.

3. FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS
3.1 Driver Attention Monitoring
FR1.1: The system shall detect driver drowsiness based on eye closure duration.
FR1.2: The system shall issue an alert if the driver's attention is diverted for more than 5 seconds.

3.2 Lane Departure Monitoring
FR2.1: The system shall detect lane markers on highways and monitor the vehicle's position.
FR2.2: The system shall warn the driver when the vehicle departs from its lane.

3.3 Driving Pattern Analysis
FR3.1: The system shall analyze OBD-II data for speed, acceleration, and rpm.
FR3.2: The system shall generate reports for unsafe driving behavior (harsh acceleration, over-speeding).

3.4 Voice Alert
FR4.1: The system shall deliver voice alerts for potential hazards and driver assistance.
FR4.2: The system shall process alerts locally on edge devices for minimal response time.

3.5 Hardware Implementation and Integration
FR5.1: The system shall integrate OBD-II sensors for vehicle data collection.
FR5.2: The system shall stream videos from in-cabin and out-cabin cameras to the models.
FR5.3: The system shall run lightweight models locally on the device.

4. NON-FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS
4.1 Reliability
NFR1: The system shall maintain an MTBF of at least 1000 hours.
NFR2: The system shall perform regular self-checks and log errors.

4.2 Usability
NFR3: The system shall allow a driver to access driving behavior feedback.
NFR4: The system shall enable fleet managers to generate reports on driving patterns.

4.3 Performance
NFR5: The system shall analyze OBD-II data and provide feedback within 4 seconds of data collection.
NFR6: Voice alerts shall be processed and delivered within 4 seconds of detection.
NFR7: Lane detection warnings shall have an approximate accuracy of 80% or higher.

4.4 Security
NFR8: The system shall limit access to sensitive data to authorized personnel only.
NFR9: The system shall log all access attempts to sensitive data.
