General
Users
General users are individuals, aged 18 and above, who seek mental health
support, particularly those experiencing anxiety, depression, or stress. They
interact with the AI-powered chatbot for mental health assessments, receive
personalized recommendations, and may use the professional directory to
find psychologists. General users prioritize anonymity, ease of use, and
quick access to mental health resources.
System Ad-
ministrators
System administrators are responsible for managing the technical operations
of Doctor AI, including ensuring user data security, performing system up-
dates, and maintaining the professional directory. They ensure that the sys-
tem runs smoothly by overseeing both the backend (e.g., database manage-
ment) and the frontend (e.g., user interface, functionality), ensuring user
data is handled securely.
Project Man-
agers and
Stakeholders
Project managers and stakeholders oversee the development and progress of
Doctor AI, ensuring the platform aligns with the project’s objectives. They
track key performance indicators such as user adoption rates, system per-
formance, and overall platform impact. Project managers require access to
system metrics, user reports, and progress dashboards to ensure the project
stays on track.

Chapter 3
System Overview
Doctor AI is a web-based mental health support platform that utilizes AI and Natural Lan-
guage Processing (NLP) to offer users personalized mental health assessments and guid-
ance. The platform’s functionality revolves around three core features: an AI-powered
chatbot, sentiment analysis, and personalized recommendations. Users can interact with
the chatbot to express their emotions, ask questions, and receive mental health support.
Through NLP, the chatbot evaluates the user’s emotional state in real time and generates
sentiment analysis, which provides insights into their mental well-being. Based on this
analysis, the platform offers tailored recommendations, such as mental health exercises,
relevant articles, or connections to professional mental health providers, ensuring person-
alized and actionable support.

3.1 Architectural Design
Figure 3.1: Architecture design diagram
3.1.1 User Interaction Module
• Role: Users interact with the system by logging in, sending queries, and receiving
responses.
• Relation: The module forwards user queries to the text-processing module and
displays the system’s responses back to the user.
3.1.2 Text Processing and Fine-tuning (LLM)
• Role: Pre-processes the user input using tokenization, lemmatization, and fine-
tuning of the language model (LLM) with a mental health dataset.
• Relation: Passes the processed text to the sentiment analysis module for further
processing.
20
3.2 Design Models
3.1.3 Sentiment Analysis Module
• Role: Converts the processed text into embeddings and analyzes the user’s senti-
ment.
• Relation: Sends sentiment results to the recommendation engine to guide the next
actions.
3.1.4 NER and Recommendation Engine
• Role: Extracts important entities (like medical terms) and compares them with a
database of mental health resources to provide personalized recommendations.
• Relation: Uses sentiment analysis and entity recognition to offer tailored guidance
and exercises.
3.1.5 Data Storage and Reporting Module
• Role: Stores user data and reports.
• Relation: Ensures user reports and data are securely saved and accessible for future
retrieval of reports.
