Project: InsightWire - News Aggregation with Bias Detection

1. INTRODUCTION
1.1 Problem Statement
In today's Information Age, abundance of news media has led to polarization caused by biased news sources. News companies often have their own agendas, controlling narratives for political and social plans. The layman blindly trusts these news sources.

1.2 Scope
InsightWire aggregates news articles from 18-20 political news sources, providing bias detection classification (left, center, right), contextual background with timelines, story comparison across outlets, news analytics, and an AI chatbot with RAG integration for user interaction and summarization.

1.3 Definitions, Acronyms, Abbreviations
- AI: Artificial Intelligence
- RAG: Retrieval-Augmented Generation
- LLM: Large Language Model
- RBAC: Role-Based Access Control

2. OVERALL DESCRIPTION
2.1 Product Perspective
InsightWire provides a balanced view of current events, helping users identify and understand inherent biases while offering deeper understanding of news landscape through analytics.

2.2 Product Functions (Summary)
- News aggregation from diverse sources
- Categorization and tagging
- Customization and personalization
- Local news highlighting
- AI chatbot with summarization
- User feedback and comments
- Bias detection classification
- Story comparison with tone/language highlighting
- Contextual background timeline
- News analytics for trend identification

2.3 User Classes and Characteristics
- General News Consumers: Seek reliable, balanced news sources
- Researchers and Analysts: Conduct in-depth research on news trends
- News Providers: Contribute content to reach broader users
- IT and Development: Responsible for development, maintenance, updates
- Potential Investors: Interested in growth and potential of InsightWire

2.4 Operating Environment
Web application, distributed NoSQL database (MongoDB), Chrome browser compatible.

3. FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS
3.1 News Aggregation, Categorization, and Personalization
FR1.1: The system shall retrieve and aggregate news articles from 18-20 sources.
FR1.2: The system shall only retrieve articles categorized as political.
FR1.3: The system shall group articles about the same event together.
FR1.4: The system shall tag articles with publication source, date, and political bias classification.
FR1.5: The system shall allow users to create profiles.
FR1.6: The system shall enable users to customize news feed by selecting interests and political balance levels.
FR1.7: The system shall determine user location to highlight relevant local news.

3.2 Chatbot, User Interaction, and Feedback
FR2.1: The system shall include a chatbot responding to user queries.
FR2.2: The chatbot shall summarize specific news articles.
FR2.3: The chatbot shall provide insights into themes/trends across minimum 3 sources for same story.
FR2.4: The system shall allow users to submit reviews on articles.
FR2.5: The system shall allow users to leave comments on articles.

3.3 Bias Detection and Story Comparison
FR3.1: The system shall analyze political bias, classifying into left, center, right.
FR3.2: The system shall, upon request, display articles from two sources on same topic with highlighted differences.
FR3.3: The system shall generate summary of detected bias for each story with single-click access.

3.4 Contextual Background and News Analytics
FR4.1: The system shall display timeline of related historical events for each story.
FR4.2: The system shall perform analytics on news articles, identifying trends or predictions mentioned in content.
FR4.3: The system shall allow users to filter contextual timeline by date.

4. NON-FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS
4.1 Reliability
NFR1: System uptime ≥ 99.9%.
NFR2: Aggregation shall continue even if up to 2 external sources fail.

4.2 Usability
NFR3: Intuitive user interface with clear navigation paths.
NFR4: Users shall customize interface to enhance experience.

4.3 Performance
NFR5: System shall handle up to 200 concurrent users without degradation.
NFR6: Chatbot response time ≤ 10 seconds.
NFR7: News analytics retrieval ≤ 5 minutes.

4.4 Scalability
NFR8: Distributed NoSQL database (MongoDB) shall allow scaling as users and articles increase.

4.5 Portability
NFR9: System accessible on Chrome without additional plugins.
NFR10: Responsive design across desktop, tablet, and mobile devices.

4.6 Security
NFR11: User authentication via HTTPS ensuring encrypted data transmission.
NFR12: Role-based access control (RBAC) for sensitive operations.
NFR13: Strong password requirements (≥ 8 characters, uppercase, number, special character).
NFR14: User input sanitization to prevent XSS attacks.

4.7 Availability
NFR15: 24/7 availability with planned maintenance ≤ 2 hours/month.
NFR16: Users notified of planned maintenance ≥ 24 hours in advance.
