1. Introduction 
The purpose of this report is to present a detailed plan on the entire development cycle for SkillSeeker. 
Here we discuss constraint, process flows, delivery and all other aspects of our project. 
 
Finding professional services can be a daunting task for layman users. The process often takes upwards 
of 60 minutes, involving extensive searching to identify valid professionals. Users frequently face the 
frustration of making countless calls to multiple service providers, only to be met with complex 
decision-making processes that require more expertise than they possess. Moreover, the lack of specific 
niche search options further complicates the task, leading to a significant amount of unnecessary 
legwork. This inefficiency and the time -consuming nature of the search process hinder users from 
quickly and easily accessing the professional services they need. 
 
We propose a system for Layman users to find these professional services through the use of extremely 
simple prompts. 
 
1.1 Problem Statement 
Let’s take a real-world example, Ahmed has been experiencing persistent back pain and needs to find 
a doctor. However, as he searches online, he becomes overwhelmed by complex medical terms like 
"orthopedic specialist" and "rheumatologist." Unsure of what he needs, John spends n early an hour 
scrolling through websites and making calls to clinics. Each conversation adds to his confusion, as 
receptionists use technical language he doesn't understand. What should have been a simple task turns 
into a frustrating and time-consuming ordeal, leaving John no closer to finding the right doctor. 
Layman users often struggle to locate the right professionals, particularly in specialized fields like law, 
computer science, and medicine. For instance, someone needing legal advice for a business contract 
may not know whether to consult a corporate lawyer or a contract attorney, while a person facing a 
cybersecurity issue might be unsure if they need a cybersecurity expert or a network engineer. In the 
medical field, a user with back pain might be confused about whether to see an orthopedic specialist, 
rheumatologist, or chiropractor. This confusion leads to wasted time and resources, and often results in 
users receiving help that isn't tailored to their specific needs, further complicating their situations. 
 
1.2 Problem Solution 
 
Introducing an all -in-one LLM application designed to bridge the gap between layman users and 
professional services. This chatbot-driven solution allows users to input their needs in simple 
terms, without requiring any prior knowledge of specific professions. The application then 
generates a comprehensive list of tailored professio nals, complete with user customization 
options and role management features. Additionally, it offers seamless booking functionalities, 
ensuring that users can easily connect with the right service providers.

4.1 Conclusion 
 
In summary, this project aims to revolutionize how users access professional services by 
introducing an all-in-one LLM application that simplifies and personalizes the search process. 
By leveraging a fully automated chatbot, users can easily input their needs and receive tailored 
recommendations. The application includes comprehensive features such as multilingual 
support, voice interaction, and an integrated booking system. With additional functionalities like 
a "Near Me" feature and a sophisticated history and personalization module, users will benefit 
from a streamlined, efficient, and user-friendly experience, ensuring they find the right 
professionals quickly and effectively. 
 
This application will allow for a streamlined process, complete transparency, and up to date 
results for any profession.
