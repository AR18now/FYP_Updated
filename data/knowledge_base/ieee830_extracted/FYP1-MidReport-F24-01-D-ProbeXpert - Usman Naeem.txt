1. Introduction
1.3 Modules
1.3.1 External Asset Scanning
This module automates the scanning of publicly accessible assets, such as IP addresses
and domain names, to identify potential vulnerabilities. It enables businesses to secure
their external digital presence without requiring extensive manual intervention.
1. Automated Scanning: Initiates On-demand scans of external systems to detect vul-
nerabilities.
2. Customizable Scan Parameters: Allows users to select from 3 scan types; i.e. Deep
scan, Shallow Scan, and Individual scan. Users can add or remove the tools of their
choice in these scans.
1.3.2 Risk Factor Calculation & Vulnerability Assessment
This module calculates the risk factors for each vulnerability, prioritizing them based on
environmental variables, network characteristics, and exploitability [11]. It ensures that the
most crucial vulnerabilities are addressed first by assigning risk values that consider the
severity of the vulnerability and the context in which it resides.
1. Environmental Variables Analysis: Incorporates network-specific variables (e.g.,
open ports, total vulnerabilities, average organization risk) into the risk assessment
to tailor the risk score for each vulnerability based on its environment.
2. Risk Factor Calculation: Calculates the risk factor by considering the probability
of occurrence and impact, using factors such as the number of vulnerabilities, open
ports, and exploitation likelihood.
3. Quantitative Score Categorization: Assigns risk levels to identified vulnerabilities
based on severity, likelihood, and impact.
4. Prioritization of Vulnerabilities: Assign a priority ranking to each vulnerability by
calculating a composite score that reflects both the vulnerability’s severity and the
environmental factors.
1.3.3 Real-Time Progress Tracking
This module monitors the progress of scans and vulnerability assessments in real-time,
giving users continuous updates on the status and performance of ongoing security oper-

1. Live Scan Results: Displays real-time progress of active scans, including percent-
age completion and time remaining.
2. Alerts and Notifications: Sends alerts for completed scans, allowing users to take
immediate action on detected vulnerabilities.
3. Estimated Time of Completion: Offers estimated times for scan completion based

1.3.4 Reporting
The reporting module is responsible for generating detailed reports based on the results
of vulnerability scans and risk assessments. These reports provide valuable insights for
compliance, risk management, and decision-making.
1. Detailed Vulnerability Reports: Includes comprehensive information on identified
vulnerabilities, including severity, risk factors, and prioritization table.[11]
2. Risk Score Visualization: Includes graphical representations of vulnerability risk
scores, making it easier for non-technical stakeholders to interpret the data.
3. Risk Summary: Provides an executive summary of the overall security posture,
including risk levels and top vulnerabilities.
4. Exportable Format: Provides the option to export reports in PDF format, allowing
for easy sharing with stakeholders and ensuring compatibility for documentation
and compliance purposes.

Chapter 3
System Overview
ProbeXpert is an online platform created for small and medium-sized businesses to au-
tomate external asset scanning, vulnerability assessment, and compliance reporting. It
tackles the issues of scarce IT resources and knowledge through a user-friendly interface
that identifies and ranks vulnerabilities in assets like IP addresses and domains.
The system scans outside platforms, evaluates weaknesses using environmental and net-
work factors, and determines which ones to fix first. It offers instant updates on scanning
progress and creates thorough reports that can be saved in PDF for compliance needs.
ProbeXpert guarantees expandability and adaptability due to its modular design. Basic
functions such as scanning, risk evaluation, and reporting work separately, with assistance
from Kubernetes for distributing workloads and Docker containers for separating services.
This design boosts dependability and guarantees the platform’s efficiency and security,
even during high traffic.
3.1 Architectural Design
The structure of ProbeXpert consists of four main components: External Asset Scan-
ning Module, Risk Factor Calculation and Vulnerability Assessment Module, Real-Time
Progress Tracking Module, and Reporting Module. Every module manages different
functions yet works together to provide a smooth and effective vulnerability detection
and reporting process.

3. System Overview
3.1.1 Modules
Modules 1: Scanning external Assets
This module starts and controls the scanning procedure for external assets such as IP
addresses and domain names. It allows users to conduct scans as needed, with adjustable
parameters, offering a choice of scan types (Deep, Shallow, or Individual) and the ability
to pick the tools for the scan. The results of the scan are examined and forwarded for
additional processing.
Communications:
i. The Flask handler coordinates requests and schedules scans, overseeing plugins and
assigning tasks to Docker worker nodes for processing.
ii. Receives analyzed scan findings from the Worker Nodes and sends them to the Risk
Factor Calculation module for additional handling.
iii. Gives scan status updates to the Real-Time Progress Tracking Module.
Module 2: Risk Factor Calculations & Vulnerability Detection
This module evaluates the scan findings using environmental factors such as open ports
and vulnerability count and computes the risk scores. It categorizes vulnerabilities based
on quantitative risk levels, considering severity, likelihood of exploitation, and impact on
the network as factors.
Communications:
i. Gets parsed scan results from the External Asset Scanning Module.
ii. Utilizes enrichment worker nodes to process data for risk score calculation and to
enhance the overall risk posture.
iii. Share the results that have been enhanced and evaluated with the Reporting Module
for creating reports.
Module 3: Real-Time Progress Tracking
The module for tracking progress in real-time keeps a constant watch on the ongoing
scans. Users are given real-time updates, such as completion percentage, time left esti-
mates, and scan reports. Notifications and alerts are triggered upon completion of scans
or in case of errors.
Communications:

i. Consistently interacts with the External Asset Scanning Module and the Risk Factor
Calculation Module to obtain the most recent updates and progress metrics.
ii. Sends alerts and notifications to users via the web interface.
iii. Gives live status updates to be shown on the web application.
Module 4: Reporting
This module generates detailed reports based on the analysis and risk assessment data.
The reports include vulnerability details, risk scores, and an executive summary of the
organization’s security posture. Graphical representations, such as bar charts, pie charts,

3.3.2 Major Data/System Entities
i. User Inputs (IP/Domain & Scan Parameters)
• Data Structure: JSON format containing IP addresses, domain names, and
selected scan parameters (scan type, tools, etc.).
• Storage: NoSQL database (e.g., MongoDB).
• Processing: The system stores and uses these inputs to initiate and configure
scans in the External Asset Scanning Module.
ii. Scan Results:
• Data Structure: JSON for scan results, detailing vulnerabilities, severity lev-
els, and metadata (e.g., scan time, IP address).
• Storage: Stored temporarily in NoSQL database (MongoDB) for fast query-
ing.
• Processing: Retrieved by the Risk Factor Calculation & Vulnerability Assess-
ment Module for further analysis.
iii. Vulnerability and Risk Reports:
• Data Structure: JSON format, containing vulnerability reports, risk scores,
and prioritization details.
• Storage: NoSQL database (e.g., MongoDB).
• Processing: Used by the Reporting Module for report generation and risk
visualization.
