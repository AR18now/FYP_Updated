INTRODUCTION
Purpose: The purpose of this Software Requirements Specification (SRS) document is to define the detailed requirements for the development of an e-commerce food delivery platform that will facilitate the online ordering and delivery of meals from various restaurants to end-users.
Scope: The scope of this project encompasses the design, implementation, testing, and deployment of the food delivery application. It does not include physical infrastructure required for delivery services.
Definitions/Acronyms:
- E-commerce: Electronic commerce involving the transfer of goods over digital networks.
- API: Application Programming Interface used for integrating different systems.
- SQL: Structured Query Language for managing relational databases.
References:
- IEEE Standard for Software Requirements Specifications (IEEE Std 830-1998). OVERALL DESCRIPTION
Product Perspective: The food delivery application aims to offer a seamless and user-friendly experience for both consumers and businesses involved in the food industry. The application will integrate with existing restaurant management systems and deliver food directly to customers' locations via third-party couriers.
Product Functions:
- User account creation and management.
- Browse and search for available food items across participating restaurants.
- Place and track food orders.
- Secure payment methods for transactions.
- Provide ratings and reviews for restaurants and dishes.
- Real-time order status updates for customers.
Constraints:
- Limited budget for initial development phase.
- Integration with third-party logistics providers.
Assumptions/Dependencies:
- Availability of internet connectivity for all users.
- Existing backend systems capable of handling transactional data. SPECIFIC REQUIREMENTS
External Interface Requirements:
- User Interface: Intuitive and responsive design suitable for mobile devices and desktop computers.
- Hardware: Compatible with standard personal computers and smartphones.
- Software: Requires web browser support and compatibility with popular operating systems.
- Communication: Secure HTTP(S) connections for transmitting sensitive information.
Functional Requirements:
FR-1: User Account Creation and Management
Input: Personal identification details including name, email address, password, and contact number.
Processing: Validate inputs, hash passwords, and store user information in a secure manner.
Output: Confirmation message indicating successful account creation along with login credentials.
Priority: High
FR-2: Browse and Search for Available Food Items
Input: Keyword searches or filters by cuisine type, price range, etc.
Processing: Retrieve matching records from the database and present them in a categorized format.
Output: List of food items with images, descriptions, prices, and ratings.
Priority: High
FR-3: Place and Track Food Orders
Input: Selected food items, quantity, delivery address, preferred time slot.
Processing: Generate order summary, calculate total cost, validate payment method, update inventory, and notify courier service.
Output: Confirmation page displaying order details and estimated delivery time.
Priority: Medium
FR-4: Secure Payment Methods
Input: Credit card details or other approved payment gateways.
Processing: Encrypt payment information before sending to server, verify transaction authenticity.
Output: Receipt confirmation sent to user's registered email.
Priority: High
Non-functional Requirements:
Performance: Ensure response times do not exceed 5 seconds under normal load conditions.
Security: Implement encryption protocols to protect user data during transmission and storage.
Reliability: Maintain system uptime above 99%.
Usability: Design interfaces that are easy to navigate and understand.
Maintainability: Code shall be modular and well-documented for ease of maintenance.
Portability: Applications shall run seamlessly across different platforms and devices.
System Features:
Feature 1: Rating and Review System
Description: Customers can rate and review individual dishes and leave comments about their dining experiences.
Implementation: Store rating and review data in a dedicated table linked to specific menu entries. Feature 2: Real-Time Order Status Updates
Description: Customers receive notifications regarding the progress of their orders throughout the preparation and delivery process.
Implementation: Use MySQL to log timestamp events related to order status changes and push updates to client applications. Feature 3: Inventory Management
Description: Restaurant partners have access to tools for monitoring stock levels and adjusting supply accordingly.
Implementation: Develop APIs for integrating with existing POS systems to synchronize inventory data. End of Document.
