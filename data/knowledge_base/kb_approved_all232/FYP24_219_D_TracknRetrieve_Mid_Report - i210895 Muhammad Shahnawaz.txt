## 1. Introduction

1. Introduction
1.2 Scope
TracknRetrieve is to build a platform that simplifies the process of finding lost items.
Users can report lost items by providing detailed descriptions, which are used to retrieve
relevant images from Google. These images can be modified to better match the lost
items. For found items, users can upload images, and the system will automatically gen-
erate descriptions to assist in matching with lost reports.
The platform offers secure real-time messaging between users and finders, real-time no-
tifications when a match is found, and geo-location tagging to help focus the search area.
A voice-to-text feature is also included to make it easier for users to report items verbally,
and the system provides an option to report stolen or sensitive items directly to authorities.
In addition, a proof of ownership verification process ensures that only the rightful owner
can claim a found item.
The ’Donation Center’ module allows unclaimed items to be donated to charity, and the
system facilitates secure donations. Users can offer rewards for the return of their lost
items and track the status of their reward offers. The platform also supports community
alerts to notify nearby users about lost items and includes multilingual support to make
the system accessible to a broader audience.
The platform also includes a "Provide Reviews and Ratings" module, where users can
leave feedback on their experience, improving overall system performance and user trust.
TracknRetrieve does not include features such as price negotiation or a marketplace for
buying and selling items. Real-time image generation is also excluded, as images are
retrieved from Google instead. The focus remains on providing a simple, secure, and ef-
fective solution for lost item recovery without the complexities of additional e-commerce
or marketplace functionalities.

1. Introduction
User Class Description
Item
Owner/User
The Item Owner is an individual who has lost a personal item and uses
the system to recover it. They can report lost items, tag geo-locations,
and submit descriptions. The Item Owner can receive notifications about
found items that match their reports and provide reviews and ratings for
finders. They may modify retrieved images to match their lost items ac-
curately. Their primary focus is on successfully recovering their lost items
while maintaining privacy throughout the process.
Finder/User A Finder is an individual who discovers a lost item and wants to return it.
Finders can report and submit found items, upload images, and have the
system generate descriptions of those items. Finders may choose to remain
anonymous and can benefit from rewards offered by Item Owners for the
return of their items.
Admin The Admin manages the overall system, including user accounts, lost and
found item reports, and the Donation Center. Admins have the authority
to handle disputes. They ensure that the system operates smoothly, man-
age communications, and maintain user privacy. Admins interact with the
system regularly to oversee operations and assist users when needed.
Authorities Authorities include law enforcement or relevant organizations that receive
reports of stolen or sensitive items. They can access documentation pro-
vided by Item Owners. Their interactions with the system occur primarily
when sensitive items are reported, ensuring that they have all the necessary
information to assist in investigations.
Donation
Center
The Donation Center is a module within the system that processes unclaimed
items. It facilitates donations to charities or community organizations when
items remain unclaimed after a specified period. Its interaction with the
system occurs as needed based on unclaimed items.
