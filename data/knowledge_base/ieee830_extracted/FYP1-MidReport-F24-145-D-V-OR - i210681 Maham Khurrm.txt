Introduction 
The purpose of this document is to outline the requirements for the Virtual Operating Room 
(V-OR), an advanced, highly interactive and immersive platform, in which users can sight 
and practice complex surgeries in a fully virtual environment. By utilizing the finest virtual 
reality, this V-OR platform has developed an extremely realistic and interactive medical 
domain in which the surgeries like pacemakers implant as well as many other sorts of 
operations can be performed with infinite repetitions. VOR is on a mission to change the way 
medical training is conducted by providing learners with access to a practice environment 
that not only can be practiced anytime and anywhere, but also without real danger. This 
document is intended for a diverse group of stakeholders, each of whom will benefit from the 
detailed requirements and specifications presented herein. The main target audiences are: 
Developers, Project Managers, Users, Testers k and Documentation Writers. 
 
1.1 Problem Statement 
In today’s era it's hard for students in medicine to become skilled professionals due to lack of 
precision and accuracy in surgical training. Firstly, the biggest challenge they face is not 
having enough opportunities for hands-on practice, thus this leads to errors and inaccuracies 
during the real surgery. Additionally, the high cost of training tools such as physical 
simulators and other surgical training equipment makes it difficult for many schools, 
especially in areas with fewer resources, to afford them. Not only this, ethical concerns limit 
the use of cadavers (dead bodies), reducing opportunities for students to perform actual 
surgeries. As a result, students strive to build the confidence and skills necessary to perform 
the surgeries perfectly. This makes it hard for students to learn the procedures in depth as 
they are living in a controlled environment. It is important to address these issues so that 
future surgeons can perform procedures accurately and safely. The graph below illustrates 
the challenges that students face during medical training V-OR is a platform that aims to 
solve this by offering an affordable and ethical alternative for surgical training tools.

1.2 Scope 
Our V-OR aims to provide a highly interactive environment for surgeons to practice complex 
cardiac surgeries. By imitating real-life operating room conditions, it offers detailed 3D 
models, step-by-step guidance, and real-time feedback/suggestions to sweeten surgical skills 
without threat to patients. 
The surgeries we will focus on include: 
1. Atrial Septal Defect (ASD) Closure: 
This procedure involves repairing a hole in the septum that separates the heart's two upper 
chambers (atria). Via our V-OR, surgeons will practice closing this genetic heart defect, 
which can cause blood to flow abnormally between the chambers, leading to difficulties 
like heart failure or stroke. 
2. Pacemaker Implantation: 
 Pacemaker implantation is a life-saving strategy that concerns positioning a little 
electronic gadget under the skin to help restrain irregular heartbeats. Our V-OR will 
imitate the insertion of the pacemaker and its leads, linking them to the heart tissue. 
Surgeons will be trained to confirm the correct positioning of the pacemaker under the 
skin. 
3. Coronary Artery Bypass Grafting (CABG): 
CABG is one of the most typical and crucial surgeries for ministering coronary artery illness. 
This approach involves bypassing clogged or constricted arteries to fix normal blood flow to 
the heart. Our V-OR will provide instructions for graft harvesting (from the leg, arm, or 
chest), creating the bypass. 
1.3 Modules 
 
The V-OR platform consists of several modules, each designed to provide specific 
functionalities for medical training. 
2.2.1 Module 1: Surgical Simulation Engine 
This module involves creating a virtual environment for surgical training, which provides a 
realistic and interactive experience for users to fully utilize. This allows users to perform 
cardiac surgeries in a completely safe environment. 
● Feature 1: Interactive 3D models of organs, tissues, and surgical tools. 
● Feature 2: Step-by-step guidance to help users perform surgeries.. 
● Feature 3: Dynamic surgical scenarios to introduce real-world complexities.

2.2.2 Module 2: Virtual Training Mode 
This module enables medical students and professionals to safely practice surgeries in a 
controlled environment. The users can either follow step-by-step guidance or practice without 
it. While practicing without guidance users will be evaluated based on how accurately they 
perform surgery on their own. 
● Feature 1: Virtual training mode for practicing surgeries with or without step by-step 
guidance. 
● Feature 2: Independent practice mode, where users can perform surgeries without 
any guidance and are evaluated on their accuracy and skills. 
 
2.2.3 Module 3: Real-time Feedback and Performance Evaluation 
This module provides immediate feedback to users while performing surgeries, helping them 
identify their mistakes in real-time. Furthermore, it also evaluates the user's performance 
throughout the surgery. 
● Feature 1: Real-time error detection and then notifying users about the mistake. 
● Feature 2: A performance evaluation system that will track the user's actions and 
gives a detailed feedback on their overall performance. 
 
2.2.4 Module 4: User Management System 
This module manages user profiles, tracks their progress, and gives access to training 
simulations. It also allows doctors or instructors to check student records and monitor their 
progress through detailed reports. 
● Feature 1: User authentication and profile management. 
● Feature 2: Tracks and saves each user's progress and performance. 
● Feature 3: 3: Enables doctors or instructors to access student records and follow their 
training progress.

2.2 Functional Requirements 
This section describes the functional requirements of the system expressed in the natural 
language style. 
 
USER MANAGEMENT SYSTEM: 
● The system should allow users to login and manage their accounts. 
● User roles should include students, surgeons and administrators. 
● Users should be able to update their profiles, view their training process and feedback 
reports. 
● The system should track and securely save the user's progress in different training 
modules. 
 
SURGICAL SIMULATION ENGINE: 
● The platform should include interactive 3D models of organs, tissues and surgical 
tools. 
● The system should provide step by step guidance during the training surgeries. 
● The dynamic scenarios generated by the system should be like real- world 
complexities. 
 
VIRTUAL TRAINING MODE: 
● Users should be able to take an assessment surgery where no help will be provided 
and at the end they will be evaluated on their performance.

● Users should be able to choose the surgery they want to perform from the 3 options 
we are currently providing; ASD, pacemaker and CABG. 
 
REAL-TIME FEEDBACK & PERFORMANCE EVALUATION: 
● The system should be able to detect real-time errors during the surgery and notify the 
user. 
● The system should be able to provide detailed feedback and by using a predefined

● The system should provide an intuitive dashboard for users to navigate to the

System Overview 
3.1 Architectural Design 
The Virtual Operating Room system lets medical trainees and surgeons practice surgeries in a VR 
environment using VR headsets and hand controllers. An API Gateway connects the user’s device to the 
system's backend services. The Surgical Simulation Engine creates 3D models and offers step-by-step 
instructions. Users can choose to practice with or without these instructions, and the Real-Time Feedback 
system gives instant error detection and tracks performance. The User Management System takes care of 
logging in and tracking progress, while all information is stored in a central database. The project uses Git 
and GitHub to manage changes and help the team work together.

3.3 Data Design 
The information domain of the V-OR system is then converted to a form of structured data 
entitles stored in a MySQL data base. These tables contain significant entities like User, 
Surgery, Progress, Feedback ,etc. Like many other resources, the 3D anatomical models used 
during surgeries in operations plans are file-based and their paths are stored in the database 
for ease of access to the specific files at the time of simulations. This architecture must 
maintain data consistency and will perform data processing without inefficiency. 
Databases and Data Storage: 
MySQL Database: Database preserves tables such as users, surgeries, progress and feedback 
and etc. 
File System on Server: Saves the actual 3D anatomical models.
