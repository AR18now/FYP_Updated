2.1.1 Research Item 1
In this paper by Serguei Popov entitled ‘The Tangle,’ a background of IOTA, a crypto-
currency designed for IoT market is provided with explanation of the mathematical con-
cepts at its foundation. The author presents the notion of The Tangle, a new direction in
blockchain technologies based on a DAG, more appropriate for implementing machine-
to-machine micropayments. Special attention is paid to Markov Chain Monte Carlo
(MCMC) algorithm for selection of transaction attachment sites in the Tangle that is more
effective and scalable compared to blockchain systems.
2.1.1.1 Summary of the research item
In the paper, the author explores the Tangle of IOTA, a novel distributed ledger system
that eliminates mining and transaction fees, making it suitable for microtransactions of
IoT environments. In this system, for instance, every new transaction has to vouch for
two different previous transactions, thus creating a Directed Acyclic Graph (DAG). This
approach precludes some weaknesses seen in the traditional form of a blockchain like

2.1.2 Research Item 2
Research paper “Computing Resource Allocation Scheme for DAG-Based IOTA Nodes”,
in which the authors examine a solution to improve the response time of the IOTA plat-
form by incorporating load balancing mechanism. IOTA as an ML ledger technology
applied to the Internet of Things uses a DAG called the Tangle to solve issues with scal-
ability, cost per transaction, and latency related to the blockchain. In order to solve this
problem, this research presents a WLC algorithm for a productive distribution of the com-
putational tasks between the full nodes within the IOTA network.
2.1.2.1 Summary of the research item
The focus of the paper lies in the efficient management of resources for network-external
transactions for IOTA’s Tangle, a DAG-based system in which full and light nodes work
together to qualify transactions. Light nodes always submit their task requests to full
nodes, which have more computational capacity, and thus performance is usually some-
times significantly hampered. To this end, the authors have come up with Weighted Least
Connection (WLC) algorithm that determines the distribution of load across the full nodes
depending on computational ability of the nodes as well as the number of active connec-
tions. Using simulation and experiment the authors show the efficiency of WLC algorithm
in allocating the tasks equally to the nodes and making the IOTA network more scalable
and efficient. The findings of the study show that there is a reduction of resource exhaus-
tion related problems and enhance system stability of WLC.
2.1.2.2 Critical analysis of the research item
The paper offers an organised approach to solving one of the significant issues in the
IOTA Tangle and illustrates how the fair distribution of resources is essential for ensuring
the effectiveness of the system. The WLC algorithm adds new knowledge to this field
and provides investigators with a potent means to solve load-balance challenges in de-
centralized IoT settings. However, the various experiments reveal what if cases and still,
the paper fails to expound much on challenges that may be characteristic of its real world
deployment-o Node availability is dynamic and several external factors for instance, net-
work latency are bound to impact the entire system. Further, although the WLC algorithm
enhances the system’s task distributing, the study lacks comprehensive exposition of its
behavior under intensely enlarging examples like IoT systems with tens of thousands of
nodes. It would be preferred that future work could attempt to address these weaknesses
and assess the proposed algorithm in a number of more varied network dimensions.

2.1.5.1 Summary of the research item
The paper goes further to identify the scalability as the challenge with the current model
of blockchain whereby the Bitcoin blockchain can only handle 7 transactions per second
compared to Visa’s 47, 000. To address this, the authors introduce the Lightning Network
as an off-chain layered system of bidirectional micro payment channels where multiple
transactions are possible between contracting parties without burdening the block chain.
Such off-chain transactions occur when the need arises and when such transactions are put
on the blockchain, they employ features such as time lock and multiple signatures. More-
over, utilization of Hashed Time lock Contracts (HTLCs) that underpin the Lightning
Network mean that multi-hop payments are also possible and with scalability to billions
of transactions per day for micro payments and yet virtually no fee.
2.1.5.2 Critical analysis of the research item
The Lightning Network is a major enhancement of Bitcoin scalability, which presents
an off-chain payment solution for Bitcoin that is decentralized, instantaneous, and se-
cure while avoiding the burden of bloating the blockchain with micropayment data. It
is most appropriate for micropayment since it has a feature of micropayment channels,
which solve the problem of high costs and time consumption in BTC. Nevertheless, the
following key issues are still questionable: Channel capacity problem bottlenecks which
may hinder users to accommodate for higher value payments and the problem of secur-
ing and increasing the strength of the participants’ network for relaying the payments.
Furthermore, the supposed taxing of few channels, and many transactions might demoti-
vate average users; the supposed future usage of a Bitcoin protocol soft-fork to deal with
malleability issues might also be a problem since the community might not like it.

Chapter 3
Proposed Approach
To propose a solution for the research problem, which is to design a fee-free, reward-
incentivized framework for decentralized applications (dApps) using IOTA’s Tangle, we
can break down the approach into different stages. Below is a methodology and theoreti-
cal framework, including a Consensus Algorithm and Reward Mechanism that addresses
the research problem.
3.1 Theoretical Framework
The research focuses on developing a modified consensus protocol for IOTA’s Tangle,
integrating a reward mechanism to incentivize users and ensure greater participation as
nodes. The approach is twofold:
3.1.1 Consensus Protocol Modification:
• Adapt the existing IOTA Tangle to include lightweight nodes with low computa-
tional overhead.
• Introduce a dynamic reward system that compensates participants for validating
transactions.
• The reward mechanism will be designed to operate seamlessly with the IOTA Tan-
gle. It aims to encourage participation by providing incentives for validating trans-
actions and contributing to the network’s security and scalability.

3.2 Methodology
The methodology involves several stages:
3.2.1 Research and Literature Review
• Analyze current consensus protocols in Distributed Ledger Technologies (DLTs),
with a specific focus on IOTA and Directed Acyclic Graph (DAG)-based consensus
mechanisms.
• Investigate reward systems used in decentralized applications to explore efficient,
scalable models that encourage participation.
3.2.2 Design of Reward Incentive Mechanism
• Develop a reward structure that fairly compensates users for validating transactions
and reduces transaction costs.
• Define how rewards can be earned and spent, ensuring that the reward mechanism
integrates well with the IOTA ecosystem.
3.2.3 Node Participation Enhancement
• Create interfaces and lightweight node implementations to make it easier for users
to act as nodes.
• Ensure that users with limited resources (e.g., low computational power) can still
contribute meaningfully to the network.
3.2.4 Integration with IOTA Tangle
• Extend IOTA’s existing Tangle protocol to include the reward mechanism.
• Ensure the modifications maintain IOTA’s scalability, security, and fee-free nature.
3.2.5 Simulation and Testing
• Use simulation tools like Hyperledger Fabric to test the performance of the pro-
posed system under various network conditions.

• Analyze metrics such as scalability, latency, network resilience, and security.
3.3 Consensus Algorithm Framework
The consensus algorithm framework will include the following components:
3.3.1 Node Validation Mechanism:
Users participate as nodes by validating transactions and each transaction must approve
two previous transactions in the Tangle, creating a DAG structure.
3.3.2 Reward System:
Nodes that validate transactions receive rewards based on their contributions and rewards
are generated dynamically based on the network’s activity and the amount of computa-
tional power contributed by the node.
3.3.3 Lightweight Node Implementation:
Enable resource-constrained users to participate as nodes by minimizing the computa-
tional requirements and provide a simplified interface for these users to validate transac-
tions.
