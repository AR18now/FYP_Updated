1.2 Scope
This project’s scope is centered on the UK market and includes developing a system that
streamlines important tasks for Amazon Seller Accounts. With the intent of streamlining
seller operations, the system will have features like dynamic pricing, sales analytics, and
product discovery.
The product discovery feature will provide niche-specific recommendations, assisting
sellers to identify profitable products across various categories. The solution will ana-
lyze products from the top five supermarkets, including Tesco and Sainsbury’s, for more
comprehensive market research so that sellers remain competing and make well-informed
decisions.
Real-time price adjustments will be made automatically by dynamic pricing in response
to competition activity and sales analytics will give insightful data on profitability, sales
patterns, and product performance. The solution will first monitor trends in a single prod-
uct category and alert sellers to significant modifications and updates in the market. This
feature can be extended to include additional categories in the future.
Supplier sourcing and acquisition will happen more quickly, saving sellers time and en-
ergy so they can focus on expanding their businesses. The system will also automate
routine tasks like listing updates and pricing modifications, assisting vendors in maintain-
ing their competitiveness. For Amazon sellers, the idea is to maximize revenue, enhance
decision-making, and save time by automating some of their most time-consuming oper-
ations.

2.2 Functional Requirements
2.2 Functional Requirements
This section describes the functional requirements of the system expressed in the natural
language style.
2.2.1 Product Discovery and Extraction
Following are the requirements for Product Discovery and Extraction:
1. System shall display the user current market trends in human readable form with
necessary information.
2. System shall allow user to enter product details and should cater any style of input.
3. System shall allow user to apply custom filters based on the option provided.
4. System shall correctly apply the filters and move from generalization to specializa-
tion for efficient sourcing.
5. System shall search for the entered product on different stores and correctly retrieve
the required information.
2.2.2 Supplier Sourcing
Following are the requirements for Supplier Sourcing:
1. System shall find the entered product on different stores specified in the scope.
2. System shall identify the suitable supplier by refining based on the profit margins.
3. System shall retrieve the required information about the product from the selected
suppliers.
4. System shall efficiently compare and match the relevant information such as title,
price and images.
2.2.3 Profitability Analysis and Listings
Following are the requirements for Profitability Analysis and Listings:
1. System shall calculate profit margins leveraging different sales and analytics tech-
niques.

3. System shall generate the required information about the product and efficiently
add the title, description, price and image in the right place.
2.2.4 Dynamic Pricing and Competitor Analysis
Following are the requirements for Dynamic Pricing and Competitor Analysis:
1. System shall relentlessly looks for changing competitors prices.
2. System shall constantly adjust the prices to be in the competitive race.
3. The system shall drop the product if it lacks profitability or sales activity.
2.2.5 Sales Analytics
Following are the requirements for v:
1. System shall present the sales and profit of different products in interactive manner.
2. System shall generate effective insights from the user sales history.
3. The system shall provide different visualization for easy and better understanding.
2.3 Non-Functional Requirements
This section specifies nonfunctional requirements.
2.3.1 Reliability
1. In case of failure in any kind of operation such as fetching data from DB, listing
product and product hunting cycle, System must rollback and try 2-3 times before
logging the error.
2. In case of continuous failure system must notify the user or administrator through
email.
3. The system shall have a minimum MTBF of 30 days to ensure continuous opera-
tions without unexpected downtime.
