Chapter 1 
 
Introduction 
 
Issue reports are a fundamental part of keeping a software system up to date and healthy in the 
modern age of software development. Issue reports are generally user -generated experiences 
and include bugs, features or questions with the products functionality. To be resolved i n an 
efficient way, reports must first pass through the intake process so that this data can be 
processed, categorized as a specific type of request or concern, and sent to the appropriate teams 
to resolve. With the increasing volume of these reports, manu al classification is simply not able 
to keep up and in turn develop cycles slow or critical fixes are delayed. 
To overcome this, CategorizeIT is developed to automatically classify issues based on 
revolutionary machine learning approaches powered by transf orming models. CategorizeIT aims 
to classify issue reports correctly into predefined categories,Bug, Enhancement, Question and 
help the management of issues. This automation can assist developers in prioritizing tasks , 
ensures quicker response times, and enhances overall software quality by reducing the manual 
effort involved in sorting and assigning issues. 
This is done by using natural language processing (NLP) and machine learning algorithms to 
process the text in issue reports and divert them to the ri ght category. In this way it allows dev 
teams to worry more about solving the real problems and spending less time managing with 
sorting huge issue reports. 
Like prior research, CategorizeIT also builds on top of pre -trained BERT models for attaining 
accurate categorization of issues. Issuable automation workflows and productivity enhancements 
are the cornerstone of this cutting -edge system, a necessity for any software development team 
in today's era. The project offers a web interface as well to the work for integrating with existing 
issue tracking systems that make more convenient to use and usable for developers and quality 
assurance teams. 
1.1 Problem Domain 
Efficient issue tracking & categorization is important to system maintenance, to a better user 
experience or to prompt updates when it comes to software development. As the size and 
complexity of software systems grow, the number of reported issues goes up rapidly as well. 
These reports take many different forms: from bug descriptions to new featur es or questions of 
the user. It is not only time -consuming but also error -prone; that means a lot misclassification of 
reports, delayed resolutions and you can imagine how useful feature would miss out being built 
as development resources has been wasted. 
The CategorizeIT project tackles this by working to automatically classify issue reports. The issue 
is in the manual process of sorting and categorizing these reports, taking up time and other

resources that could be better spent improving on actual softwa re. Additionally, incorrect 
categorization of issues can lead to delays in resolving critical bugs, while less important tasks may 
be prioritized due to misclassification. 
Besides, with the growing importance of agile methodologies in software development, 
categorization is not something that can wait either. Operations teams need to be able to quickly 
respond when something needs fixing and not being able to categorize reports correctly, slows 
down this process. This inefficiency eventually results in diss atisfied customers, security 
vulnerabilities and decreased software quality among others. 
CategorizeIT operates in issue report management from large software development 
environments trend. CategoryIT aims at using machine learning methods, more specifica lly 
transformer-based models to address issues with manual issue classification. Three types of 
issues—Bug, Enhancement and Question —are automatically identified in issue content to 
facilitate focused assignment for resource management, response time minim ization and over 
lifecycle improvement. 
Through this project, we aim to bridge the gap between traditional manual categorization 
methods and modern automation technologies while addressing the critical challenge of handling 
large volumes of issue reports in a scalable and efficient manner. 
1.2 Research Problem Statement 
One of the problems typical for modern software development is control over a rapidly growing 
number of issue reports. These reports include multiple types, including bug, enhancement, and 
support, in the form of user questions. In the past, the typing and categorization besides allocation 
of such reports used to be done by personnel and this was time -consuming, tended to be 
erroneous and also time -consuming. When the number of issue reports being received or 
collected is high, it becomes a lot difficult to proceed with this manual and systematic workflow, 
thus becoming a strong constraint in the whole software de velopment process. When reports are 
wrongly or delayed categorized, important bugs stay unfixed, resources are directed to the wrong 
course and less important issues take priority over more serious ones. 
However, it is worth pointing out that, despite using issue tracking systems, most of these systems 
are still mostly handled in a manual manner, in that, developers and the quality assurance teams 
will have to sift through the numerous reports. This process is however time consuming and also 
involves human error and therefore slows the process of solving software problems. Further, the 
fully manual approach to report categorization hampers the evaluation of the repetitiveness of 
issues and the optimizing of resources on tasks with the highest degrees of urge ncy. 
The challenge that Categorize IT aims to solve is thus the absence of an effective, precise 
categorization solution to assist in the classification of issue reports in extensive software 
development scenarios. The research aims to fill the gap by deve loping a machine learning-based 
solution that automates the classification of issue reports into three main categories: It may be 
classified as Bug, where an application or feature doesn’t work as it was intended; Enhancement,

where a user seeks for an improvement to a feature that is already there and Question, a problem 
which a user poses concerning the application. As such, CategorizeIT will help to minimize the 
number of manual efforts exerted by developers and quality assurance teams, and will increas e 
the efficiency of the report categorizing process while making sure that important issues are dealt 
with in the shortest possible amount of time.

The concerns analysed in the review relate to prior ten yea rs of research around issue report

2.1.1 Research Item #1: 
ClassifAI – Automating Issue Reports Classification Using Pre -Trained BERT 
Language Models 
2.1.1.1 Summary 
In ClassifAI, we propose a transformer -based approach utilizing the RoBERTa -Large model for 
categorizing issue reports to Bug, Enhancement, and Question. For the given model, the training 
and fine -tuning was performed on a large number of issue reports retrieved from open -source 
projects, and the final F1 -score of the model turned out to be 83.2% of the overall evaluated 
datasets. The study also shows the efficiency of using deep learning to work with text classification 
in software engineering 
2.1.1.2 Critical Analysis 
Strengths of the paper: 
● The paper utilizes a state -of-the-art BERT -based model (RoBERTa -Large) that excels in 
capturing contextual meanings in t extual data, leading to high classification accuracy. 
● Achieves an F1 -score of 83.2%, demonstrating strong performance in categorizing 
software issues. 
● The model is fine -tuned on multiple datasets from open -source projects, ensuring that 
the findings are generalizable and relevant to real -world applications. 
Weaknesses of the paper: 
6 
 
● The model requires a large amount of labeled data for effective training, which may limit 
its applicability in smaller projects or less -documented environments. 
● The study does no t explore domain -specific versions of BERT, which could potentially 
improve accuracy in specific fields or programming languages. 
● No discussion is provided regarding the computational cost or speed of the model, which 
may be an issue for large -scale real-time classification systems. 
2.1.1.3 Relationship to the Proposed Research 
ClassifiAI has acted as a reference point for the knowledge and approaches that will be obtained 
by the CategorizeIT project. CategorizeIT will also use transformer models, with reference to 
RoBERTa -Large but the model will be optimally designed for use with lesser data and with better 
time computational characteristics as well.

reports and discovered Random Forest as the most efficient, with an F1 -score greater than other 
models benchmarked. 
2.1.2.2 Critical Analysis 
Strengths of the paper: 
● The study provides a comprehensive comparison of several well -established machine 
learning models, offering valuable insights into their performance on real -world data. 
● Random Forest emerged as the most effective model, which demonstrates the potential 
of traditional machine learning techniques for binary classification tasks. 
● The study utilizes a real -world dataset, ensuring that the findings are applicable to 
practical software development environments.

deployed in real-world systems like GitHub. 
 
 
Weaknesses of the paper:

Chapter 3 
Proposed Approach 
This chapter gives the framework to solve the research problem that was postulated in 
CategorizeIT as a means to solve classification of software issue reports issues by applying 
intelligent techniques. The proposed solution is based on a transformer -based deep learning 
framework that will classify issue reports into three primary categories: There are three main 
classifications for a bug which are Bug, Enhancement, and Question. This chapter presents a 
briefing on the research methodology with regard to the steps, tools and the models that were 
used to accomplish the goals of the study. 
3.1 Framework Overview 
CategorizeIT system will function in the form of an issue report processor and classifier based on 
a pre -trained transformer model which may include BERT. We start with issue report 
preprocessing, where the reports are cleaned and tokenized followe d by training a transformer 
model on a labeled data set. The system then categorizes the issue reports according to their 
contents to the available categories as displayed below. 
The key components of the proposed approach are: 
● Data Collection and Preprocessing: The details of inputs are issues reported in the 
software repositories which are firstly preprocessed by deleting the noise, word 
tokenizing and normalizing the data. This helps to make the data to be in a correct format 
required by the model. 
● Model Selection and Training: The main of CategorizeIT is based on a transformer model 
like BERT or similar. The basic model is adapted with an aim to enhance its ability to 
correctly classify text into categories of Bug, Enhancement, and Questions by t raining it 
with a pre-labeled dataset of issue reports. 
● Classification: When trained, this model can take preprocessed issue reports and 
categorize it into any of the three solutions. This output in turn is preserved in a structure 
that could be easily fed to an issue management system. 
● Performance Evaluation: As for the test and assessment of the model’s performance, 
accuracy, precision, recall, and F1 -score are well -known benchmarks. Furthermore, the 
practical applicability of the model under conditions o f real-time classifications will be 
evaluated. 
3.2 Methodology 
The proposed methodology consists of the following steps: 
3.2.1 Data Preprocessing 
● Tokenization: The text in issue reports is tokenized into smaller units (tokens), which the 
model can easily process. 
12 
 
● Text Cleaning: Noise such as special characters, stopwords, and unnecessary whitespaces 
is removed. 
● Lowercasing: All text is converted to lowercase to standardize the input. 
● Lemmatization: The words in the text are reduced to their base or root forms to prevent 
variation in word forms from affecting classification. 
3.2.2 Model Training 
● Fine-Tuning BERT: In this study, the pre-trained BERT is adapted to the scope of problem 
reports. In this way, the transformer model included contextual connection between 
words in the reports, and learned how to categorize them into the given classes. 
● Cross-Validation: To enhance the model’s generalizability it will be cr oss validated thus 
improving on a situation where the model provides pessimistic estimates of the 
prediction accuracy by over -fitting to the training observations. 
3.2.3 Classification and Prediction 
● Issue Classification: At the end of the training process the model will be able to predict if 
the new issue report is a Bug, Enhancement, or Question. 
● Real-Time Processing: Practicality of the system also means that new issue reports can 
be quickly consumed and classified in real -time. 
3.2.3 Evaluation Metrics 
● Accuracy: Measures the overall correctness of the classification. 
● Precision: Evaluates how many of the classified reports were correctly identified. 
● Recall: Determines how well the model identifies all relevant reports. 
● F1-Score: Combines precision and rec all into a single metric for a balanced evalua tion of 
the model's performance.

3.3 Conclusion 
CategorizeIT is being envisioned as an advanced solution, which shall act as an AI -driven solution 
to facilitate automatic classification of issue reports using m achine learning algorithms such as 
RoBERTa and BERT. Once developed, it will be also convenient to distinguish software issues as 
bugs, enhancements, and questions with relative high precision which will decrease manual work 
with these reports. 
There will also be flexibility inherent in CategorizeIT to deal with new projects where there could 
still be minimal labeled data. Using SETFIT and Sentence-BERT, the system will work well with little 
training due to few -shot learning, making it feasible and versati le for large numbers of software 
repositories. 
As it advances, CategorizeIT could extend and solve other type of issue reports, with many labels 
or labels not well defined. Thus, in its work, the system will try to achieve high performance, 
although it wi ll combine distal models such as DistilBERT with classic approaches. In addition, 
dynamic transformers may be useful for dealing with large report loads in real -time as it may be 
for projects that have an active set of users and frequent refreshes.
