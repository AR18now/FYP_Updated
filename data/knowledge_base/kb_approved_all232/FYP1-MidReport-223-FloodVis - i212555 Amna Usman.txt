1. Introduction
1.2 Scope
The scope of this project includes the development and enhancement of flood monitoring
and detection through a flood disaster management system that integrates deep learning
with SAR satellite and UA V imagery. The system will perform critical functions such
as flood detection, mapping affected areas, and detecting humans and animals in flood
zones. To achieve this, FloodVis will acquire, analyze, and interpret satellite images and
UA V aerial images. Flood exposure zones will be mapped using semantic segmentation
techniques.
The system will use custom object detection models to recognize and count affected in-
dividuals, livestock, and damaged buildings. It will provide approximate coordinates for
rescue teams, enabling faster and more accurate deployment of resources. FloodVis will
consist of a backend system that focuses on data storing, model deployment and APIs,
while the front-end will contain a dashboard with information and reports on the effects
of floods. The models will also be deployed on the cloud, allowing scalability especially
during large scale disasters.
Project boundaries include: detecting floods and providing data analytics (exclude han-
dling ground-based rescue operations or non-flood-related disasters). This solution will
focus solely on improving response times and resource allocation during large-scale floods
for disaster relief teams, making it a critical tool for disaster management in vulnerable
regions.
1.3 Modules
1.3.1 Module 1 (Data Collection + Preprocessing)
1. Acquire, clean, augment and generate mask labels for optical satellite imagery of
flooded regions (pre, during, post-flood) from Sentinel-1 and Sentinel-2 satellites through
Google Earth Engine and/or Sentinel Hub.
2. Acquire, clean, augment and label aerial imagery of floods having visible humans,
animals and infrastructure from the internet and/or a simulated environment.

1.4 User Classes and Characteristics
User class Description
Emergency
Responder
Emergency responders are individuals who partake in, as well as coordinate,
rescue and relief efforts during flood events. Emergency responders will use
FloodVis to acquire the count and locations of humans and animals stranded
in the flood. They also need to stay up-to-date on the flood’s progression.
All this information can be obtained through the web application before the
responders head out into the field. They are individuals involved on-field
in more hands-on activities such as evacuation and providing aid. They are
collaborative and resourceful individuals who can assess the severity of a
situation and take quick actions.
Disaster
Manager
Disaster managers are people who make the key decisions regarding re-
source allocation during and after the flood. They also keep a check on the
general state of the flood response. Disaster managers may also use the web
application in order to prepare for a flood event. They are individuals with
strong analytical skills. They are fast decision-makers who ensure effective
communication.
System Ad-
ministrator
A system administrator is responsible for maintaining the FloodVis appli-
cation, ensuring it runs smoothly and securely. They ensure that users are
properly authenticated, that the system remains fully operational, that data
integrity is preserved, and that security breaches are prevented. Addition-
ally, they implement regular patches and updates to keep the application up-
to-date and secure. System administrators are IT experts who have a strong
grasp on computer science concepts such as Information Security, Computer
Networks, and Database Systems. They can quickly diagnose and resolve
technical issues in the system.

2.2 Functional Requirements
2.2 Functional Requirements
1. The system should classify regions as flooded or non-flooded.
2. The system should detect and count the number of critically affected and non-
critically non-affected humans.
3. The system should detect and count the number of critically affected and non-
critically affected cattle.
4. The system should detect number of submerged infrastructure.
5. The system should provide quantitative data via a dashboard, including flood area
size and severity of the flood.
6. The system should allow users to export reports containing flood analysis and de-
tection summaries.
7. The system should allow the comparison of pre- and post-flood images to assess the
extent of damage and the speed of water receding.
2.3 Non-Functional Requirements
1. The system should be able to process images with varying levels of noise.
2. The system should support medium-resolution satellite and UA V images for flood
assessment.
3. The system should be able to process satellite and UA V images captured from dif-
ferent angles for better flood mapping.
4. The system must provide accurate flood segmentation and detection of cattle, hu-
mans, and affected infrastructure.
5. The system should process large amounts of data (satellite and UA V images) quickly
to provide insights during emergencies.
6. The interface should be user-friendly, enabling decision-makers and rescue teams
to quickly understand the information presented.
7. The system should ensure the confidentiality and integrity of satellite and UA V data,
as well as the processed results, especially in sensitive disaster scenarios.
