1.2 Research Problem Statement
People with hearing impairments face a daily struggle to communicate with the general
public, they face substantial hurdles on a day to day basis because of the communica-
tion gap and the inability to hear, as well as not being able to understand what the other
person might be saying. While many hard-of-hearing people rely on lipreading and the
movement of lips, this alone does not suffice or encompass all possible scenarios either,
such as, rapid speech and complex language. Still the current speech recognition sys-
tems, for the majority part, rely on the audio inputs, discarding the visual inputs like,
lip movements, facial features and other nonverbal cues that make a sentence whole and
contextfull. These models and systems do not perform up to the mark when factors like
background noise, poor audio quality and audio-corruption are involved. Due to this,
hearing impaired people can’t not fully rely on speech to text models to communicate as
the text generated by them is not very robust or reliable.
Our focus is to combine the two main aspects that are, audio input and visual input. Au-
dio input includes the speech itself and visual input includes the lip movement of the
speaker. The approach we will be taking to achieve this is using separate models for au-
dio and visual inputs and then combining the two models using a form of Model Fusion.
This would consist of combining the Audio-Speech-Recognition model’s output with the
Visual-Speech-Recognition model’s output to produce a more robust text representation
of speech.
Our software and research aims to find an approach to generate robust, reliable and ac-
curate captions by utilizing both audio and visual inputs so that the audibly impaired can
easily communicate with anyone without fear of miscommunication. Our software will
address the shortcomings of other speech recognition and caption generation software by
catering to the problems that cause other software to give erroneous results. ImpairAs-
sist will cater to problems such as background noise affecting speech recognition, audio
corruption, video blurring and other problems. After addressing the above issues Im-
pairAssist will generate captions in real time.

Visual-Landmark Transformers (2023)
2.1.1.1 Author:
Feng Xue, Yu Li, Deyin Liu, Yincen Xie, Lin Wu, Richang Hong
2.1.1.2 Summary:
This paper introduces a state of the art multi-modal approach to Visual Speech Recogni-
tion called LipFormer. The approach focuses on using the changes of visual landmarks to
improve lip reading performance on unseen speakers. Lipreading models perform really
well when interpreting overlap speakers,but on unseen speakers, the models fail to pro-
duce accurate results due to the limited number of speakers in datasets. LipFormer seeks
to improve generalization across a range of speakers by utilizing a two-stream architec-
ture that combines visual features from both lip motion and facial landmarks. By effi-
ciently aligning and fusing these modalities, the framework’s distinct self-attention and
cross-attention mechanisms enable it to outperform conventional lipreading techniques
on benchmark datasets. So this paper proposes a solution to above described problem by
making use of visual landmarks

3.1 Proposed Solution/Method
3.1.1 Overall Approach:
Our proposed solution consists of multi model approach that will utilize both audio and
visual aspects of speech. Our approach will receive input in the form a video and then
extract the visual and audio aspects of speech. The visual aspect that is the movement
of the lips will be fed to the visual speech recognition module, the audio aspect will be
fed to the audio speech recognition module. Then the results of the audio and visual
modules will be passed to across attention module that will combine the audio and visual
speech recognition results and generate text caption that are more robust than other speech
recognition approaches that utilize a single type of input for recognizing speech. The cross
attention module will use either model fusion, a biderectional transformer or a multi-
model archtecture to combine the models and their results.
3.1.2 Input and Segmentation:
The pipeline will receive input in the form of a video either in real-time or recorded
along. The first step would be isolating the visual component from the audio component,
basically separating the audio signals in the video and the actual frames in the video. A
simple script will be used to extract the audio and and frames from a videos and save them
separately so that then can be utilized in the next step of the pipeline.
17
3. Proposed Approach
3.1.3 Preprocessing:
After obtaining the audio and video frames separately we need to perform preprocessing
on the input so that the data contains less noise and it is easier to process it and perform
speech recognition using this input. The frames will be denoised and sharpened using
tools such as openCV and similarly the audio input will be processed to remove noise
present in the audio using tools such as librosa.
3.1.4 Deep Learning:
After the two types of inputs have been preprocessed cleaned and noise has been removed
from them, they will pass to the next step in the pipeline which is the deep learning
model.The audio input will be fed to a audio-speech-recognition model and the video
will be fed to a visual-speech-recognition model, both models will use some form of self-
attention and cross-attention and other deep learning techniques to produce caption or text
representation of the speaker’s speech in the video. These outputs from the two models
will be fed to module which is the component responsible for combining the results of
audio-speech-recognition and visual-speech-recognition.
3.1.5 Model Combining:
After the visual-speech-recognition model and audio-speech-recognition models have
given their respective outputs we need to combine then and get a average result of both
the models. This is where we need to combine the two models or carry out model fusion,
this will be done by either using a model averaging technique or using cross-model fu-
sion techniques or approaches such as BERT. After the two outputs have been combined,
we can move to the last step or section of the pipeline which is post-processing and final
output.
3.1.6 Post-processing and Output:
After the model has given it’s output the final step would be post-processing it to remove
small grammatical errors in-order to increase the intelligibility of the text out and after
post-processing the generated text representation of the speech will be added as captions
in the original video that was given to the pipeline as an input.

substituted, inserted, or deleted in the recognized output. Similar to WER but at character
level.
