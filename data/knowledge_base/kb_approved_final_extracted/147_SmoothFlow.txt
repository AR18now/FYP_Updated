SOFTWARE REQUIREMENTS SPECIFICATION (IEEE 830)
Project: SmoothFlow - Speech Therapy Assistant for People Who Stutter
================================================================================

1. INTRODUCTION
1.1 Purpose
This document outlines the requirements for SmoothFlow, a web-based application that assists English-speaking individuals with speech disfluencies (stuttering) through AI-powered detection, classification, and gamified speech therapy exercises, connecting them with Speech Language Pathologists (SLPs) as needed.

1.2 Scope
The system provides detection and multi-label classification of speech disfluencies (blocks, prolongations, repetitions). It offers four gamified speech therapy exercises (Reading Passage, Matching Syllables, Sound Mimic, Breathing Control). Features include establishing a baseline, progress tracking with feedback reports (PDF export), SLP consultation (text/video), and an information center (chatbot) about stuttering.

1.3 Definitions, Acronyms, Abbreviations
- SLP: Speech Language Pathologist
- AI: Artificial Intelligence
- LLM: Large Language Model
- BPM: Beats Per Minute
- XP: Experience Points

2. OVERALL DESCRIPTION
2.1 Product Perspective
SmoothFlow provides a robust digital solution for individuals who stutter, offering science-backed exercises, AI analysis, and professional connection to improve speech fluency in a structured, motivating (gamified) environment.

2.2 Product Functions (Summary)
- Detection and Multi-label Classification: Detect and classify speech disfluencies in patient audio.
- Speech Therapy Exercises: Reading Passage, Matching Syllables, Sound Mimic, Breathing Control.
- Baseline of the Patient: Create profile, establish normal rate of speech.
- Progress Tracking and Feedback Reports: Provide feedback after exercises, view history, export reports (PDF).
- SLP Consultation: Connect with SLPs via text/video, view patient reports.
- Information Centre: Chatbot to answer questions about speech disfluencies and therapy.

2.3 User Classes and Characteristics
- Person Who Stutters (PWS): Primary end-user who uses the app to improve speech fluency.
- Speech Language Pathologist (SLP): Provides guidance and support to patients via consultation and monitoring.

3. FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS
3.1 Detection and Multi-label Classification of Patient's Speech
FR1.0.0: The system shall detect the presence of speech disfluency in the recorded audio of the patient.
FR1.1.0: The system shall recommend relevant speech therapy exercises based on the classification of the patient's speech.

3.2 Speech Therapy Exercises
FR2.0.0: Reading Passage Exercise: A passage shall be generated by LLM. The patient shall read it while their speech is recorded and transcribed.
FR2.0.1: Custom Passage Generation via LLM: The system generates a custom passage based on the patient's selected difficulty.
FR2.1.0: Matching Syllables Exercise: The patient selects a challenging word and practices pronouncing it with spaced-out syllables using a metronome.
FR2.1.1: BPM Syllable Practice: The patient shall be able to adjust the BPM of the metronome (options: 64, 92, 118, 144).
FR2.2.0: Sound Mimic: The patient listens to a pre-recorded sentence and then repeats it; the system records and transcribes their speech.
FR2.3.0: Breathing Control: The system displays a breathing animation; the patient follows it, then reads a sentence for recording and transcription.

3.3 Baseline of the Patient
FR3.0.0: The system shall provide a set of passages for the patient to read (starting high complexity, becoming simpler) to establish baseline.
FR3.0.1: Once the system detects no more speech disfluencies, it shall calculate the patient's normal rate of speech and store it.

3.4 Progress Tracking and Feedback Reports
FR4.0.0: After every speech therapy exercise, an analysis shall be conducted on the patient's speech to check disfluency severity.
FR4.1.0: Before every speech therapy exercise, an analysis shall be conducted to measure current state (read a passage).
FR4.2.0: Based on pre and post analysis, the system shall generate a feedback report (performance, difficulty, improvement, time, badges, XP).
FR4.3.0: Analysis on the patient's speech after completing the reading passage exercise (compare transcription with original passage).
FR4.4.0: Analysis on the patient's speech after completing the matching syllable exercise (compare pronounced syllables with actual word).
FR4.5.0: Analysis on the patient's speech after completing the sound mimic exercise (compare transcription with original).
FR4.6.0: Analysis on the patient's speech after completing the breathing exercise (provide reading passage, transcribe, analyze).

3.5 SLP Consultation
FR5.0.0: The patient shall be able to contact an SLP registered on the system through text message.

3.6 Information Centre
FR6.0.0: The patient can use the chatbot to ask questions about speech therapy and speech disfluencies; it will provide answers related only to these topics.

4. NON-FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS
4.1 Reliability
NFR1: The system shall have a 99 percent uptime to ensure availability.
NFR2: The system shall have backups to make sure data is not lost in case of failure.

4.2 Usability
NFR3: The system shall provide a clear and intuitive user interface for easy navigation.
NFR4: The system shall display instructions before each exercise.

4.3 Performance
NFR5: The system shall have a response time of less than 4 seconds.
NFR6: The system shall process and analyze speech audio within 10 seconds.
NFR7: The system shall be able to support up to 500 users.

4.4 Security
NFR8: The system shall use encryption while storing critical information in the database.
NFR9: The system shall have user authentication and authorization.
NFR10: The system shall have input validation.
NFR11: The system shall have role-based access control for administration.
