Chapter 1
Introduction
An AI system that can be deployed as a Software as a Service (SAAS) product on an
already in-use CCTV camera network to provide the users with smart notifications along
with an AI-enabled caller to contact emergency services or contacts on their behalf taking
a step towards cost-efficient security. [11].
1.1 Problem Statement
Traditional CCTV systems provide only basic surveillance and lack advanced features
such as intrusion detection. Upgrading to smart camera networks requires costly replace-
ments, which is often not feasible for many users. This creates a gap in the market for
affordable and scalable solutions that can enhance existing CCTV infrastructure without
the need for full system overhauls.
1.2 Scope
This project aims to develop an AI-driven intruder detection system "Sentinel AI" that
enhances existing CCTV infrastructure without costly hardware upgrades. Integrating
advanced facial recognition capabilities to identify individuals from various angles and
with different hairstyles ensures flexibility in real-world conditions, including a liveness
detection model to prevent spoofing attempts using photos or videos. The project aims
to categorize individuals into two groups: household members, who can move freely at
any time and frequent visitors, who will trigger notifications if they arrive at unusual
times. Real-time alerts will be generated for unfamiliar individuals or security breaches,
immediately notifying the owner or even contacting emergency services through an AI-
driven response system. Additionally, the solution will recognize faces even if partially

2.2 Functional Requirements
The functional requirements describe what the system will do, grouped into three main
modules: Face Recognition, AI Caller, and Web/App Development.
2.2.1 Face Recognition Module
1. The system shall detect faces from various angles, including front, left, and right
views.
2. The system shall perform facial recognition even if the individual has changed their
hairstyle or is partially covering their face (e.g., with a mask/muffler).
3. The system shall include liveliness detection to ensure that the face is real and not
a photo.
4. The system shall allow the owner to add known personnel’s faces to the database
with the appropriate permissions.
5. The system shall trigger an intruder alert for unfamiliar faces trying to enter the
facility and log the event for further review.
2.2.2 AI Caller Module
1. The system shall automatically notify the owner via notifications when an intruder
is detected.
2. The system shall automatically contact emergency services or designated contacts
in case of a confirmed intruder or emergency situation.
2.2.3 Web/App Development Module
1. The system shall provide a client web app for the owner to monitor real-time video
feeds, and review past events.
2. The system shall provide a client mobile app with similar functionality, enabling
notifications and alerts on-the-go.

2.3 Non-Functional Requirements
This section specifies non-functional requirements for the Sentinel AI system. These
quality requirements are specific, quantitative, and verifiable.
2.3.1 Reliability
The Sentinel AI system shall maintain a high level of reliability, with a Mean Time Be-
tween Failures (MTBF) of no less than 1000 hours. A failure is defined as any event that
prevents the system from accurately detecting and notifying about intruders.
2.3.2 Usability
Usability requirements focus on providing an intuitive and efficient user experience. The
system shall allow users to:
• Retrieve previous incident alerts with a single interaction.
• Access facial recognition settings and notifications without extensive training.
2.3.3 Performance
The Sentinel AI system shall meet the following performance requirements:
• 95% intruder alerts shall be processed within 2 seconds of detection.
• The system shall support a minimum of 100 concurrent users without degradation
in performance.
2.3.4 Security
The system shall implement robust security measures to protect user data and system
integrity. The security requirements include:
• The effort required to compromise the system shall be sufficiently high to deter
unauthorized access.
• The system shall regularly audit user access and alert administrators of suspicious
activity.

Chapter 3
System Overview
Sentinel AI is an AI-driven intruder detection system designed to enhance existing CCTV
infrastructure without the need for expensive hardware upgrades. The system integrates
facial recognition, liveness detection, and categorizes individuals into known and un-
known categories to trigger real-time alerts in case of security breaches or intrusions.
It can also notify emergency services and the property owner via AI-driven response sys-
tems. Sentinel AI is able to recognize faces even with masks, mufflers, or hats, and offers
an efficient and scalable solution for residential and commercial security.
3.1 Architectural Design
The system architecture is modular, consisting of multiple subsystems that work together
to achieve the complete functionality of Sentinel AI. Each subsystem has clearly defined
responsibilities, and the system is decomposed into the following core modules:
• Face Detection Module: Responsible for capturing live video feeds and detecting
faces in real time from multiple angles (front, left, right).
• Liveness Detection Module: Verifies whether the detected faces are real or fake,
ensuring that spoofing attempts are caught.
• Face Recognition Module: Compares the detected face embeddings with known
faces and identifies individuals based on stored embeddings.
• Intrusion Detection Module: Monitors sensitive areas within the camera feed for
any unauthorized access or intrusions.
• AI Caller & Notifier Module: Alerts the property owner and emergency contacts
by making automated calls and sending notifications in case of intrusion detection.
9
The modules interact as follows: The Face Detection Module continuously captures the
camera feed and detects faces, passing them to the Liveness Detection Module. If a live
face is detected, it is then sent to theFace Recognition Modulefor identification. If an un-
recognized or unauthorized person is detected in a sensitive area, the Intrusion Detection
Module triggers the AI Caller & Notifier Module to alert the relevant personnel.
The architectural style follows a layered approach, where each module operates inde-
pendently but collaborates via well-defined interfaces. This modular approach allows
flexibility and easy maintenance.

3.3 Data Design
Sentinel AI leverages a structured approach to handle and process data. The system stores
data in two main components:
• Face Embeddings Database: This database stores the facial embeddings of known
individuals, which are used for face recognition. The embeddings are extracted
using deep learning techniques and stored as numerical vectors.
11
• Event Logs and Intrusion Records : Stores all detected intrusions, their times-
tamps, and details regarding the individuals involved. This is used for system audits
and to provide a historical overview of intrusions.
The system uses MongoDB to store structured data (face embeddings, detected faces, and
logs) in a highly scalable manner. The database is optimized for fast retrieval of face
embeddings during the recognition process.
Additionally, emergency contact information is stored securely to ensure timely notifica-
tion in case of security breaches.
