1. INTRODUCTION
Purpose: To define the system requirements for a food delivery application that allows customers to browse restaurants, place customized orders, schedule deliveries, and track rider progress; enables restaurants to manage incoming orders, prepare them, and communicate with drivers; and provides real-time updates and management tools for all parties involved.
Scope: This document covers the requirements for the food delivery application from both customer and restaurant perspectives, including functionalities related to placing orders, managing orders, and tracking deliveries.
Definitions/Acronyms:
- API: Application Programming Interface
- DBMS: Database Management System
- GUI: Graphical User Interface
References: IEEE Std 830-1998, ISO/IEC 25010:2011
Overview: The food delivery application aims to streamline the process of ordering food by providing a seamless experience for customers, efficient order handling for restaurants, and easy navigation for drivers.
2. OVERALL DESCRIPTION
Product Perspective: The product is a web-based and mobile application designed to facilitate food delivery services between customers and restaurants through a network of drivers.
Product Functions:
- Customer functions: Browsing restaurants, customizing orders, scheduling deliveries, and tracking rider progress.
- Restaurant functions: Receiving orders, confirming preparation times, marking orders as ready, and pausing orders during rush hours or outages.
- Driver functions: Accepting delivery tasks, navigating optimized routes, reporting incidents, and receiving real-time updates.
User Characteristics: The primary users are food consumers who want convenient access to various dining options and professional chefs who wish to efficiently manage their business operations.
Constraints: The system must operate within legal boundaries regarding data privacy and payment processing. It also needs to ensure compatibility across different devices and operating systems.
Assumptions/Dependencies: The system relies on third-party APIs for geolocation services and payment gateways. It assumes continuous internet connectivity for all users.
3. SPECIFIC REQUIREMENTS
External Interface Requirements:
- User interface: A responsive design supporting desktop browsers and mobile applications.
- Hardware interfaces: Compatible with standard smartphones and tablets.
- Software interfaces: Integration with popular payment gateways and mapping services via APIs.
- Communication interfaces: Real-time messaging capabilities for customer support and internal communications.
Functional Requirements:
FR-1: Browse Restaurants
Input: None
Processing: Display a list of registered restaurants along with their menus and ratings.
Output: List of restaurants with menu items and ratings.
Priority: High
FR-2: Customize Orders
Input: Selection of menu items and customization options.
Processing: Update order details based on selected items and preferences.
Output: Updated order summary showing total cost and customization choices.
Priority: Medium
FR-3: Schedule Deliveries
Input: Delivery date and time selection.
Processing: Confirm availability and add order to delivery queue.
Output: Confirmation message indicating successful booking.
Priority: Low
FR-4: Track Rider Progress
Input: Order ID.
Processing: Retrieve current location and estimated arrival time of assigned driver.
Output: Live map displaying rider's position and eta.
Priority: High
FR-5: Receive Notifications
Input: Status changes in the order lifecycle.
Processing: Send push notifications to relevant stakeholders.
Output: Notification messages to customers, restaurants, and drivers about order status updates.
Priority: Medium
Non-functional Requirements:
Performance: The system shall respond to user actions within 2 seconds under normal load conditions.
Security: Implement encryption for sensitive data transmission and storage, comply with PCI-DSS standards for payment transactions.
Reliability: Ensure 99.9% uptime with automatic failover mechanisms for critical components.
Usability: Design intuitive UI elements and provide comprehensive help documentation.
Maintainability: Codebase shall follow industry best practices for ease of maintenance and future enhancements.
Portability: Support deployment on multiple platforms including cloud services and local servers.
