IntroductionThe purpose of this mid evaluation report is to define the scope, systemlevel requirements, andfirstiteration deliverables for our VRbasedapplicationnamed“FearLESS”. FearLESS helps peopleovercometheirfearsofheights(Acrophobia)anddarkness(Nyctophobia)usingVR, gaugesaperson’sfearintensity,and connects its users to therapists/psychiatrists for counseling. This document is intended for theFearLESS team, our supervisor Mr. Bilal Khalid Dar, our co-supervisor Mr. Wasif Ali Wasif, andtheFYPpanel.
ProblemStatementFear is a natural survival response to danger that becomes irrational, dysfunctional, and even phobicwhen the level of danger is insignificant compared to the reaction it caused [1]. Such irrational fearsand/or phobias vary in intensity amongst individuals. They oftendisturbthesuffering individual’s dailylife, making simple daily activities and pleasures difficult to achieve, as they go out of their way toovercometheirfear.
For instance, anirrational fear of heightswouldinvolvethepersonhavingpanicattacksbystandingonaladder or walking up a flight of stairs. A person’s fear of darkness may cause them to miss sleep,depression, and they limit their life to the daytime since leaving the house after dark becomes anextremely stressful and difficult task. Avoidance strategies do help the person avoid their fears andtriggers, but it canalsolimit what a personcandointheirlife, andmayevenbecomedangerousfortheperson. An irrational fear of clowns may cause the person to miss their family’s birthday parties andcelebrations. Likewise, a person can miss medical appointments, and life saving drugs due to theirintensefearofneedles.
According totheNational Comorbidity Survey, among theadults withphobias intheUnitedStates, “anestimated 21.9% had high fear intensity, 30.0% had moderate impairment, and 48.1% had mildimpairment” [3]. Treatment for such fears, and phobias include exposure therapy andcognitive-behavioral therapy (CBT), however, only about23%ofthepeoplewithphobiasseektreatment[2]. Taking therapyorevencheckingtheintensityofone’sfearrequiresfundsandaccesstoprofessionalswhich most people end up avoiding, either because of their geographic location, or social stigma thatcomes with seeking help frommental health professionals. Moreover, it is a challenge tocontrol andreplicatetheexposuretherapytreatmentinasafeenvironment.
