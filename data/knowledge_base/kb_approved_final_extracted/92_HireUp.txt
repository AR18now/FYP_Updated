Project: HireUp - Automated Hiring Platform

1. INTRODUCTION
1.1 Purpose
This document outlines the requirements for HireUp, a web application designed to cut down the cost and time spent during hiring processes by automating CV filtration, video interview analysis, and test creation.

1.2 Scope
The system allows applicants to apply for jobs, search and receive recommendations, and schedule interviews/tests. Recruiters can post jobs and receive shortlisted candidates. It features CV filtration, video interview analysis, and knowledge-based tests.

1.3 Definitions, Acronyms, Abbreviations
- CV: Curriculum Vitae
- MCQ: Multiple Choice Question
- API: Application Programming Interface

2. OVERALL DESCRIPTION
2.1 Product Perspective
HireUp addresses the inefficiencies and biases in traditional hiring by automating the CV screening and initial assessment process, saving time and resources for companies.

2.2 Product Functions (Summary)
- CV Filtration using AI.
- Video Interview Analysis (facial expressions, answer accuracy).
- Knowledge-based Test creation and scoring.
- Job Posting & Management.
- Job Application Submission.
- Job Search & Recommendations.
- Interview & Test Scheduling.
- Application Status Notifications.
- Application Tracking.
- Profile Management.

2.3 User Classes and Characteristics
- Applicant: Seeks a job and applies through the application.
- Recruiter: Finds most eligible candidates for a job opening.

2.4 Operating Environment
Web application.

3. FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS
3.1 CV Filtration
FR1.1: The system shall provide a form to the applicant.
FR1.2: The system shall extract information such as skills, experience, etc., from the provided data.
FR1.3: The system shall verify experience through LinkedIn and GitHub.
FR1.4: The system shall score the CV based on the requirements of the job description.

3.2 Video Interview Analysis
FR2.1: The system shall provide an option to assess shortlisted applicants via an automated video interview.
FR2.2: The system shall take questions entered by the recruiter.
FR2.3: The system shall question the applicants.
FR2.4: The system shall record the interviews.
FR2.5: The system shall process and analyze emotions from facial expressions.
FR2.6: The system shall analyze the answers provided by the applicant.
FR2.7: The system shall score the applicant based on facial expressions and answer accuracy.

3.3 Test Creation
FR3.1: The system shall provide an option to recruiters to create an MCQ-based test.
FR3.2: The system shall score test answers based on the key given by recruiters.
FR3.3: The system shall show the test scores to recruiters.

3.4 Job Posting & Management
FR4.1: The system shall provide recruiters with an option to post a job opening.
FR4.2: The system shall show recruiters a history of all job openings they have posted.
FR4.3: The system shall allow recruiters to view the CVs, recorded interviews, or test scores of shortlisted candidates.

3.5 Job Application Submission
FR5.1: The system shall allow applicants to apply for a job vacancy.
FR5.2: The system shall allow applicants to upload CVs/resumes and other information.

3.6 Job Search & Recommendations
FR6.1: The system shall allow the applicant to search for jobs.
FR6.2: The system shall display jobs tailored to the applicant's specified field.
FR6.3: The system shall suggest jobs to applicants based on domain and location.

3.7 Interview & Test Scheduling
FR7.1: The system shall generate a schedule for applicants, listing all their interviews and tests with timings.

3.8 Application Status Notifications
FR8.1: The system shall notify applicants of any updates or changes in the status of their job applications.

3.9 Application Tracking
FR9.1: The system shall allow applicants to track the progress of jobs they have applied to.

3.10 Profile Management
FR10.1: The system shall provide access to recruiters or applicants through sign-up or login.
FR10.2: The system shall send a verification email after sign-up.
FR10.3: The system shall allow users to edit their profiles.
FR10.4: The system shall allow users to delete their profiles.

4. NON-FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS
4.1 Security
NFR1: Only authenticated users can access the main functionalities.
NFR2: User data will be encrypted using AES-256 encryption.

4.2 Usability
NFR3: Clear and informative error messages are provided.
NFR4: The UI will be user-friendly, following User-Centered Design principles.

4.3 Performance
NFR5: The application should respond to user actions within 2 seconds under normal load conditions.
