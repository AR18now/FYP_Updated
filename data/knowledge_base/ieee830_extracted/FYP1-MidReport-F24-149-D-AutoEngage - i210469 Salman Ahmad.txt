1. Introduction
1.1 Problem Statement
It becomes extremely difficult to handle and communicate with clients especially when
the firm is operating in the current era of globalization. The problems are typical for the
conventional call centers, including these situated in Pakistan; it is due to these reasons
that it is challenging for organizations of this type to deliver high quality services over
the prolong period. These include time, culture, language and human agents who may
tire, make a slip, or may just not understand what the situation requires, and this is very
frequent when dealing with foreign Clients. In addition, high expenses incurred in getting
and training human Agents also form another disadvantage that hinders the expansion of
these companies in a cheaper manner. In most circumstances, such problems result to the
erosion of profit, levels of satisfaction among Clients going down, and with interacting
markets such as the US, the rate of conversion coming down.
1.2 Scope
The AutoEngage project seeks to have a fully automated call center that will replace
human beings by using the phone to interact with the clients. This solution deploys Speech
to Text to transcribe real time client conversation, Large language models to mimic natural
language/ human responses to client conversation and Text to speech to translate these
responses back to speech.
It aims to solve crucial issues that outdated large call center faces including high oper-
ating cost, problems with management of overseas clients and problems with scalability.
AutoEngage automates these processes and hence provides all the servicewithout fail and
without the need to consult a time difference chart regarding the clients located in different
countries. One of the system’s advantages is the presence of an analytics panel that tracks
the progress and optimizes it. The first goal is to get more clients in an hour, increase the
percentage of correct orders and solve the problem of being too formal. AutoEngage will
be mainly used for keeping relationships with the customers and answering their product
inquiries using script-based sales conversations, the aim being to provide a voice as close
to human as possible in order to reassure customers and increase their confidence. Still,
it is worth mentioning that, certain enhancements including voice cloning, deepfake, and
multiple languages are not within the scope of this work.
In sum, AutoEngage targets to offer a proven and growing all-in-one software solution for
call centres that will improve clients’ satisfaction, optimise work, and contain expenses,
thus confirming the instrument’s effectiveness as a large-scale solving tool for call centre
activity..

3.1 General Description
The AutoEngage system is an AI-powered client engagement and sales automation plat-
form designed to streamline and enhance call center operations. The primary goal of
AutoEngage is to replace human agents with AI-driven interactions, enabling businesses
to handle a large volume of client interactions with minimal human intervention. This
automation not only reduces operational costs but also ensures scalability and consistent
performance across various client interactions.
The system leverages advanced technologies such as Speech-to-Text (STT) for transcrib-
ing real-time conversations, Large Language Models (LLMs) to generate human-like re-
sponses, and Text-to-Speech (TTS) to relay these responses back to clients. AutoEngage
is intended to improve client interactions by offering quick, accurate, and personalized
communication. Furthermore, it features an analytics module that tracks and evaluates
the performance of agents, calls, and overall system operations.
3.2 Context
In the modern call center environment, companies face several challenges such as high
operational costs, limitations of human agents, and the complexity of managing global
clients. Traditional call centers often struggle to meet these demands due to time zone
differences, human fatigue, and the cost of hiring and training agents.
AutoEngage addresses these challenges by automating interactions with AI-driven agents

that can handle a wide range of client inquiries, upsell opportunities, and service requests.
The system is designed to operate 24/7, allowing businesses to manage client interactions
across different time zones without additional staffing. Additionally, AutoEngage pro-

enabling call centers to optimize their operations continuously.
3.3 Design Overview
The design of the AutoEngage system is modular and scalable, ensuring that it can be
easily integrated into existing call center infrastructures. The system is composed of the
following key modules:
• Speech-to-Text (STT) Module : This component transcribes the client’s speech
into text, allowing the system to understand and process client inquiries.
• Intent Classification Module: Once the client’s speech is converted to text, this
module classifies the intent of the client, such as a request for information, a com-
plaint, or an interest in purchasing additional services.
• Large Language Model (LLM) Module : The LLM generates human-like re-
sponses based on the classified intent. It adapts the response according to predefined
sales scripts or the client’s inquiry.
• Text-to-Speech (TTS) Module: This module converts the generated text responses
back into speech, which is then delivered to the client, creating a seamless and
human-like interaction.
• Call Center Dashboard: The system includes a web-based dashboard that displays
real-time call center analytics, agent performance, and customer satisfaction met-
rics. The dashboard is accessible to managers and supervisors to monitor system
efficiency and adjust operations accordingly.
• Analytics Module: This module collects data on client interactions, agent perfor-
mance, call outcomes, and customer feedback. It generates reports that help the call
center to make data-driven decisions and improve overall efficiency.

3.4 System Workflow
3.4 System Workflow
The workflow of AutoEngage can be summarized as follows:
1. The system initiates a call to a client from the call center’s database.
2. When the client answers, their speech is transcribed into text by the STT module.
3. The Intent Classification Module analyzes the transcribed text and determines the
purpose of the call.
4. The LLM generates an appropriate response based on the client’s intent and prede-
fined scripts.
5. The response is converted back into speech by the TTS module and delivered to the
client.
6. The system tracks the client’s responses and decisions, offering upgrades or further
assistance as needed.
7. At the end of the call, the analytics data is updated, providing real-time insights into
the call’s outcome and the agent’s performance.

3.6 Architectural Design
In this section, we will develop a modular program structure for the AutoEngage sys-
tem based on the Client-Server Architecture. This structure ensures that each part of the
system focuses on a specific responsibility, while still allowing different components to
collaborate effectively to achieve the overall functionality.
3.6.1 Modular Program Structure
The AutoEngage system is composed of two main components: the Client Side and the
Server Side, each with its own set of subsystems. These components collaborate through
well-defined interfaces to ensure smooth and efficient operation of the system.
3.6.1.1 Client Side
The Client Side is responsible for managing user interactions and presenting data in a
user-friendly format. It contains two main modules:
• User Interface (Call Center Dashboard) : This module provides the front-end
interface for call center agents and managers to interact with the system. It displays
real-time analytics, agent performance data, and call status updates. It also allows
users to initiate or terminate calls.
• Client Request Handling : This module manages communication between the
client and server, sending user requests (e.g., starting a call or retrieving analytics)
to the server, and receiving the necessary responses (e.g., call outcomes or system
health information).
3.6.1.2 Server Side
The Server Side handles the core business logic, data processing, and data storage. It
consists of the following functional modules:
• Speech-to-Text (STT): This module transcribes client speech into text for further
processing.
• Intent Classification: This module analyzes the transcribed text to determine the
client’s intent (e.g., inquiry, complaint, or service request).
• Response Generation (LLM): This module generates human-like responses based
on predefined scripts and the client’s detected intent.

• Text-to-Speech (TTS): This module converts the generated text responses into
natural-sounding speech, allowing the system to maintain a conversational inter-
face with the client.
• Call Management: This module handles the entire lifecycle of a call, from initia-
tion to termination, tracking the duration and managing call states.
• Analytics: This module collects and analyzes data from completed and ongoing
calls, providing insights into agent performance, client satisfaction, and system ef-
ficiency.
• Data Storage: This module ensures persistent storage of call data, agent perfor-
mance metrics, and analytics. It interfaces with a database for secure and efficient
data management.
3.6.2 Relationships Between Modules
The interaction between the Client Side and Server Side is based on a request-response
model. The following high-level sequence describes the flow of data between the mod-
ules:
3.6.2.1 Client Side → Server Side
• The client sends a request (e.g., initiating a call) through the Client Request Han-
dling module to the server.
• During a call, the client’s speech is sent to theSpeech-to-Text module on the server,
which transcribes the speech for further processing.
3.6.2.2 Server Side → Client Side
• Once the Speech-to-Text module has transcribed the client’s speech, the text is
passed to the Intent Classification module, which identifies the intent behind the
client’s request.
• Based on the classified intent, the Response Generation (LLM) module generates
an appropriate response.
• The Text-to-Speech module converts the generated text response into speech and
sends it back to the client through the Client Request Handling module.

3.8.4 Data Flow and Processing
The system follows these general steps to process and store the data:
1. Client Call Initiation : The AI agent retrieves client information from the Client
Data Store and initiates a call.
2. Speech Processing: The client’s speech is converted to text by the Speech-to-Text

3. Intent Classification: The transcribed text is classified to understand the client’s
intent and logged in the Call Data Store.
4. Response Generation: Based on the classified intent, a prompt is generated, and
the LLM generates a response. The generated response is stored along with the
intent in the Call Data Store.
5. Analytics Logging: After the call, key performance metrics such as call duration,
client intent, and call outcome are logged in both the Call Data Store and the Ana-
lytics Data Store for future analysis.

system performance. The system employs multiple data stores to keep track of client
profiles, call histories, and performance metrics, ensuring that every interaction is logged
and stored for future analysis.
