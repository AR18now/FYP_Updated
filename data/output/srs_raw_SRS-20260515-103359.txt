INTRODUCTION
Purpose: To specify the system requirements for an Insurance Claim Processing Platform designed to streamline the submission, evaluation, and resolution of insurance claims.
Scope: This Software Requirements Specification (SRS) defines the functionality required for the Insurance Claim Processing Platform, including user interactions, system operations, and external interface specifications.
Definitions/Acronyms:
- ICPP: Insurance Claim Processing Platform
- Policy Holder: An individual or entity holding an insurance policy
- Claim Officer: A staff member responsible for evaluating and processing claims
- Supervisor: A manager overseeing multiple Claim Officers and managing workflow
- Deductible: The amount a policy holder is responsible for paying out-of-pocket before the insurer pays benefits
- Settlement Statement: A detailed financial report summarizing the claim outcome
Overview: The ICPP will enable efficient management of insurance claims through automated processes, ensuring accurate and timely handling while maintaining compliance with regulatory standards.

OVERALL DESCRIPTION
Perspective: The perspective of the ICPP is from the end-user's viewpoint, encompassing both internal stakeholders such as policy holders and claim officers, as well as external stakeholders such as supervisors and customers.

Functions:
- Submission of claims
- Verification and validation of policies
- Calculation of deductibles
- Workflow management and escalation
- Status notification to customers
- Review of workload and metrics by supervisors

Users:
- Policy Holders
- Claim Officers
- Supervisors
- Customers
Constraints:
- Compliance with relevant insurance regulations
- Integration with existing systems and databases
- Security measures to protect sensitive customer data
Assumptions/Dependencies:
- Availability of necessary backend services and databases
- Adequate network infrastructure support

SPECIFIC REQUIREMENTS
External Interfaces:
- Interface with policy database
- Interface with customer service portal
- Interface with payment gateway
Functional Requirements:
FR-01
- Claim Submission
Input: Incident details, supporting documents, bank information
Processing: Validation of policy, calculation of deductible, generation of unique claim ID
Output: Confirmation message and claim reference number
Priority: High
FR-02
- Policy Verification
Input: Claim reference number
Processing: Retrieval of policy details, verification against submitted information
Output: Verdict on policy validity and coverage limits
Priority: Medium
FR-03
- Deductible Computation
Input: Claim reference number
Processing: Determination of applicable deductible based on policy terms
Output: Deductible amount
Priority: Medium
FR-04
- Workload Management
Input: Pending claim IDs
Processing: Assignment of claims to available Claim Officers, tracking progress
Output: Updated workload dashboard
Priority: Low
FR-05
- Fraud Detection
Input: Claim reference number
Processing: Analysis of claim details for suspicious patterns
Output: Flagging potential fraudulent claims
Priority: Medium
FR-06
- Customer Notifications
Input: Claim status updates
Processing: Generation of notifications via email or SMS
Output: Delivery of notifications to customers
Priority: High
Non-Functional Requirements:
NFR-01
- Usability
The system shall provide clear and intuitive interfaces for all users, minimizing learning curves and errors.
NFR-02
- Reliability
The system shall ensure high uptime and minimal downtime, with regular backups and disaster recovery plans.
NFR-03
- Performance
The system shall process claims within specified timeframes, ensuring timely responses to policy holders and supervisors.
NFR-04
- Portability
The system shall support deployment across various operating environments and devices, ensuring compatibility and accessibility.
System Features:
- Automated claim processing
- Real-time status updates
- Secure transaction handling
- Comprehensive reporting capabilities
