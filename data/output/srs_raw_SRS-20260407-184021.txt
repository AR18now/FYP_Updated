INTRODUCTION
The purpose of this Software Requirements Specification (SRS) is to define the requirements for a Ticket Generating System that facilitates users in searching, comparing, reserving, and managing their travel itineraries. This document outlines the scope, definitions/acronyms, references, and provides an overview of the project.
Scope: The Ticket Generating System will provide functionalities related to itinerary searches, fare comparisons, seat reservations, insurance additions, and e-ticket generation. It will also manage voucher redemptions, fare changes, and trip reminders while ensuring secure storage of passenger profiles.
Definitions/Acronyms:
- TGS: Ticket Generating System
- ITINERARY: A planned journey consisting of one or more segments of transportation.
- FARE: The cost associated with a specific segment of a journey.
- VOUCHER: A certificate entitling the holder to payment or goods/services.
- E-TICKET: An electronic document representing a ticket for travel services.
References:
- IEEE Standard for Software Requirements Specifications, IEEE Std 830-1998
- Project Management Plan
Overview: The TGS aims to simplify the process of planning and booking travel itineraries through an intuitive interface. Users will have access to various features designed to enhance their experience, including fare comparisons, seat reservations, and trip management tools. OVERALL DESCRIPTION
Product Perspective: The product perspective includes defining the target audience, core functions, constraints, assumptions, and dependencies necessary for the successful implementation of the TGS.
Product Functions:
- Search for multi-city itineraries
- Compare fares between different itineraries
- Filter itineraries based on baggage and refund policies
- Reserve seats for passengers
- Add insurance coverage for trips
- Generate and send out e-tickets upon reservation completion
- Handle voucher redemptions at the time of purchase
- Manage fare changes and updates throughout the booking process
- Provide alternative options if inventory expires during checkout
- Offer trip reminders via email or SMS notifications
- Allow simple changes or cancellations to booked trips
- Store passenger profiles securely for future reference
User Characteristics: The primary users of the TGS are travelers who require assistance in planning and executing their journeys efficiently. These users may range from frequent flyers to occasional travelers seeking convenience and ease-of-use in managing their travel arrangements.
Constraints:
- Legal and regulatory compliance regarding privacy and data protection
- Performance limitations due to server capacity and network bandwidth
- Compatibility across diverse devices and operating systems
Assumptions/Dependencies:
- Availability of real-time flight information from airlines and other transport providers
- Secure integration with financial processing systems for transactions
- Reliable third-party services for sending reminder notifications SPECIFIC REQUIREMENTS
External Interface Requirements:
- User Interface: The application shall present a clean and user-friendly interface allowing easy navigation through all available features.
- Hardware Interface: Compatible with standard desktop computers and mobile devices running iOS and Android OS.
- Software Interface: shall integrate seamlessly with existing travel-related applications and websites.
- Communication Interface: Capable of exchanging data over internet protocols to fetch real-time flight details and communicate with backend servers.
Functional Requirements:
FR-1: Search Multi-City Itineraries
Input: Origin airport, destination airport, departure date, return date (optional), number of passengers.
Processing: Retrieve relevant flight schedules from database or external API sources.
Output: Display list of available itineraries along with detailed information about each route.
Priority: High
FR-2: Compare Fares Between Itineraries
Input: Selected itineraries from previous step.
Processing: Calculate total costs considering base fares, taxes, fees, and additional charges applicable to chosen routes.
Output: Present side-by-side comparison highlighting differences in overall expenses.
Priority: Medium
FR-3: Filter Itineraries Based on Baggage and Refund Policies
Input: Selected criteria such as maximum allowed luggage weight and flexibility of cancellation policy.
Processing: Apply filters to narrow down results matching specified preferences.
Output: Show filtered set of itineraries meeting given conditions.
Priority: Low
FR-4: Reserve Seats for Passengers
Input: Chosen itinerary, passenger details including name, contact info, and preferred seating preference.
Processing: Allocate appropriate seats within selected flights according to availability and passenger needs.
Output: Confirm seat allocation and update passenger profile accordingly.
Priority: High
FR-5: Add Insurance Coverage for Trips
Input: Confirmation request from user indicating interest in purchasing travel insurance.
Processing: Present options for different types of insurance plans alongside pricing details.
Output: Allow selection of desired plan and proceed with transactional processing.
Priority: Medium
FR-6: Generate and Send Out E-Tickets Upon Reservation Completion
Input: Successful reservation confirmation.
Processing: Create digital representation of tickets containing essential trip information.
Output: Deliver generated e-tickets directly to user's email address or mobile device.
Priority: High
FR-7: Handle Voucher Redemptions at Time of Purchase
Input: Valid promotional codes entered by user before finalizing payment.
Processing: Validate entered vouchers against active offers and apply corresponding discounts.
Output: Update total amount payable after applying valid promotions.
Priority: Medium
FR-8: Manage Fare Changes Throughout Booking Process
Input: Request from user to modify previously made reservation.
Processing: Assess feasibility of requested change taking into account current inventory status and operational rules.
Output: Inform user whether modification can be processed successfully and provide updated pricing details.
Priority: Medium
FR-9: Provide Alternative Options If Inventory Expires During Checkout
Input: Notification from system indicating insufficient remaining seats for original choice.
Processing: Propose substitute options that still meet user’s initial requirements but accommodate available slots.
Output: Guide user towards selecting new alternatives without disrupting ongoing booking procedure.
Priority: High
FR-10: Offer Trip Reminders Via Email Or SMS Notifications
Input: Subscription request from user opting to receive alerts about upcoming departures.
Processing: Schedule timely messages reminding recipients of their scheduled travels.
Output: Dispatch reminder communications either electronically or via cellular networks depending on subscriber preference.
Priority: Low
FR-11: Allow Simple Changes Or Cancellations To Booked Trips
Input: Change/Cancellation request submitted by registered traveler.
Processing: Evaluate validity of proposed alteration requests adhering strictly to company guidelines.
Output: Approve or reject changes based on evaluation outcome; notify affected parties promptly.
Priority: Medium
FR-12: Store Passenger Profiles Securely For Future Reference
Input: Information provided by users during registration or subsequent interactions.
Processing: Encrypt sensitive personal data stored locally or remotely following industry best practices.
Output: Maintain comprehensive records accessible solely by authorized personnel.
Priority: High
Non-functional Requirements:
Performance: The system shall ensure quick response times under normal load conditions and remain responsive even when handling peak traffic volumes.
Security: All transmitted data must be encrypted using robust cryptographic methods to protect against unauthorized access or interception.
Reliability: The application must function correctly despite potential failures in underlying components or external factors affecting service delivery.
Availability: Ensure high uptime rates minimizing downtime due to maintenance activities or unexpected technical issues.
Usability: Design the UI elements intuitively so that users can easily find what they need without requiring extensive training.
Maintainability: Codebase shall follow established coding standards facilitating easier modifications and enhancements moving forward.
Portability: Develop the solution in cross-platform compatible technologies enabling deployment across multiple environments effortlessly.
