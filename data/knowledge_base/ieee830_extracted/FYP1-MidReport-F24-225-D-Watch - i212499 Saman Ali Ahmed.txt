1.2 Scope
HaloSight is a real-time worker monitoring web application. It is a construction-place safety
and compliance system. As a starter, it uses object detection to detect Personal Protective
Equipment (PPE) violations to ensure worker safety.
HaloSight stands out from all other systems as it integrates the use of facial recognition to
analyze stress among the workers to ensure productivity and avoid any mishaps. This system
does not aim to be a general construction worker management system. Instead, it is focused on
using object detection and facial recognition to increase productivity and safety of the workers
on-site.

Chapter 3
System Overview
HaloSight is a holistic worker safety surveillance tool developed for construction sectors. The
first objective of the system is the real-time identification of safety protocols’ infringements,
including the incorrect application of the Personal Protective Equipment (PPE), and the second
one is the worker’s condition assessment by facial emotions and stress levels. As a result of us-
ing video analytics and artificial intelligence, HaloSight provides supervisors with notifications
in cases of non-adherence to PPE and signs of worker drowsiness.
The features of the system include; identification of PPE, real-time alerts, historical records,
and generation of reports to monitor the workers’ compliance and their emotional well-being.
Also, it organizes and encourages positive safety behavior through a reward and recognition
program. The system helps organizations to adhere to safety standards these include OSHA
thus offering businesses a means of improving on the safety and productivity of the workers.

3.3 Data Design
3.3 Data Design
3.3.1 Selecting Unstructured Databases
In our project, we are utilizing unstructured and semi-structured databases such as Mon-
goDB and PostgreSQL due to their inherent flexible qualities. These databases are par-
ticularly well-suited for our needs because:
Flexibility: Unlike traditional relational databases that require a predefined schema, un-
structured databases allow us to store data in various formats. This is beneficial for
our project as we are dealing with different types of data (like video files, compliance
records, and emotional analysis data) that may evolve over time without requiring exten-
sive schema modifications.
Scalability: Unstructured databases can easily accommodate large volumes of data,
making them ideal for our PPE Detection System, which may generate substantial data
from video feeds and compliance records as it scales.
Rich Data Types: These databases support complex data types such as arrays and nested
documents, enabling us to represent relationships and hierarchies more naturally. For
instance, a worker’s compliance history can include multiple records without needing
separate tables.
Performance: By using document-based storage, we can retrieve entire documents in a
single query, which improves performance, especially when dealing with large datasets,
such as those generated by the video analysis for PPE compliance.
3.3.2 Information Transformation into Data Structures:
For unstructured databases, it is considerably harder to visually display the schema, due
to their abstract nature and scalable structure.However, despite these limitations, we pro-

Worker: Represents each worker with attributes such as worker ID, name, role, contact
