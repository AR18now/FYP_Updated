1.2 Scope
The scope of the project is the development of our app Little Learner, the AI Tutor that is
specified for children aged between 3-7 [55]. The Tutor will:
• Target Audience: Focus on children aged 3-7, including those with disabilities,
ensuring the system is inclusive and accessible to children with various communi-
cation challenges.
• Technological Focus: Utilize advanced AI technologies, including adapting and
fine-tuning and training current technologies to accurately understand and interact
with children in their native languages (Urdu). The project will create a custom
dataset tailored to the linguistic and accent variations found in children from Pak-
istan.
• Educational Goals: Enhance the educational opportunities for young children by
improving foundational language skills through personalized AI-driven interac-
tions. The project aims to make learning more accessible and engaging, thereby
fostering greater inclusivity in early childhood education.
• Model Development: Fine-tune existing transcription models, followed by devel-
opment of a prompted intent classification model. This will accurately interpret and
respond to children’s voice inputs.
• Data Collection and Processing : Creating a custom dataset of Pakistani chil-
dren(aged 3-7 years old), speaking numbers 0-9. This will include data annotation,
pre-processing and structuring of the data.
• Implementation and Evaluation: The AI tutor will be refined using this custom
dataset, ensuring that the final product is effective for the educational needs of each
child.

1.3 Modules
The project focuses on both technical development of AI models and the practical appli-

1.3 Modules
1.3.1 Voice Interaction and Processing Module
This is the core module of the system that will handle the voice-based interaction and
processing of the little learner app.
1. Speech Recognition: Records and transcribes the children voice using our fine-
tuned whisper model, recognising the diverse patterns.
2. Answer Validation: Use LLM to properly analyze and understand the children’s
responses by using the context of the question.
3. Error Handling : Sometimes due to random reasons, the system is not able to
recognize the response it will ask to speak again.
1.3.2 Content Management and Personalization Module
This module will manage the content based on the kid’s preferences and interests. It will
also personalize the questions based on the difficulty level of the kid.
1. Dynamic Content Repository: Store and manage a library of educational content
that will be used to ask the questions.
2. Personalization: Customize the questions based on the difficulty level of the kid
that is being maintained with time.
3. Progress Tracking: Keep a record of the child’s progress and then change the
difficulty level of questions accordingly.
1.3.3 Data Collection and Processing
This module is responsible for collecting and making the dataset that will be used later to
fine-tune our transcription model. Collecting audio through a web page and then process-
ing and labeling those audio files will be done in this module.
1. Web-Based Audio Collection Interface: Ensure the interface is intuitive for users,
potentially children or their guardians, to submit audio samples.
