Project: PenTrack - Football Penalty Kick Analysis and Goalkeeper Simulation
================================================================================

1. INTRODUCTION
1.1 Purpose
This document outlines the requirements for PenTrack, a system that analyzes a football striker's body pose during penalty kicks using pose estimation and machine learning to predict shot outcomes and simulate a goalkeeper's reaction.

1.2 Scope
The system analyzes user-uploaded penalty footage using pose estimation (OpenPose/MediaPipe) to extract key body points. An LSTM model predicts shot direction and power. A 3D simulation then shows a pre-trained goalkeeper animation reacting to the predicted shot. The system includes a user-friendly mobile/web interface for uploading videos and viewing results.

1.3 Definitions, Acronyms, Abbreviations
- LSTM: Long Short-Term Memory
- CNN: Convolutional Neural Network
- UI/UX: User Interface/User Experience
- 3D: Three-Dimensional

2. OVERALL DESCRIPTION
2.1 Product Perspective
PenTrack helps players, especially in regions with limited access to high-level training, improve their penalty kick technique by providing precise, pose-based insights and a realistic training environment.

2.2 Product Functions (Summary)
- Data Collection: Gather video data (YouTube, SoccerNet).
- Model Training: Process pose data, train/fine-tune LSTM model, validate performance.
- App Development: UI/UX design, application development, model/simulation integration.
- Simulation: Set up 3D environment, render goalkeeper animations based on model predictions.

2.3 User Classes and Characteristics
- Player: Individual wanting to improve penalty kicks using the app individually or with a trainer.
- Coaches: Use the app to analyze player posture and shot outcomes to give personalized feedback.
- Football Clubs: Standardize training, manage player analytics.
- Researchers: Use the app to learn workflow and bring further innovation to sports analysis.

3. FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS
3.1 Video Upload & Preprocessing
FR1: The app shall allow users to upload their recorded penalty kick videos.
FR2: The app shall preprocess uploaded videos to extract relevant frames for analysis.
FR3: The app shall validate uploaded video format and size to ensure compatibility.

3.2 Pose Estimation Module
FR1: The app shall extract body pose keypoints from the video frames.
FR2: The app shall store the extracted keypoints for subsequent processing.

3.3 Prediction Module
FR1: The app shall process video data to make predictions about shot outcomes (shot direction and power).
FR2: The app shall validate the predictions to ensure consistent results.

3.4 Simulation Module
FR1: The app shall render simulation results based on the model's predictions.
FR2: The app shall simulate the interaction between the penalty shot and a goalkeeper model reacting to the predicted outcome.
FR3: The app shall provide a visual representation of the goalkeeper's reaction alongside the simulation results.

3.5 App Module
FR1: The app shall provide a user-friendly interface for users to navigate through the app.
FR2: The app shall allow users to easily upload videos and view simulation results.
FR3: The app shall ensure that the insights provided on penalty kicks are easy to understand for non-technical users.

4. NON-FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS
4.1 Reliability
NFR1: The app shall ensure that prediction results for different videos exhibit a consistency rate of at least 95% across multiple tests.
NFR2: The app shall guarantee that data is saved reliably, with a data retention rate of 99.9%.
NFR3: The app shall generate accurate simulations, achieving an accuracy rate of at least 80% when comparing predicted outcomes to actual outcomes based on historical data.

4.2 Usability
NFR4: At least 90% of users shall complete key tasks (uploading a video, viewing results) without assistance during usability testing.
NFR5: The insights provided on penalty kicks shall be rated as "easy to understand" or higher by at least 80% of users in post-interaction surveys.

4.3 Performance
NFR6: The model should be efficient enough to provide the penalty results within (10-20) seconds for a player's uploaded video.
NFR7: Users should be able to upload their videos successfully within 20 seconds.
NFR8: The simulation should run smoothly on at least 85% of devices without buffering or lag.

4.4 Security
NFR9: User's personal data (uploaded videos, insights) should be stored securely and only made visible to authenticated users.
NFR10: The app should ensure no unnecessary data is saved for enhanced security.
