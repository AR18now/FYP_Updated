1.2 Scope
The scope of our project includes detection and reporting of crimes within indoor envi-
ronments. However, it can be extended to outdoor environments. In the case of extension,
there is a possibility of reduction in accuracy due to external factors present in the out-
door environments that our product does not cater to. Our system will be engineered to
detect a wide range of crimes including fight detection, weapon detection, detection of
fire induced crimes and destruction of property. Our project includes a reporting system.
It comprises of alerting the relevant authorities through Whats App and the user through
the mobile application when a crime is detected. The alert for the user will be generated
on the desktop as well as the mobile of the user. This makes sure user is not left in the
dark regarding the crime taking place and is alerted at all costs. Our solution also provides
a user friendly interface for easy viewing of the CCTV footage and performing relevant
actions. Sharing of CCTV footage with other users of Crime Sight can easily be done
through our product using our easy-to-navigate interface. Suspicious person tracking fea-
ture allows the user to track the culprit, aiding in capturing the responsible person by the
relevant authorities. The desktop interface also provides a deep insight into the crimes
that have taken place through an analytics portion. This section allows the user to have
an overview of the previous crimes. It also allows the user to better understand on how to
predict and prevent such crimes in the future.

System Overview
Crime Sight is an application that provides user with the ease of mind that their belongings
are always safe, under watch and if anything was to happen to them, it would be instantly
reported. Crime Sight continuously scans the scene for any abnormal crime activity that
may lead to harm or damage to goods.
Crime Sight employs machine learning models to scan your surroundings that are being
monitored by CCTV Cameras and make sure that everything is in order. This is made
possible by predicting on the frames in the video by extracting useful features from them
and processing them alongside other frames to be able to come at a conclusion on what
is happening in real time. [ 33] [66]If any abnormal crimes are detected, Crime Sight will
automatically notify all relevant users and authorities to make sure it does not take time
for news to spread and for necessary action to be taken.
Crime Sight also provides the user with the option to be able to track individuals in past
stored video feeds of the CCTV Cameras. It does this job by tracking people through
their faces and assigning unique IDs to them so that they can be tracked across multiple
cameras. [44] [22] In the end, the tracking module provides you with the combined clips of
the tracked person, providing a complete and comprehensive solution in case the activity
of a person is to be studied.
Moreover, Crime Sight also provides functionalities such as remote feed viewing from
another device, clip editing and sharing with others, one tap emergency contacts and
detailed analytics of both crimes and tracking. It gives the user more control over their
CCTV devices while also providing a very easy to use user interface allowing the users to
fully enjoy the services on their devices with ease.

3.1.2.2 Server Side
Manages all operations that require careful maintenance and high level security such as
personnel data of users, high compute tasks such as the tracking module or effectively
handling reporting to the Police Authorities to make sure they do not get spammed.

3. System Overview
3.2.5.1 Tables and Relationships:
• Primary Keys (PK): Each table has a UUID as its primary key (K1).
• Foreign Keys (FK): The tables are connected via foreign keys using certain pa-
rameters.
• Relationships: These are represented with the help of crow’s foot association.
3.2.5.2 Key Tables:
• USERS: Main table which contains information about the users. The username and
the email have unique constraints.
• CAMERAS: Associated with the users, holds information about the cameras.
• VIDEO_FEEDS: Handles data related to CCTV video feeds.
• TRACKING_REQUESTS: Logs requests initiated by users to track specific ac-
tivities, storing tracking parameters, start and end times, and created timestamps.
• TRACKING_RESULTS: Stores the results of tracking requests, including track-
ing data, timestamps, and completed times.
• CRIME_DATA:Captures detected crimes from video feeds, storing crime types,
detection details, confidence scores, and verification statuses.
• ALERTS: Stores alerts triggered by detected crimes, tracking alert statuses, crime
IDs, and alert creation and update times.
• SHARED_CLIPS: Manages video clips shared by users, including video feed IDs,
download counts, expiration details, and active statuses.
• EMERGENCY_CONTACTS: Contains emergency contact details for users, in-
cluding phone numbers and contact statuses.
3.3 Data Design
3.3.1 CCTV Video Feed
• The video stream is loaded in, and individual frames are extracted from the video.
• Frames are extracted at a fixed interval of every 10th successive frame.
42
3.3 Data Design
• Once the frames are extracted, they are processed and sent to the crime classifier
model for inference.
• The frames are discarded after the model is done with inference, depending upon
the output.
3.3.2 Crime Model Output
• The probabilities generated by the model are processed to determine the type of
action detected.
• The probabilities are then used to determine whether to alert the relevant authorities.
• The frames are converted back into video format to be used as evidence and sent to
the owner and relevant authorities.
• The output at the end is discarded.
3.3.3 User Personal Information
• User data is stored securely in a database and can only be accessed by the Database
Administrator or the user.
• Data is used by the application to perform actions such as logging in, sharing videos,
and reporting to relevant authorities.
3.3.4 Data Storage Items
• PostgreSQL will be used to store the private information of the users.
• A Video Sequence Generator is used during the model training and testing pro-
cess. It is responsible for handling all data related to model training.
