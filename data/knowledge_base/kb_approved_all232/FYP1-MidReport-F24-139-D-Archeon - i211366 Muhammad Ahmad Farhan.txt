Chapter 1
Introduction
"A picture is worth a thousand words", but "freedom" isn’t one of them. Historically,
novel forms of visualization have always revolutionized how information is conveyed.
After all, the human brain is very receptive to visual perceptions, being capable of iden-
tifying images seen for as little as 13 milliseconds–about 8x faster than the blink of an
eye. From painting portraits to printing paper, capturing images to recording videos, vi-
sual media has always restricted us to only seeing what is captured from a single fixed
perspective. This limits the potentials that lie in visual media for depth of immersion and
the feeling of being there.
What if we take it a step further? What if we could add depth to visuals and provide the
freedom to interact with them? This is where 3D media comes into play. 3D media offers
an immersive experience that transcends the constraints of fixed perspective visuals. The
power of 3D visualization lies not only in its ability to capture the essence of a space but
also in its potential to convey the ambiance, functionality, and spatial abstracts in a way
that traditional media cannot.
The Archeon project envisioned in this report addresses the limitations of traditional me-
dia in conveying the vibe and ambiance of a space. By providing virtual, 360° indoor
views of reconstructed real-world venues, Archeon lets users interactively explore the en-
vironment and get a better feel of the space. The core objective of the project include har-
nessing advanced deep learning and computer vision to create realistic, multi-dimensional
novel views of interior spaces using scans captured from everyday mobile devices.
The scope of this report covers the vision, concept, design, development, anticipated im-
pact and potential applications of this project. It aims to provide a comprehensive per-
spective on the project, from its inception to the technical details that make it a reality.

Chapter 3
Project Vision
3.1 Problem Statement
When choosing a venue for an event, an apartment to purchase, or a new workspace,
we often find ourselves wondering: Does the space have the right ambiance? Does the
lighting feel comforting? Is the layout functional for productivity? Is the environment
cramped or spacious? While photos and videos give us some idea, they are no substitute
for feeling like you’re there, even when visiting in person isn’t an option. This is where
digital 3D environment models come into play. There is a need for immersive experiences
that are interactive, photorealistic, and multi-dimensional[ 33]. Experiences that recreate
the essence, feeling, and vibe of a space, thereby enabling users to naturally and intuitively
find the information they need.
Archeon aims to help with this by creating realistic, multi-dimensional digital models of
interior spaces using scans captured from everyday mobile devices. This project uses re-
cent advances in deep learning and computer vision to make 3D reconstruction technology
available to a broader audience.
3.2 Business Opportunity
The motivation behind Archeon is to revolutionize how we capture and explore real-world
spaces. Traditionally, 3D modeling has been largely confined to niche design industries
due to its high complexity and costs. However, with a market projected to grow from
$6.77B in 2024 to $22.19B by 2032[11], there is immense potential in making the technol-
ogy more accessible. Whether it’s for selecting event venues, exploring homes or hotel
rooms, inspecting new workspaces or restaurants, or touring heritage sites, the ability to
capture real-world spaces in 3D has widespread applications across worlds.

3. Project Vision
3.3 Objectives
1. Deep learning Reconstruction: Use of deep learning methods to allow users to gen-
erate high-quality models without requiring expert knowledge.
2. User-Friendly Mobile Interface: Provide an intuitive interface for capturing, recon-
structing, and viewing indoor spaces to make the process accessible for profession-
als and casual users alike.
3. Cross Platform Compatibility: Make the app able to run on widely available mobile
devices to eliminate the need for professional equipment.
4. Virtual Reality Integration: Include features for users to explore and interact with
their reconstructed environments in VR.
3.4 Project Scope
The scope of the Archeon project encompasses the development of a mobile application
for high-fidelity 3D neural reconstruction of indoor spaces. The application will use ad-
vanced deep learning techniques to allow users to scan and reconstruct real-world scenes
into detailed 3D geometry.[22] The project consists of three main components:
• Research and Model Development: Identifying and fine-tuning the most effective
deep learning models for 3D reconstruction. The goal is to achieve high visual and
geometric fidelity.
• Mobile Application Development: Creating a cross-platform mobile application
that integrates these deep learning models. The application will enable users to
capture, reconstruct and view 3D environments with ease.
• Virtual Reality Integration: Develop support for immersive VR experiences. Users
will be able to explore and interact with their reconstructed environments in a virtual
reality setting.
3.5 Constraints
The project will be limited to static indoor environments and will not handle dynamic
scenes with moving objects. The results will also depend on the quality of input video as
well as surface and environment complexity. Additionally, the systems performance will
vary based on device and network capabilities. Lastly, accuracy will be prioritized over
speed, so there may be a trade-off in the inference time

5.1 Architectural Design
Architecture Choice Justification
The client-server architecture is ideal for this system due to the following reasons:
1. Resource Efficiency: Offloads GPU-intensive deep learning processing to a powerful
server to minimize client hardware requirements.
2. Scalability: Supports a growing user base by allowing multiple clients to connect with-
out degrading server performance.
3. Centralized Processing: Ensures consistent and reliable 3D model generation by main-
taining control over algorithms, models and hardware.
4. Lightweight Clients: Simplifies client design, focusing on scan capture and result dis-
play.
5. Data Security: Centralizes storage of sensitive scans and models, reducing risks asso-
ciated with local data storage on client devices.
This architecture effectively balances performance, user experience, and security for 3D
model generation.
Procedural Approach Justification
A procedural approach was chosen for the Archeon project due to its efficiency in man-
aging the system’s data flow and processing tasks. Given the nature of 3D reconstruction
and environment rendering, a procedural approach allows for clear, linear sequences of
operations that can be easily followed and optimized. This approach facilitates the devel-
opment of specialized functions for each critical process, such as scanning, processing,
and rendering. Additionally, the procedural model simplifies debugging and testing, as in-
dividual functions can be assessed in isolation before integration. While object-oriented
programming offers advantages in terms of encapsulation and reusability, the procedural
method aligns more closely with the system’s immediate needs for rapid data processing
and minimal computational overhead. Thus, the procedural approach is ideal for deep
learning and 3D graphics rendering required in this project.
