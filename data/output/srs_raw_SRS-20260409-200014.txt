INTRODUCTION
Purpose: To provide a comprehensive medical appointment scheduling system for patients and healthcare providers.
Scope: This document outlines the requirements for the hospital appointment management system focusing on functionalities related to patient-doctor interactions, including finding doctors, booking appointments, managing schedules, handling walk-ins, and maintaining records.
Definitions/Acronyms:
- DOCTOR: Healthcare professional providing medical services.
- PATIENT: Individual seeking medical care.
- WALK-IN: Patient arriving without pre-scheduled appointment.
References: IEEE 830-1998
Overview: This system will facilitate efficient appointment management through intuitive interfaces for both patients and doctors, ensuring timely access to healthcare while minimizing administrative overhead.

OVERALL DESCRIPTION
Product Perspective: The system is designed to streamline the process of making and managing medical appointments between patients and doctors within a hospital setting.
Product Functions:
- Patients can search for doctors based on specialties and book appointments.
- Patients receive reminders via SMS or email about upcoming appointments.
- Doctors can manage their schedules, block unavailable time slots, and record visit outcomes.
- Walk-in patients can be accommodated and scheduled accordingly.
User Characteristics: Users include patients who require medical attention and doctors responsible for treating them.
Constraints: The system must not allow double bookings, ensure that all booked times are unique, and support basic patient history visibility during booking.
Assumptions/Dependencies: The system assumes reliable internet connectivity and standard SMS/email delivery mechanisms.

SPECIFIC REQUIREMENTS
External Interface Requirements:
- User Interface: Graphical interface for patients and doctors.
- Hardware Interface: Standard computer systems and mobile devices.
- Software Interface: Integration with existing hospital databases.
- Communication Interface: SMS gateway for sending reminders and email server for notifications.
Functional Requirements:
- FR-01: Find Doctor By Specialty
Input: Patient searches for doctor by specialty name.
Processing: System retrieves list of available doctors matching the specialty.
Output: List of doctors displayed to the patient.
Priority: High
- FR-02: Book Appointment
Input: Patient selects preferred date, time slot, and doctor from the list.
Processing: System checks for conflicts and reserves the selected slot if available.
Output: Confirmation message sent to the patient.
Priority: High
- FR-03: Get Reminders
Input: Patient provides contact information.
Processing: System sends reminder messages at specified intervals before the appointment.
Output: Reminder sent via SMS or email.
Priority: Medium
- FR-04: Block Unavailable Slots
Input: Doctor marks specific dates/times as unavailable.
Processing: System updates schedule to reflect these unavailability periods.
Output: Updated schedule shown to other users.
Priority: High
- FR-05: View Daily Schedule
Input: Doctor logs into the system.
Processing: System displays the full day’s schedule including reserved and blocked slots.
Output: Detailed schedule presented to the doctor.
Priority: High
- FR-06: Mark Completed Visits
Input: Doctor indicates completion status after seeing a patient.
Processing: System records the outcome of the visit.
Output: Visit marked as complete in the database.
Priority: High
- FR-07: Handle Walk-In Patients
Input: Patient arrives unexpectedly without a reservation.
Processing: System allows the doctor to add a new entry for the walk-in.
Output: Walk-in appointment added to the schedule.
Priority: Medium
- FR-08: Reschedule Appointments
Input: Patient requests to change the original appointment details.
Processing: System finds alternative slots and rebooks the appointment.
Output: New confirmation message sent to the patient.
Priority: Medium
- FR-09: Cancel Appointments
Input: Patient cancels the previously made appointment.
Processing: System removes the reservation and makes the slot available again.
Output: Cancellation confirmed to the patient.
Priority: Medium
Non-functional Requirements:
- Usability: The system shall be easy to navigate and understand for both patients and doctors.
- Reliability: The system must accurately track all appointments and prevent double bookings.
- Performance: The system shall respond quickly to user inputs and handle up to 100 concurrent users efficiently.
- Portability: The system shall run on various operating systems and devices commonly used in hospitals.

SYSTEM FEATURES
Feature 1: Patient Registration
- Allows patients to create accounts and store personal information securely.
- Ensures that patient data is protected according to privacy laws.

Feature 2: Doctor Login
- Enables doctors to log in and access their schedule and patient history.
- Provides secure authentication methods to protect sensitive medical information.

Feature 3: Appointment Management
- Facilitates booking, rescheduling, canceling, and marking appointments as completed.
- Manages walk-in patients effectively and integrates seamlessly with existing hospital workflows.

Feature 4: Notification System
- Sends automated reminders to patients and doctors via SMS and email.
- Tracks the status of sent notifications and ensures high delivery rates.

Feature 5: Audit Trail
- Maintains a log of all significant events related to appointments, such as changes in schedules or cancellations.
- Supports auditing and compliance with regulatory standards.
