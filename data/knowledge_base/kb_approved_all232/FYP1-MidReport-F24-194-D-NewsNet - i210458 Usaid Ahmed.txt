1.2 Scope
Causal relationship detection through causal arguments by feeding into a knowledge
graph to then help an LLM (Large Language Model) leveraging GraphRAG (Retrieval
Augmented Generation) to retrieve a Cause/Effect sequence of events chained together
giving a new sense of depth to news.
Scraping unstructured news text data from the web to extract meaningful causal re-
lationships. Adhering by an ontology of news to find specific repeating relationships to
create a graph. Applying a model approach on extracting relationships. Detecting events
and assigning them as anchor points in our knowledge graph to make sense of the news
and how different entities have a causal relationship among them. Querying the said
knowledge graph to find various relationships in depth depending on type of query. Thus
aiding the LLM to know where to look and how entities have a deeper relationship with
one another than how it seems on the surface. Searching based on category and applying
user chosen geographic filters to further create user tailored recommendations and results.

System Overview
NewsNet aims to deliver a system that builds causal relationships in news articles using
knowledge graphs. It addresses the complexity of news events and the need for automated
tools to analyze vast amount of news data to provide users a better understanding of the
cause-effect relationships between events. The system follows a client-server architecture
which focuses on entity detection, graph generation and user interaction by providing a
user-friendly platform for journalists, researchers and news analysts.
3.1 Architectural Design
The architecture of NewsNet is designed based on Client-Server model. The modular
system is composed of multiple interconnected subsystems. The design is structured in
order to extract and handle large amount of news data using Natural Language Process-
ing(NLP) techniques to highlight the casual relationship between events.
Major subsystems:
1. Data Collection:
Scraping news articles from different online sources (to ensure continuous data flow)
2. NER and Event Extraction:
Processing collected news data to extract key events and their respective relations using
NER (Named Entity-Event Recognition)
3. Casual Graph generation:
Building a casual knowledge graph by identifying relationships between events (based on
NER output.)
4. Knowledge Graph(KG) Visualization:
Displaying an interactive interface for users to explore the cause-effect relationships.
5. User Interaction and Search:
Provides search functionality for querying the knowledge graph for improved results.

The data design of NewsNet primarily focuses on storing and organizing information
from the news articles about events and their relationships to make it easy to access and
visualize. While json files act as database for storing and managing structured details
like article titles, news content and publication dates, a graph database aids in handling
connection between different events and entities.
