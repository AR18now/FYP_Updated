1. Introduction
1.2 Scope
The scope of our Enhanced Driver Assist (EDA) project is to create a device that will
monitor the driver’s attention and distraction, will provide alert warnings in case of dan-
gerous situation, and if the driver monitoring is not enough, it will also include warning
beeps at lane departure, so if the driver is moving out of lane unintentionally or not, he
will be alerted. We are targeting highways and motorways of Pakistan, since the lanes
are marked there, and dangerous problems such as driver fatigue, distraction, and risky
driving behaviors are rampant. The product is specifically designed for Locally assem-
bled and popular cars, such as Toyota Corolla, Honda Civic, Honda City, etc, which can
provide the minimum required OBD data for driving behavior analysis.
It will provide the following functionalities:
• Monitor the driver’s eye or facial movements to detect any signs of drowsiness or
fatigue.
• Lane detection and departure alert system which will alert the driver in case the car
drifts out of lane.
• The system analyzes driving patterns through OBD-II data, identifying dangerous
behaviors such as harsh acceleration or speeding.
• V oice alerts keep the driver focused by offering hands-free alerts and warnings.
• Dashcams and hardware work together to monitor both the driver and road condi-
tions, with local processing for good response times.

timely driver assistance and potential safety risks. To protect against failures, the sys-
tem shall perform regular self-checks. A strategy for error detection will involve logging
errors and monitoring system performance metrics, while error correction shall include
alerts to the administrator.

System Overview
The Enhanced Driver Assist (EDA) system is designed to improve road safety and driver
performance through advanced monitoring and feedback mechanisms. The system uti-
lizes a combination of hardware and software components, including in-cabin and out-
cabin cameras, OBD-II sensors, and data processing capabilities to assess driver behavior,
monitor lane positions, and provide timely alerts for hazardous situations.
This system integrates machine learning models to analyze driving patterns, drowsiness
detection, and lane departure monitoring, ensuring that drivers receive immediate feed-
back to enhance their driving experience and safety. The system is particularly suited for
highway driving, focusing on mid-range sedan cars like Toyota Corolla and Honda Civic.
3.1 Architectural Design
The EDA system is modular, comprising several interconnected components that work
collaboratively to deliver the desired functionality. The main modules include:
• Driver Attention Monitoring: This module assesses driver alertness and provides
warnings if drowsiness or distractions are detected.
• Lane Departure Monitoring: This module detects lane boundaries and warns the
driver when the vehicle deviates from its lane.
• Driving Pattern Analysis: This module analyzes OBD-II data for driving behavior,
generating reports on unsafe driving patterns.
• V oice Alert: This module provides voice notifications for potential hazards and
driver assistance, helping to keep the driver focused.

3.3 Data Design
The Enhanced Driver Assist (EDA) system handles a variety of data entities critical to its
functionality. This section describes how the information within the system is transformed
into data structures, processed, and stored using MongoDB Atlas as the primary database
solution. Each data entity is organized to optimize storage, retrieval, and processing,
ensuring the system’s overall performance and reliability.
3.3.1 Data Entities
The major data entities in the EDA system include:
• Driver Information: Contains driver-specific details such as name, ID, vehicle
type, driving history, and relevant profiles.
• Logs: Stores continuous logs from the vehicle, including OBD-II sensor data,
timestamps, and driving patterns for analysis.
• Alerts: Records alerts generated by the system, such as drowsiness detection, lane
departure warnings, and hazard alerts, along with timestamps and context of the
alert.

3.3.2 Data Storage and Processing
MongoDB Atlas is used as the cloud-hosted NoSQL database, providing a flexible and
scalable solution for storing the unstructured data generated by the EDA system. Key
considerations include:
• Driver Information Collection: This data is stored in a collection called ‘drivers‘,
where each document contains driver-specific details and metadata.
• Logs: Continuous OBD-II data logs are stored in a ‘logs‘ collection. Each docu-
ment includes a reference to the associated driver, vehicle, and timestamp to allow
for pattern recognition and performance tracking.
• Alerts: Generated alerts are stored in an ‘alerts‘ collection, tagged with event data
such as the type of alert, associated driver, and the system’s response.
• Feedback: All logs and alerts are complied for each diver into a report providing
feedback and allowing accurate time-based analysis of driving behavior.
• Admin: can view reports of logs and alerts in the feedback.
• vehicle: contains vehicle information (which would preferably a sedan according
to domain specified.
This data design allows the EDA system to handle large volumes of diverse data effi-
ciently, ensuring scalability and performance in driver assistance scenarios.
