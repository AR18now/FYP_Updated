1.3 Modules
1.3.1 Detection and Multi-Label Classification of Patient’s Speech
This module is aimed at the detection and multi-label classification of speech disfluencies
in the patient’s speech through audio input. Main objective of this module is to assist the
patient in identifying the exact nature of their speech disfluencies.
1. The system shall be able to detect the presence of speech disfluency in the recorded
audio of the patient.
The patient will be required to read a randomly generated passage. The patient’s
speech will be recorded by the system. The patient’s speech will then be analysed
by Machine Learning models for the presence of any speech disfluencies. The
results will be displayed on the screen.
2. The system shall be able to classify the audio of patient into multiple speech disflu-
ency classes.
3. The system shall be able to recommend relevant speech therapy exercises based on
the classification of the patient’s speech.
1.3.2 Speech Therapy Exercises
This module is concerned with the speech therapy exercises that the patient will try in
order to improve the fluency of their speech. Each speech therapy exercise follows a
gamified approach to better engage the patients and to help with their retention as well.
1. Reading Passage Exercise: A passage shall be generated byLarge Language ModelLarge Language Model
(LLMLLM). The patient shall read the passage while their speech is being recorded

by the system. The difficulty setting shall determine the number and com-
plexity of the words in the passage. The recorded audio of the patient shall be
transcribed into text and compared with the given passage. Any differences

2. Matching Syllables Exercise: The patient shall speak challenging words whose

shall pronounce the given word over 4 different BPMs: 64, 92, 118, 144 with
the word repeating 3 times each. The patient’s audio shall be recorded and
transcribed to text in order to compare their pronounced syllables to the actual
word which shall determine their performance. At the end of the exercise, a
feedback report will be displayed which will inform the patient about their
performance in the exercise (disfluency severity), how much they improved
and difficulty setting of the exercise.
3. Sound Mimic: A pre-recorded audio shall be played to the user, containing 1-2
clauses that form a sentence. This sentence shall be free of any stuttering.
The patient shall then repeat the sentence from the played audio. The speech
of the patient would be recorded and transcribed and then compared with the
original audio that was played. The difficulty for this exercise shall be in-
creased by using sentences that have more challenging words to pronounce.
Any differences detected shall be used for detecting speech disfluencies, mea-
suring severity and measuring performance metric. At the end of the exercise,
a feedback report will be displayed which will inform the patient about their
performance in the exercise (disfluency severity), how much they improved
and the difficulty setting chosen for the exercise.
4. Breathing Control: The patient shall follow animations for breathing in and breath-
ing out to help control their anxiousness, promote relaxation and reducing fre-
quency of stuttering moments. The patient shall then read a sentence after the
breathing exercises to gauge their performance and impact of the breathing
activity on the patient. This activity does not have difficulty options. At the
end of the exercise, a feedback report will be displayed which will inform the
patient about their performance in the exercise (disfluency severity) and how
much they improved.

1.3.3 Baseline of the Patient
This module is concerned with establishing a baseline of the patient’s speech. The main
objective of this module is to create a user profile for the patient along with estimating
the normal rate of speech of the patient. This baseline would assist in the analysis of the
patient’s speech and checking for any improvement in the fluency.
1. The system shall be able to take input of the patient’s details and create a profile for
the patient.
An input form will be displayed which the patient will fill according to their specific
details. Once confirmed, the system will save the patient’s profile.
2. The system shall be able to establish the normal rate of speech of the patient.
This will be done by providing the patient with passages to read from. The difficulty
level of the passages will start from high complexity and gradually move towards
easy and simple passages. Each time the patient reads the passage, the patient’s
speech will be checked for any disfluencies. As soon as the analysis of patient’s
speech shows that there are no more speech disfluencies, the system would then
calculate the normal rate of speech of the patient.
1.3.4 Progress Tracking and Feedback Reports
This module is concerned with providing the user with feedback of their speech therapy
exercises and allowing the user to track their overall progress of improving their speech.
1. The system shall provide the patients with feedback reports after every speech ther-
apy exercise.
The feedback report provided by the system will contain the patient’s performance
(disfluency severity) in the speech therapy exercise, difficulty setting of the exer-
cise, how much the patient improved, time taken to complete the exercise, any
achievement badges unlocked and the amount of XP gained.
2. The patient will be able to check their history of feedbacks.
All feedbacks will be stored and the patient will be able to view their past feedbacks.
This will allow the patient to track their overall progress.
3. The patient will be able to export their feedback reports in PDF format.

The non-functional requirements for the project shall primarily follow standards for web
based applications. The constraints are as follows:

Our application is a web-based application, the system must adhere to standard practices

its features. The system shall have minimal downtime so to make sure user experience

The system shall be easy to use for patients and SLPs for their respective needs. The
system makes sure that patients can focus on improving their speech through the exercises
and be give instructions on the therapy exercises.
USE-1: The system shall provide a clear and intuitive user interface to make it easy for
patients to navigate through different features and exercises.
USE-2: The system shall display instructions before each exercise to help understand
how to complete tasks.
2.3.3 Performance
The system shall have fast response time during low and peak hours of usage.
PER-1: The system shall have a response time of less than 4 seconds.
PER-2: The system shall process and analyze speech audio within 10 seconds.
PER-3: The system shall be able to support up to 500 users.

Appendix A
Dataset
A.1 Title of Dataset
We have utilized two datasets. The title of the first dataset is "Stuttering Events in Podcasts
(SEP-28k)" and the title of the second dataset is "Fluency Bank".
A.2 Description of Dataset
Stuttering Events in Podcasts (SEP-28k): A dataset containing over 28k clips (23 hours
total) labeled with five event types including blocks, prolongations, sound repeti-
tions, word repetitions, and interjections. Audio comes from public podcasts largely
consisting of people who stutter interviewing other people who stutter.
FluencyBank: It is a shared database for the study of fluency development. Partici-
pants include typically-developing monolingual and bilingual children, children
and adults who stutter (C/AWS) or who clutter (C/AWC), and second language
learners. There are around 4k clips in this dataset (3.5 hours total).
A.3 Annotation of Dataset
Audio clips in both datasets were annotated by at least three people who received training
via written descriptions, examples, and audio clips on how to best identify each dysfluency
but were not clinicians.
The annotation of datasets was done using a variant of time-interval based assessment in
which audio recordings are broken into 3 second clips and annotated with binary labels.
A clip may contain multiple stuttering event types along with non-dysfluency labels such

Appendix B
Gamification Elements for Patient
Retention
B.1 Introduction
Gamification refers to "the strategic attempt to enhance systems, services, organizations,
and activities by creating similar experiences to those experienced when playing games
in order to motivate and engage users".
Speech therapy requires consistency in order to improve the fluency of the speech. In
order to encourage patients to stay consistent and better engage them with the speech
therapy, we will employ various gamification elements. This will ensure patient retention,
better engagement and consistency.
B.2 Description of various Gamification Elements
The different gamification elements that we have planned to utilize in the speech therapy
exercises are listed below.
Levelling System: There will be a levelling system for the patients. The patient would
be able to level up by gaining the required amount of Experience Points (XP). XP
is awarded by playing and completing the Speech Therapy exercises. The amount
of XP gained depends on the number of therapy exercises played, percentage im-
provement and consistency. Each level in the levelling system requires a different
amount of XP.
Achievement Badges: The patient will be able to earn Achievement Badges by reaching
certain milestones. The patient will be able to display the Achievement Badges on
36

their profiles. The milestones are:
• Played Speech Therapy Exercise for the First Time
• Played Speech Therapy Exercises daily for X days
• Improved Speech Fluency by 10%
• Achieved Fluency of 80%
• Reached Level 10
• Reached Level 100
Streak: The patient can start a streak by playing speech therapy exercises daily. The
streak will be displayed on their profile. The streak would end if the patient misses
a day of playing therapy exercises.
