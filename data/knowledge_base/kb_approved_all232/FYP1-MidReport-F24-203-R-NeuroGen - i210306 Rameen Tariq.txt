Introduction 
1.1 Problem Domain 
Present Challenge: MRI scans typically generate high-resolution images, but they often 
show missing or corrupted regions, which nega tively affects the reliability of the 
diagnosis. This makes pinpointing brain tumors extremely difficult. 
Essential Requirement: Precise evaluation of glioma tumors is necessary for efficient 
diagnosis and therapeutic strategizing. Unfortunately, because o f the limits of the 
imaging data, medical experts frequently struggle to provide accurate MRI 
interpretations. 
Study Motivation: By producing upgraded pictures that correct for missing or corrupted 
regions, this study seeks to solve the deficiencies in MRI analysis for patients diagnosed 
with gliomas. Our goal is to enhance the accuracy of MRU scans to enable healthcare 
providers to make well-informed decisions and optimize patient outcomes in the setting 
of brain tumour management. 
1.2 Problem Statement 
The project tackles the difficulty of interpreting brain MRI scans with gliomas, where 
standard algorithms frequently underperform due to the presence of lesions. Such 
algorithms are usually trained on healthy brain pictures, which limits their usefulness in 
clinical situations when analysing tumor -affected regions.The proposed program 
attempts to address this issue by using advanced inpainting techniques to generate 
healthy 3D brain tissue in tumor -affected areas. This approach will help to resto re MRI 
data integrity, increasing the dependability of essential therapeutic operations including 
brain tissue segmentation, anatomical parcellation, and tumour growth modelling. The 
method improves the performance of brain MRI analysis by providing realis tic tissue 
reconstructions, making it better suited for clinical decision making. 
1.3 Proposed Solution/Method 
The proposed approach is an extensive machine learning method that employs a number 
of cutting-edge models, including Denoising Diffusion Probabilistic Models (DDPMs), to 
in-paint (create) 3D healthy brain tissue in the region impacted by the glioma tumour. 
 
2 
We’ll use MRI scans of glioma-affected brains as input, together with an in-painting mask 
to highlight the tumor -affected area. The solution wi ll generate healthy tissue brain 
within the masked region in voided part of the MRI by experimenting with a variety of 
in-painting techniques, including DDPMs, Generative Adversarial Networks (GANs), 
Variational Auto-encoders (VAEs), and possibly Latent Diffusion Models (LDMs), resulting 
in a complete brain MRI scan that accurately represents a healthy state. This in-painting 
procedure will aid in the reconstruction of missing or damaged portions with realistic 
tissue, ultimately boosting the quality of data used in therapeutic jobs. 
The training phase will involve T1 MRI modality scans from the BraTs dataset 2024 , which 
includes voided tumor-affected MRI scans of Glioma-affected patients. The models will 
learn to synthesize healthy tissue in tumor-affected areas, using the healthy brain tissue 
mask as input with voided MRI . Model performance will be assessed using conventional 
metrices such as SSIM, PSNR and MSE to verify that the synthetically generated regions 
closely mimic real healthy brain tissue. Fur thermore, several approaches such as 3D 
Wavelet-based models or memory efficient 3D models, will be explored to overcome 
scaling concerns with high -resolution MRIs The project will not only generate realistic 
healthy brain tissue, but will also seek to inc rease the ability to enhance the analysis of 
brain MRI , making it more useful for clinical applications such as tissue segmentation, 
anatomical parcellation, and tumour growth tracking. The approach will use both 
diffusion models and alternative in -painting approaches, resulting in complete results 
and a strong answer to medical imaging difficulties.

2.5 Blind MRI Brain Lesion In-painting Using Deep Learning 
2.5.1 Summary: 
This research paper [12] focusses on an inpainting technique for brain MRI images 
damaged by lesions using deep learning. By using a 3D U -Net architecture for blind 
inpainting, the technique replaces the requirement for manual lesion delineation b y 
automatically reconstructing parts of the lesion with brain tissue that seems healthy. The 
network is taught in two stages: first, artificial training data is created using a non -blind 
inpainting network, and then the inpainted images are utilised to train a blind inpainting 
network. The model improves robustness against the existence of lesions, which 
improves the performance of existing pipelines, such as brain tissue segmentation. 
2.5.2 Critical Analysis: 
1-The time -consuming and error -prone nature of conventional in -painting techniques is 
decreased by using blind in -painting, which eliminates the need for manual lesion area 
identification. 
2-The use of a complete 3D U-Net, rather than 2D slices or 3D patches, allows for better 
context awareness and more accurate in-painting of entire brain volumes. 
3-Given the solutions’ possible clinical use, the computational efficiency of the model , 
which can handle data in as little as 0.5 seconds is remarkable. 
4-As the method is primarily intended to treat white matter anomalies, it is not 
generalized to other brain tumors, such as gliomas or vascular lesions.

2.8 Brain MR Atlas Construction Using Symmetric Deep Neural 
Inpainting 
2.8.1 Summary: 
This paper [19]describes a deep learning -based method for in -painting brain MRI data, 
focusing on tumor -affected areas. The method seeks to replace tumor regions with 
synthetic normal tissue to ease brain atlas building, which is a bit difficult when tumor s 
are present. The proposed method constructs a statistical brain atlas using only 
pathological MRI data [5], filling irregularly shaped tumor patches with realistic tissue. 
Handling randomly dispersed tumor locations, leveraging a symmetry constraint 
between brain hemispheres, and making use of partial datasets are all examples of key

12 
improvements. The method was tested on the Multimodal Brain Tumor Segmentation 
(BraTS) dataset and demonstrated higher reconstruction quality, cross -correlation, and 
mutual i nformation metrics than earlier methods.The 2018 Multimodal Brain Tumor 
Segmentation (BraTS) dataset is used in this paper .This database consists of 
approximately 210 clinical 3T multimodal MRI scans of glioblastoma and lower -grade 
glioma with pathologica lly confirmed diagnoses. The tumor locations were manually 
identified by expert neuro -radiologists and used as ground truth. The dataset includes 
T1-weighted, T2-weighted, and T1-weighted contrastenhanced (T1ce) MRI scans [1]. 
2.8.2 Critical Analysis: 
1.The technique successfully handles incomplete datasets with irregular tumour regions, 
overcoming a common limitation in brain atlas creation. 
2.The symmetry restriction between brain hemispheres improves in -painting accuracy, 
yielding more realistically reproduced regions. 
3.Several assessment metrics (SSIM, PSNR, and L1 error) show that the method 
outperforms earlier in-painting techniques such as patch-match and partial convolution. 
4.In this paper method relies mostly on 2D slices, restricting its potenti al to capture 3D 
information, which could increase in-painting accuracy in MR data. 
5.The symmetry notion, while useful, may not always hold true due to natural asymmetry 
in brain structures, and significant misalignments might impair performance. 
6.The method is strongly reliant on the manual modification of loss function weights, 
which may reduce in-painting performance and create bias. 
2.8.3 Relation to the project: 
This study closely resembles our project in that both aim to rebuild brain tissue in tumor 
affected areas. While this research focusses on statistical brain atlas building and uses 
2D slices, our project will broaden its scope by focussing on 3D inpainting, making it more 
useful to clinical analysis tasks such as tumour monitoring and tissue segmentation. The 
symmetry restriction they applied can serve as inspiration for our method, but we want 
to take it a step further by combining multi-modal MRI data and advanced 3D inpainting 
techniques.

normalized between the 1st and 99th percentiles to [0, 1] or [-1, 1]. This helps in 
ensuring that learning process is not disturbed.
