Project: SmartGuard - AI-Based Shoplifting Detection System
================================================================================

1. INTRODUCTION
1.1 Problem Statement
According to National Retail Federation (NFR), retail theft recorded in 2017 was over $112 billion, projected to rise above $140 billion by 2025. Traditional CCTV surveillance systems are ineffective as humans are prone to failures, making it hard for retailers to respond in time.

1.2 Scope
In-Scope: Identify shoplifting behaviors including picking a product from shelf, examining a product, putting product back, placing product in bag/pocket/clothing without paying. Out-of-Scope: Physical aggression, property damage, verbal aggression, consumption of products in-store. Facial recognition requires clear visibility of face.

1.3 Definitions, Acronyms, Abbreviations
- AI: Artificial Intelligence
- MTBF: Mean Time Between Failures
- AES: Advanced Encryption Standard
- NFR: National Retail Federation

2. OVERALL DESCRIPTION
2.1 Product Perspective
SmartGuard automates detection of shoplifting activities and alerts store management using computer vision to track behaviors in real-time.

2.2 Product Functions (Summary)
- Live feed monitoring
- Suspicious behavior detection
- Alert management
- Facial recognition
- Centralized watchlist across multiple stores
- Customer visit tracking
- Incident reporting and analytics
- Image-based search
- Store management

2.3 User Classes and Characteristics
- Store Managers: Receive real-time alerts, access reports via mobile/web
- Store Employees: Monitor camera feed, respond to alerts
- System Administrators: Handle system, add/remove stores, provide technical support
- CCTV Technicians: Ensure integration, handle hardware maintenance

2.4 Operating Environment
Mobile and web applications, Android devices (version 10+), modern browsers.

3. FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS
3.1 Real Time Monitoring and Detection
FR1.1: The system shall continuously monitor connected cameras for suspicious behavior.
FR1.2: The system shall detect suspicious behavior (picking item from shelf, putting in bag).
FR1.3: The system shall send notification to store management when suspicious behavior detected.

3.2 Facial Recognition and Centralized Watchlist
FR2.1: The system shall perform facial recognition on detected persons.
FR2.2: The system shall compare detected faces with common watchlist to detect known offenders.
FR2.3: The watchlist shall be accessible by all stores.
FR2.4: The system shall send notification when known shoplifter detected.

3.3 Customer Insights and Analytics
FR3.1: The system shall record number of customers visiting the store.
FR3.2: The system shall log shoplifting incidents (date, time, store location).

3.4 Advance Search and Store Management
FR4.1: The system shall allow store management to search a person by image-based input.
FR4.2: The system shall allow store managers to manage multiple stores from single interface.

4. NON-FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS
4.1 Reliability
NFR1: MTBF ≥ 1,000 hours of operation.
NFR2: Data integrity shall be ensured during failures (no loss of recorded data, logs, alerts).

4.2 Usability
NFR3: Core functions accessible with ≤ 3 clicks.
NFR4: Users shall operate system after ≤ 2 hours of training.
NFR5: Mobile app compatibility with Android versions 10 and above.
NFR6: Web interface compatibility with Chrome, Firefox, Safari, Edge.

4.3 Performance
NFR7: Live video feed latency < 8 seconds.
NFR8: Suspicious activity detection ≤ 10 seconds of event capture.
NFR9: System shall handle up to 50 concurrent users without noticeable lag.
NFR10: Facial recognition searches ≤ 5 seconds against watchlist.
NFR11: Alerts sent ≤ 10 seconds after detecting suspicious behavior.

4.4 Security
NFR12: Data encrypted at rest and in transit using ≥ 256-bit AES encryption.
NFR13: Account locked for 10 minutes after 3 failed login attempts.
NFR14: User actions logged with logs stored for 1 year.

4.5 Scalability
NFR15: System shall handle up to 2 stores, each with up to 3 cameras.
NFR16: Cloud infrastructure shall auto-scale when CPU utilization exceeds 70%.
