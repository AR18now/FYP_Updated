Project: newsNexus - AI-Powered Personalized News Aggregation Platform

1. INTRODUCTION
1.1 Purpose
This document presents a detailed plan for the development of newsNexus, an AI-powered news aggregation platform designed to provide a personalized and efficient news consumption experience.

1.2 Scope
The platform personalizes news feeds based on user preferences and consumption patterns. It includes features like query functionality (text/voice), article summaries, video responses, and saved responses.

1.3 Definitions, Acronyms, Abbreviations
- AI: Artificial Intelligence
- ML: Machine Learning
- API: Application Programming Interface
- SQL: Structured Query Language

2. OVERALL DESCRIPTION
2.1 Product Perspective
newsNexus aims to solve problems of information overload, lack of personalization, and difficulty in comparing perspectives by offering a tailored, AI-driven news experience.

2.2 Product Functions (Summary)
- Personalized news feed.
- User profiling.
- Real-time news updates.
- Article summaries.
- Source selection.
- Text and voice search.
- Video query responses.
- Saved responses and history.

2.3 User Classes and Characteristics
- Casual News Reader: Interested in general awareness and trending topics.
- Informed User/Researcher: Seeks in-depth knowledge and multiple perspectives.
- Content Creators/Analysts: Gather news insights and identify trends.
- Project Managers and Stakeholders: Oversee development and progress.

2.4 Operating Environment
Web application.

3. FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS
3.1 Personalization Module
FR1.1: The system shall tailor the news feed based on user preferences (sports, politics, health) and past interactions.
FR1.2: The system shall continuously learn from user behavior to refine recommendations.
FR1.3: The system shall create and maintain a user profile based on reading habits.

3.2 News Content Management Module
FR2.1: The system shall provide real-time news updates.
FR2.2: The system shall generate concise article summaries.
FR2.3: The system shall categorize news articles into topics (politics, sports, technology).

3.3 Source Management Module
FR3.1: The system shall allow users to choose and filter news based on their preferred sources.
FR3.2: The system shall provide an option to modify source preferences.

3.4 Search and Query Module
FR4.1: The system shall allow users to search for news using text-based queries.
FR4.2: The system shall provide a voice search feature for audio queries.
FR4.3: The system shall generate video responses for queries related to videos, showing relevant highlights.

4. NON-FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS
4.1 Reliability
NFR1: Real-time news updates will be made every 3 hours.

4.2 Availability
NFR2: The platform shall be accessible 24/7.
NFR3: The system shall provide previous data effectively for 15 days.

4.3 Usability
NFR4: The UI shall be intuitive, allowing new users to familiarize themselves within 5 minutes.
NFR5: The system shall provide article summaries, audio integration, and video query responses.
NFR6: The platform shall offer a seamless experience across devices.

4.4 Performance
NFR7: The platform shall load the main newsfeed quickly.
NFR8: Search queries shall return results within 3 seconds.
NFR9: The personalized news feed shall adapt to user preferences within 1 minute of new interaction data being collected.

4.5 Security
NFR10: User data, including preferences and saved articles, shall be secure.
NFR11: The system shall have MFA for user accounts.

4.6 Scalability
NFR12: The platform shall be capable of handling many concurrent users without a significant drop in performance.
NFR13: The system shall support the addition of new sources, categories, and features.
