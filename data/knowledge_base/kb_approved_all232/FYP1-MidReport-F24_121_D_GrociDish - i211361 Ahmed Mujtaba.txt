1.2 Scope
The app will feature a personalized grocery list generation system that takes into account
user preferences, budget, and categories such as dietary needs. For managing recipes,
users will have access to a variety of pre-existing options with complete nutritional in-
formation, including those specifically tailored for conditions like diabetes, hypertension,
and obesity. Calories will be recommended based on each user’s Body Mass Index (BMI)
and Ideal Body Weight (IBW), ensuring portion control that aligns with individ- ual health
goals. Nutritional recommendations, including the balance of macro-nutrients (carbohy-
drates, fats, and proteins), will be adjusted according to the user’s activity levels. This will
optimize health outcomes by ensuring that the diet aligns with user’s lifestyle and specific
needs. The app will recommend recipes based on the ingredients available from the user’s
grocery list, providing a more customized experience. Additionally, the app will also fea-
ture daily caloric tracking for each family member, providing insights into their overall
consumption patterns for better health management. However, the app does not calculate
nutritional information in real time, meaning users will not receive immediate updates on
the nutritional content of their selected ingredients individually. Instead, the recipes will
have pre-calculated values. Similarly, the app does not generate new recipes on the fly
based on user inputs or available ingredients. Instead, it relies on a pre-existing database
of recipes, from which it suggests the most appropriate meal.

1.3.2 Grocery List Modification via Chatbot
This module integrates a chatbot that assists family members in managing their grocery
lists. The chatbot performs various operations such as retrieving, adding, and updating
items in the grocery list, ensuring an interactive and convenient experience for users.
1. Feature 1: Allows user to append items to their grocery list via a chat interface.
Users can simply input commands like "Add item".
2. Feature 2: Retrieves the current grocery list, providing users with an updated view
of the items already added.
3. Feature 3: Incorporates natural language processing to enhance user interaction,
making it easier to perform operations mentioned above.
1.3.3 Ingredient-Based Recipe Suggestions
This module recommends recipes based on the ingredients available from the generated
grocery list. It enhances customization by offering recipe suggestions that align with what
users already have, making meal planning more convenient and efficient.
1. Feature 1: Suggests recipes based on ingredients from the user’s grocery list, en-
suring minimal food waste and efficient meal planning.
2. Feature 2: Allows users to filter recipe suggestions by dietary preferences or health
conditions.

2. Feature 2: Includes alternative Meal options for variety while maintaining nutri-
tional balance.
3. Feature 3: Nutritional calculations(Total Calories,Fats,carbs,protein and per serving
calories) are done with the help of a nutritionist to ensure accuracy and suitability
for local cuisine.

This module calculates recommended serving sizes for each family member, ensuring

3.1 Architectural Design
The architecture diagram for GrociDish shows the interaction between the frontend ap-
plication, backend services, and the AI models. Users access the system through the
frontend application, which communicates with the API Gateway. The gateway acts
as the central hub for routing requests to the various backend services, including User
& Family Management, Grocery List Service, Meal Tracking Service, Recipe Man-
agement, and the Chatbot Service. These services interact with a database to manage
users, recipes, and grocery data. TheAI services — specifically the Grocery Generation
Model and the Recipe Suggestion Model — provide tailored recommendations for gro-
ceries and recipes, enhancing the app’s functionality based on user data. The architecture
ensures a modular design with AI integration for personalized recommendations.

3.2 Design Models
Approach:Object Oriented Development Approach
OOP is better suited for GrociDish because it organizes the system into reusable, modular
components that represent real-world entities like users, recipes, and grocery lists. This
structure enhances maintainability and scalability as the app grows, allowing features like
calorie tracking, recipe selection, and family member management to be handled effi-
ciently within separate classes. OOP also enables encapsulation, securing sensitive data
like user health information, and supports re-usability through inheritance, making it eas-
ier to extend functionality in the future without major rewrites. This flexibility makes
OOP ideal for a complex, evolving system like GrociDish.
