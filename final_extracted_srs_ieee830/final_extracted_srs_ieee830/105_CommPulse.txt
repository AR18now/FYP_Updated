Project: CommPulse - Real-Time Meeting Quality Monitoring System
================================================================================

1. INTRODUCTION
1.1 Purpose
This document outlines the objectives, background, and technical details for CommPulse, a desktop application that monitors the quality of online meetings in real-time and post-meeting.

1.2 Scope
The system monitors audio streams from Microsoft Teams to track metrics like sentiments, active participation, clarity, and agenda adherence. It provides real-time feedback and post-meeting analysis through a user-friendly interface.

1.3 Definitions, Acronyms, Abbreviations
- API: Application Programming Interface
- MTBF: Mean Time Between Failures
- RBAC: Role-Based Access Control
- TLS: Transport Layer Security

2. OVERALL DESCRIPTION
2.1 Product Perspective
CommPulse fills the gap in quality monitoring tools for online meetings by providing a streamlined system to track important communication traits and give actionable feedback to improve effectiveness.

2.2 Product Functions (Summary)
- UI/UX design for meeting management, real-time and post-meeting graphs.
- Front-end development (ElectronJS, ReactJS) for user management, audio capture, meeting history.
- Back-end meeting quality monitoring (sentiment, participation, clarity, agenda adherence).
- Meeting history database (SQL/NoSQL).
- Integration of front-end and back-end.

2.3 User Classes and Characteristics
- Admin: Can track live meetings, perform post-meeting analysis, manage user roles.
- Regular User: Can analyze past meetings and evaluate metrics after the meeting has ended.
- Guest User: External participant with limited access.

2.4 Operating Environment
Desktop application.

3. FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS
3.1 UI/UX design
FR1.1: The system shall provide user management screens for registration, login, and profile management.
FR1.2: The system shall present a home screen with options for starting or joining meetings, accessing meeting history, and user settings.
FR1.3: The system shall display real-time graphs and figures representing meeting quality metrics.
FR1.4: The system shall provide a post-meeting screen with summarized and detailed meeting statistics.
FR1.5: The system shall generate post-time graphs showing key metrics such as clarity, agenda adherence, and speaker engagement.
FR1.6: The system shall display a meeting history screen where users can view data from previous meetings.

3.2 Front-end development
FR2.1: The system shall provide user management and authentication functionality.
FR2.2: The system shall display a home screen with options to manage meetings, user profiles, and settings.
FR2.3: The system shall include a start meeting screen, capturing necessary meeting details.
FR2.4: The system shall capture audio data from the user's device through an API for real-time analysis.
FR2.5: The system shall allow the user to manage ongoing meetings, including displaying real-time participant data and metrics.
FR2.6: The system shall display a post-meeting page showing results, statistics, and summary.
FR2.7: The system shall generate and display post-time graphs.
FR2.8: The system shall maintain a meeting history page.

3.3 Meeting Quality Monitoring System Back-End
FR3.1: The system shall use an API to identify and distinguish speakers.
FR3.2: The system shall analyze and display real-time sentiment metrics.
FR3.3: The system shall monitor active participation and display engagement scores.
FR3.4: The system shall monitor adherence to the meeting agenda and provide feedback.
FR3.5: The system shall generate time-stamped highlights.

3.4 Meeting History Database
FR4.1: The system shall store session-level data (start time, end time, participants, agenda).
FR4.2: The system shall store speaker-level data (sentiment, engagement, clarity, highlights).

3.5 Integration of Front-End and Back-End
FR5.1: The system shall capture audio from the front-end and transmit it to the back-end in real-time.
FR5.2: The system shall display real-time plots of meeting quality metrics.
FR5.3: The system shall generate and display post-time plots.
FR5.4: The system shall retrieve and display meeting history data.

4. NON-FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS
4.1 Reliability
NFR1: The MTBF of the system shall be 1,000 operational hours.
NFR2: The system shall minimize storage loss of meeting data to less than 1 minute during failure.
NFR3: Within one minute of failure, error logs shall be created, and recovery will take place in less than five minutes.
NFR4: If the system fails, it shall recover automatically with the user not needing to pause ongoing meetings.

4.2 Usability
NFR5: Users shall reach post-meeting reports via the dashboard in just 5 clicks.
NFR6: A user shall initiate live meeting tracking as soon as the meeting has started.
NFR7: Help documentation and tooltips shall be present on every screen.
NFR8: Within 30 minutes of engagement, users shall execute basic analysis tasks.

4.3 Performance
NFR9: For up to two-hour meetings, analysis shall be completed in five minutes.
NFR10: Up to 100 users shall be handled simultaneously with stable performance.
NFR11: The system shall ensure instant feedback in 10 seconds during live meeting tracking over a 20 Mbps or faster connection.
NFR12: The system shall perform real-time monitoring with a maximum delay of 15 seconds for meetings with up to ten attendees.

4.4 Security
NFR13: The system shall require multiple authentication methods for all admin users.
NFR14: Role-Based Access Control (RBAC) shall be implemented.
NFR15: The system shall support a connection protocol of TLS 1.2 or greater.

4.5 Maintainability
NFR16: Changes in one module shall affect fewer than 10% of the other modules.
NFR17: Codebase updates shall be noted within 2 days.
NFR18: No system downtime for users shall be allowed during any meetings.
