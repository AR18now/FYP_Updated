2.1.1 Research Item 1
Askarian et al„ 2021
2.1.1.1 Summary of the research item
In this research study Askarian and colleagues have proposed a portable, easy to use
telemedicine solution for detecting cataracts with more than 95 percent accuracy using
smartphone captured pictures, they have done this in order to find the feasibility of de-
tecting cataracts using smartphones. To accomplish this they have developed a novel
luminance based method for feature extraction in which instead of regular rgb, they use
the brightness and luminance values of an image in order to extract features for cataract
classification. Their methodology is to first capture an image of an eye using a smart-
phone camera from 10 cm to 50 cm distance, then the image goes through preprocessing
including a median filter and watershed transformation to take out artifacts. Next lumi-
nance values are calculated based on the specific phones camera sensor characteristics, the
converted image is then sent to a support vector machine algorithm which then classifies
the presence of cataract within the eye. They trained the model on 100 pictures of artifi-
cial eye models, in which 50 were cataracts and 50 were non cataracts, and then used 10
fold cross validation for the dataset to reduce chances of overfitting. They found that this
method not only detects cataracts with 96.6 percent accuracy but also that environmen-
tal factors such as lighting conditions, camera angle and distance, and what smartphone

2.1 Related Research
2.1.2.1 Summary of the research item
This study proposes a method for detecting refractive errors in pediatric patients using a
deep learning network based detection method using smartphone images. In the study,
photo refraction images were obtained of children using a smartphone camera with flash-
light in dim lighting using a specific method to obtain a crescent like shape in the eye, over
300 images were obtained, and the patients were then tested by ophthalmologists using
cycloplegic (dilated eyes using eye-drops) photorefraction to obtain ground truth. Some
of the dataset was augmented to increase the dataset quantity, after which 5 fold cross
validation was used and the dataset was then used to train the ResNet-18 CNN model in
order to classify between 7 ranges of refractive errors in the eye. Performance of different
AI models was tested and the ResNet-18 model obtained the highest accuracy of ˜80 per-
cent across all 7 ranges. The study found that with a limited dataset, an AI model could be
trained to find ranges of refractive measurements in children which can be used to detect
refractive errors with ˜80% accuracy, it also suggested that with a larger dataset, specific
measurements of refractive errors could also be obtained.
2.1.2.2 Critical analysis of the research item
Strengths:
1. The research was done in real life environments and on people rather than models
and was tested against results from ophthalmologist experts making the study very
credible
2. The results covered a wide range of refractive errors.
3. Different models were tried and tested to obtain the best one
Weaknesses:
1. The methodology needed to obtain an image of the patient is tedious as it requires
a room with little lighting (under 10LX) and for the image to be taken from a cer-
tain distance and angle to obtain the crescent shape in the eye using smartphone
flashlight
2. Although the study was able to obtain a large range of refractive errors it could not
make specific measurements as it lacked the dataset quantity to do so
3. The model was trained only on korean children and may not be able to perform well
with other ethnicities.

2.1.4 Research Item 4
DO ET AL. (2022)
2.1.4.1 Summary of the research item
In this study, a smartphone autorefraction technique was used to detect refractive errors in
children’s eyes and compared with the results from an autorefractor (an automated device
to measure refractive errors). The smartphone based method does not use AI or machine
learning, rather they take images from a smartphone from different angles and then cal-
culate the refractive error, by calculating the pupil diameter size and the crescent image
width reflected from the eye from the smartphone’s flash. The study was conducted on
141 chinese schoolchildren, first the smartphone refraction technique was used and then
it was compared to autorefraction technique. They compared the techniques under both
Cycloplegic (drops given to eye for dilation) and non cycloplegic conditions. They found
that the correlation between the two techniques was 88% for non-cycloplegic conditions
and 80% for cycloplegic conditions, meaning they achieved impressive accuracy in de-
tecting refractive measurements using smartphone images

3.0.1 AI Model Approach
For our project we are making a mobile application that will have the capability to detect
Types and stages of cataracts and refractive errors suing images taken from the smart-
phone camera. Here i will present the methodology and framework we intend to use to
accomplish the two main tasks.
1. Detecting Cataracts:
For this task we are using the following methodology taken from Askarian Et Al [1]
1. Image Aquisition:
Taking images of patients eye using the smartphone camera with flash on, with a dis-
tance of 10 to 50 cm from the eye, using the phones autofocus and cameras maximum
resolution.
2. Preprocessing:
Preprocessing involves 2 main steps
a. Image segmentation: With image segmentation we crop the image using a circle mask
by marking the center of the circle and the radius of the circle to extract the region of
interest this being the cornea of the eye,
b. Denoising For denoising first we use median filter on the image to remove unneccesary
glare and noise from the image. Secondly we apply watershed transformation to the
image in order to extract the lens boundaries and to extract the ROI from the filtered
image. Because most cataract diseases appear in the shape of a circle in the lens area,
the watershed algorithm can be applied for image segmentation and visualization of the
diseased areas.
3. Feature Extraction
Next we need to convert the image rgb values into luminance and brightness values since
we are trying to detect cataracts from the luminance values reflected from the eye. In a
