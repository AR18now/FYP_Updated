1. Introduction
1.2 Scope
The scope of the InsightWire project is to provide a news platform that addresses the
issues of bias, lack of context, and fragmented information in news consumption. The
system is designed to aggregate news articles from 18 to 20 sources to present a balanced
view of current events. We are only concerned with political news, nonpolitical news
is outside our scope. It includes a bias detection mechanism that classifies news arti-
cles into various political leanings, helping users identify and understand inherent biases.
InsightWire also features a contextual background component that creates a timeline of
events as they have unfolded and led up to the present story. Additionally, the platform has
news analytics to identify and assess any forward looking predictions or trends mentioned
in the articles. However, the project does not extend to predictive analytics based on his-
torical data or in-depth investigative journalism. It focuses solely on the aggregation, bias
detection, contextual analysis, and trend evaluation of news articles as presented in the
platform. The scope also excludes real-time fact-checking and user-generated content
moderation, which are outside the boundaries of this project.

2.1 Functional Requirements
This section describes the functional requirements of the system.
2.1.1 Module 1: News Aggregation, Categorization, and Personal-
ization
Following are the functional requirements for Module 1:
1. FR1: The system shall retrieve and aggregate news articles from a minimum of 18
sources and a maximum of 20 sources.
2. FR2: The system shall only retrieve news articles that are categorized as political;
non-political articles shall be excluded during the aggregation process.
3. FR3: The system shall group each retrieved article by subject matter, ensuring that
articles about the same event are grouped together.
4. FR4: The system shall tag each news article with publication source, publication
date, and political bias classification.
5. FR5: The system shall allow users to create their profiles.
6. FR6: The system shall enable users to customize their news feed by selecting spe-
cific interests and desired levels of political balance.

7. FR7: The system shall determine a user’s location to highlight news articles rele-
vant to that location.
2.1.2 Module 2: Chatbot, User Interaction, and Feedback
Following are the functional requirements for Module 2:
1. FR1: The system shall include a chatbot that can respond to user queries.
2. FR2: The chatbot shall be able to summarize the contents of specific news articles.
3. FR3: The chatbot shall provide insights into themes or trends across a minimum of
three sources for the same news story.
4. FR4: The system shall provide users the ability to submit reviews on news articles.
5. FR5: The system shall allow users to leave comments on news articles, which will
be stored and displayed alongside the article.
2.1.3 Module 3: Bias Detection and Story Comparison
Following are the functional requirements for Module 3:
1. FR1: The system shall analyze the political bias of each news article, classifying it
into one of the following categories: left, center, right.
2. FR2: The system shall, upon user request, display articles from two sources on the
same topic, with differences in language and tone highlighted for comparison.
3. FR3: The system shall generate a summary of the detected bias for each news story,
making the overview accessible to the user with a single click.
2.1.4 Module 4: Contextual Background and News Analytics
Following are the functional requirements for Module 4:
1. FR1: The system shall display a timeline of related historical events for each news
story, allowing users to view how the story has evolved over time.
2. FR2: The system shall perform analytics on the news articles, identifying trends or
predictions explicitly mentioned within the content.
3. FR3: The system shall allow users to filter the contextual timeline by date, display-
ing events that occurred within a specified timeframe.

2.2 Non-Functional Requirements
2.2 Non-Functional Requirements
This section specifies the non-functional requirements. These quality requirements are
specific, quantitative, and verifiable, ensuring the system functions as intended beyond its
core features.
2.2.1 Reliability
1. RL1: The system shall provide 99.9% uptime during normal operations to ensure
continuous availability for users.
2. RL2: The system shall ensure that the aggregation of news sources continues even
if up to 2 external news sources fail to deliver data.
2.2.2 Usability
1. US1: The system shall provide an intuitive user interface with clear navigation
paths.
2. US2: The system shall allow users to customize the interface to enhance the user
experience.
2.2.3 Performance
1. PE1: The system shall handle up to 200 concurrent users without noticeable degra-
dation in performance.
2. PE2: The system shall ensure that the response time of the chatbot remains under
10 seconds.
3. PE3: The system shall support the retrieval of news analytics in under 5 minutes.
2.2.4 Scalability
1. SC1: The system shall store news articles and user data in a distributed NoSQL
database (e.g., MongoDB) that allows for scaling data storage as the number of
users and articles increases.
7
2. Project Requirements
2.2.5 Portability
1. PR1: The system shall be accessible on Chrome without requiring additional plug-
ins or extensions.
2. PR2: The system shall provide a responsive design that works seamlessly across
desktop, tablet, and mobile devices with varying screen sizes.
2.2.6 Security
1. SE1: The system shall require all user authentication to be conducted via HTTPS,
ensuring that all data transmitted between the client and server is encrypted.
2. SE2: The system shall implement role-based access control (RBAC) to restrict
access to sensitive operations, such as content moderation and system configuration,
to authorized personnel only.
3. SE3: The system shall enforce strong password requirements (minimum 8 charac-
ters, including at least one uppercase letter, one number, and one special character)
for user accounts.
4. SE4: The system shall prevent cross-site scripting (XSS) attacks by sanitizing user
inputs.
2.2.7 A vailability
1. A V1: The system shall be available 24/7, with planned maintenance periods not
exceeding 2 hours per month.
2. A V2: The system shall notify users of any planned maintenance or outages at least
24 hours in advance, displaying alerts in the application and via email.
