SOFTWARE REQUIREMENTS SPECIFICATION (IEEE 830)
Project: BeAWarden - AI-Powered Community-Based Traffic Violation Detection System
================================================================================

1. INTRODUCTION
1.1 Purpose
This document outlines the requirements for BeAWarden, an app that uses AI to detect traffic violations through registered users acting as virtual wardens, aiming to reduce human effort and capture violations missed by static cameras and traffic wardens.

1.2 Scope
To develop an app that allows registered users (virtual wardens) to record traffic violations using their mobile phones. The app will use AI models to detect violations (e.g., illegal number plates, HTV in the first lane), identify license plates via OCR, and notify authorities to issue e-challans.

1.3 Definitions, Acronyms, Abbreviations
- AI: Artificial Intelligence
- OCR: Optical Character Recognition
- HTV: Heavy Transport Vehicle
- LTV: Light Transport Vehicle
- FPS: Frames Per Second

2. OVERALL DESCRIPTION
2.1 Product Perspective
BeAWarden is a mobile application that transforms community members into active participants in traffic law enforcement by using AI to automatically detect and report violations.

2.2 Product Functions (Summary)
- App development (camera recording, voice command for auto-recording, UI/UX)
- License plate detection (OCR)
- Illegal number plate detection (custom dataset, model training)
- HTV first lane detection (vehicle classification, model training)
- Violation reporting and notification to authorities

2.3 User Classes and Characteristics
- Virtual Warden: Registered user who records violations via the app after passing a test. They use a voice command to save violation footage.

2.4 Operating Environment
Mobile application communicating with a centralized server for AI processing and reporting.

3. FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS
3.1 App Development & License Plate Detection
FR1.1: The app shall continuously record video through the mobile fixed on the dashboard.
FR1.2: The app shall save a chunk of the video being recorded using a voice command by the user.
FR1.3: The recorded video shall be saved locally until uploaded to the server.
FR1.4: The system shall detect specific voice commands (e.g., "Violation") to save recorded video chunks.
FR1.5: The app shall have a user-friendly interface for recording and uploading.
FR1.6: The app shall give voice feedback on saving video and starting/stopping.
FR1.7: The app shall have a dashboard for user records, analytics, and incentive tracking.
FR1.8: OCR shall be used to get the license plate number of the violator.

3.2 Illegal Number Plate Detection & HTV First Lane Detection
FR2.1: The system shall allow the collection of a custom dataset of images containing various types of illegal number plates.
FR2.2: The system shall support dataset updates with new images to improve model accuracy.
FR2.3: The system shall train an ML model to detect illegal number plates in video footage.
FR2.4: The system shall detect the HTV on the first lane by first detecting the first lane and then the HTV on it.
FR2.5: The system shall classify all vehicles using object detection to detect violations based on vehicle type.
FR2.6: The system shall train an ML model for HTV detection based on a custom dataset.

4. NON-FUNCTIONAL REQUIREMENTS
4.1 Reliability Requirements
NFR1: The system shall have an MTBF of at least 200 hours of continuous operation.
NFR2: The system shall have a downtime of no more than 2 hours for maintenance.
NFR3: Recorded videos shall be uploaded without corruption.

4.2 Usability Requirements
NFR4: The app shall have an appealing UI for easy recording and uploading.
NFR5: The voice command feature for recording shall have an accuracy rate of at least 90%.

4.3 Performance Requirements
NFR6: The mobile app shall function smoothly, maintaining at least 30 FPS for video recording.

4.4 Security Requirements
NFR7: All personal data (videos, user information, violation reports) shall be secured.
NFR8: Unauthorized access to uploaded videos or violation data must be prevented.
NFR9: App access will be given only to authorized users.
