1.6 Modules: 
 
The modules of the app are as follows: 
 
Voice Assistant Module: 
This is the core module of the application, where users talk to the app and it responds 
through voice commands. 
 
1) Training the Voice Assistant: 
The foundation of the V oice Assistant Module relies on accurate and 
comprehensive data. To make voice assistant work well, we need to train it 
with lots of Urdu phrases: including formal language and casual words etc. It 
will also learn to understand Urdu mixed with some English words (that are 
used in Urdu language as well). For example, when the user mention product 
names in English, the model will help the assistant to recognize different 
ways people speak as well. 
 
2) Speech -to-Text (ASR): 
In this module, the app will listen to what the user says and changes it into 
text. For example, if someone says, “mujhe chawal dekhao”, the app will turn 
7 
 
that into text so that it can understand the requests. We will make sure that 
the app understands different ways by which people pronounce words in 
Urdu. 
 
3) Intents model (Understanding the user): 
Once the speech is converted to text, it figures out what the user wants to do. 
Explaining the previous example, if someone says, “mujhe chawal dekhao”, 
the app will know that they want to search for rice. If they say, “isme sabsy 
acha konsa wala ha?” the app will understand that they want a comparison of 
different brands and qualities of rice. This model is trained to recognize 
different commands and requests. 
 
4) Text-to-speech (TTS): 
After processing the user's request, the app responds by speaking. TTS 
converts the textual response generated by the app into Urdu speech. For 
example, the app might say, "Yeh chawal aap ke liye behtreen hai" (This is 
the best rice for you). This makes the interaction smooth and easy for the 
user. 
 
 
 
 
Client Module: 
This is the user-facing interface where customers can interact with the app aside from the 
voice features. 
 
1) User Interface and User Experience (UI/UX): 
While the app is voice-centric and most things are voice-controlled, it still 
provides a simple user interface for basic functions like registration, 
profile management, and settings. For people who cannot read (illiterate) 
or elderly users, the design will be super minimal with large buttons and 
easy-to-understand visual aids (e.g., product images with minimal text). 
Our goal is to make sure that anyone can use the app easily, even if they 
are not used to technology. 
 
2) Shopping Cart & Checkout: 
The app will automatically add the products and items to the cart when the 
user tells it to. After they are done shopping and ready to buy, the app will 
8 
 
guide them through the checkout process using voice commands. It will 
also confirm the total cost and asking about the delivery and payment 
choices. 
 
Delivery Module: 
This part handles everything about delivering groceries to our respected customers. 
 
1) Order Assignment : 
Once the user has placed the order, the app will automatically assign it to the 
closest delivery person, making sure the order gets to the customer quickly. 
 
2) Order Tracking : 
This module allows the user to track their order through voice commands like 
"Mera order kahan tak pohncha hai?" (Where has my order reached?). The 
system will respond with live updates on the delivery status, giving the user a 
sense of control over the delivery process. 
 
3) Delivery Confirmation: 
When the groceries are delivered, the app will ask the user to confirm that 
they received everything. It will help making sure all deliveries are 
completed correctly. 
 
Admin Module: 
The Admin Module provides the necessary tools for app administrators to manage the 
platform. 
1) Product Management: 
Administrators will have the ability to add, edit, or remove products from 
the catalog, ensuring that the latest items are available for users. This 
includes adding product details, pricing, availability, and descriptions. 
 
2) Order Management: 
Admins can keep an eye on all ongoing and past orders, resolve issues, 
and handle customer queries. If a problem arises with an order, such as an 
unavailable product, admins can contact the user through automated voice 
notifications or directly intervene to resolve the issue. 
 
3) User Management: 
Admins can also manage user accounts, including the registration and

better.

3 System Overview: 
VoiceGrocery is a mobile application designed to empower visually impaired users and those 
with limited literacy by providing an accessible, user-friendly, and voice-operated grocery 
shopping experience. The app leverages voice recognition technology and a virtual assistant to 
facilitate the entire grocery shopping process, from browsing products to making purchases, 
tracking orders, and managing deliveries. 
The app's primary goal is to create a barrier-free shopping experience, allowing users to interact 
with the system through natural language commands in both English and Urdu, without the 
need for traditional visual interfaces or text-based inputs. This significantly enhances autonomy 
and ease of use for individuals who may struggle with conventional shopping apps due to visual 
or literacy constraints. 
 
3.1 System and Architecture Design: 
 
The VoiceGrocery system comprises multiple layers that work together to provide seamless 
functionality: 
 Frontend (Mobile App): 
 
The app interface is designed using cross-platform frameworks like React Native or 
Flutter, ensuring compatibility with both Android and iOS devices. Users interact with 
the system using voice commands facilitated by an intuitive interface that requires 
minimal visual engagement. 
 Voice Processing Layer: 
 
The app integrates with advanced Speech-to-Text (ASR) and Text-to-Speech (TTS) 
engines, converting spoken commands into actionable inputs and providing feedback in 
natural language. Natural Language Processing (NLP) is employed to interpret user 
commands, extract intent, and facilitate interactions in both English and Urdu. 
 Backend (Business Logic): 
 
The core backend handles user management, product management, order processing, 
payment integration, and delivery scheduling. This layer interacts with external 
services like payment gateways and delivery platforms to ensure a smooth workflow. 
 Database: 
18 
 
 
A centralized database stores user profiles, product catalog, order histories, and 
preferences. This data is accessed in real-time to personalize the shopping experience for 
individual users. 
 
 External Integrations: 
 
VoiceGrocery integrates with third-party APIs such as payment gateways (e.g., Stripe, 
PayPal), delivery services, and voice-processing platforms (e.g., Google or AWS 
services) to provide voice recognition and payment functionality.
